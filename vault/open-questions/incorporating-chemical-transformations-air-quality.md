title: Modeling Chemical Transformations
---

**Background:** Air quality forecasting involves complex atmospheric transport mechanisms such as chemical transformations and secondary pollutant formation that are not fully captured by current diffusion-advection transport models. Extending these models to include such processes could significantly improve the realism and accuracy of long-term concentration predictions.

**Question / Future Work:** The current framework focuses primarily on physical diffusion and advection. Future research is required to incorporate detailed chemical transformation and secondary pollutant formation mechanisms within the latent dynamics to better approximate real-world atmospheric behavior.

**Why It Matters:** Incorporating chemical kinetics is critical for bridging the gap between simplified physical transport models and the complex, reactive nature of real-world atmospheric pollution.

**Evidence:** The framework could be extended to incorporate additional physical mechanisms, such as chemical transformations or secondary pollutant formation, to enhance realism.
