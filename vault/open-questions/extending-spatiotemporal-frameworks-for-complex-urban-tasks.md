title: Extending Spatiotemporal Forecasting Frameworks
---

**Background:** Spatiotemporal forecasting models attempt to achieve a balance between global dependency modeling and computational efficiency. Current approaches combining attention and state-space modules struggle to maintain performance when scaled to diverse urban contexts or multivariate dependencies.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to systematically extend hybrid spatiotemporal architectures to handle multivariate traffic forecasting, adaptive spatial graph structures, and multi-task urban prediction objectives. Developing mechanisms that integrate heterogeneous context beyond historical flow remains a critical open area.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing these challenges is essential for transitioning from specialized traffic forecasting benchmarks to real-world, dynamic urban intelligence systems.
