title: Geodesic-based probabilistic scoring rules
---

**Background:** Probabilistic forecast reconciliation methods for nonlinear constraints often rely on Euclidean-distance based scoring rules, which may not be optimal for non-linear manifolds.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop a proper scoring rule based on geodesic distance that is more meaningful for evaluating forecast distributions on nonlinear manifolds. Existing Euclidean-based scores can lead to misleading results in nonlinear reconciliation settings.

**Why It Matters:** Evaluating the quality of probabilistic forecasts on nonlinear manifolds requires geometry-aware metrics rather than standard Euclidean metrics to ensure validity and interpretability.

**Evidence:** Developing a new geodesic-distance based proper scoring rule to evaluate the reconciled forecast distribution, which is more meaningful on the nonlinear manifold, is thus important.
