title: Predictability source regime transitions
---

**Background:** Stratospheric polar vortex (SPV) predictability varies significantly between normal winters and winters characterized by sudden stratospheric warming (SSW) events.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to delineate the physical mechanisms that govern the transition of predictability contributions between different dynamical regimes and to identify potential precursors that signal when the predictability source shifts from simple persistence to complex, multi-state interaction.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the state-dependent nature of SPV predictability is crucial for improving subseasonal-to-seasonal (S2S) forecasting, as current models may fail to capture the shift in predictive sources during extreme events like SSWs.
