title: Higher-order innovation forecasting
---

**Background:** Technological innovation is widely modeled as the recombination of existing elements, but forecasting the specific timing and emergence of these combinations remains a challenge. Current innovation forecasting models typically focus on pairwise interactions between technologies rather than complex multi-field synthesis.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to transition from pairwise technological combination forecasting to modeling higher-order assemblies involving three or more technological components. Capturing the simultaneous alignment of multiple technological vocabularies as a multi-way phenomenon is necessary for high-fidelity mapping of innovation trajectories.

**Why It Matters:** Moving beyond binary combinations is critical for modeling the synthesis of complex technological fields and provides a path toward mapping the 'adjacent possible' in innovation.

**Evidence:** we have restricted attention to pairwise combinations, whereas innovation frequently involves higher-order assemblies of three or more technologies whose joint actualization cannot be reduced to their pairwise precursors.
