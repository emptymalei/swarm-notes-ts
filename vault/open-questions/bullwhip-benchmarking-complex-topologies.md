title: Bullwhip Benchmarking for Complex Topologies
---

**Background:** Standardized evaluation of bullwhip mitigation strategies in multi-echelon supply chains is limited by a lack of shared benchmarks covering non-serial topologies and advanced, non-linear ordering policies.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed to evolve current bullwhip benchmarking to handle complex, non-serial network topologies, upstream capacity constraints, and modern ML-based or deep reinforcement learning ordering policies to bridge the gap between synthetic experiments and real-world supply chain dynamics.

**Why It Matters:** This transition is critical for moving beyond simplified, serial theoretical models toward scalable, robust digital twins of complex global supply chains.

**Evidence:** Expanding the catalog further to include deep reinforcement learning policies and end-to-end newsvendor methods would increase the framework’s value as a community resource.
