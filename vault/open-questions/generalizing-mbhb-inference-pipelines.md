title: Generalizing MBHB Inference Pipelines
---

**Background:** Gravitational-wave parameter estimation for massive black hole binaries (MBHBs) is critical for triggering electromagnetic follow-up, but current amortized inference models often rely on restricted parameter spaces and idealized detector noise assumptions. Scaling these pipelines to include astrophysical complexities is necessary for real-world deployment.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work must evaluate the robustness of amortized neural inference pipelines when generalized to include binary spins and realistic, non-stationary or colored detector noise profiles.

**Why It Matters:** This question addresses the gap between idealized simulation-based neural inference and the physical complexity required for operational space-based GW observatories.

**Evidence:** applying the method to longer observation windows and to larger, astrophysically motivated MBHB populations will allow us to assess performance across the relevant parameter space, including spinning binaries and more complex noise conditions.
