title: Adaptive Mortality Uncertainty Calibration
---

**Background:** Current mortality forecasting models often rely on Gaussian assumptions for process noise, which may fail to capture the heavy-tailed nature of extreme, low-probability exogenous events.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing fully adaptive uncertainty calibration frameworks that move beyond simple Gaussian process noise is required to better represent the fat-tailed risk profile of extreme mortality events in capital requirement calculations.

**Why It Matters:** Essential for robust calculation of Solvency Capital Requirements where standard models frequently underestimate tail risk.
