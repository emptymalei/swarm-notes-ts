title: Refining Scalar-Tensor Wave Formalism
---

**Background:** Gravitational waves induced by scalar-tensor mixing are generated during cosmological epochs such as matter-dominated eras, yet the full non-linear treatment and its impact on the stochastic gravitational wave background remain incompletely explored.

**Question / Future Work:** The current modeling of STGWs lacks a rigorous matching prescription for tensor fluctuations across transitions between different cosmic expansion eras and requires further investigation into higher-order perturbative contributions and potential theoretical artifacts in energy density calculations. Improving these aspects is crucial for increasing the precision and interpretability of data from future high-sensitivity gravitational wave observatories.

**Why It Matters:** These points represent fundamental theoretical bottlenecks in the reliability of cosmological forecasts, limiting the ability to definitively characterize the stochastic gravitational wave background observed by arrays like NANOGrav and future instruments like the SKA.
