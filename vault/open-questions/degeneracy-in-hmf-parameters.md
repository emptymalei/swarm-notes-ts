title: Degeneracies in HMF Parameters
---

**Background:** Strong gravitational lensing is a powerful probe of dark matter substructure, but inference on halo mass function (HMF) parameters is often hindered by degeneracies where different parameter combinations produce similar observational effects on lensed images. Identifying strategies to break these degeneracies is essential for distinguishing between dark matter models.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a persistent degeneracy between high-amplitude/steep-slope and low-amplitude/shallow-slope halo mass functions in lensing data. Future work is needed to characterize the structural origins of these degeneracies and develop observational or methodological strategies to resolve them.

**Why It Matters:** This degeneracy directly impacts the ability to constrain dark matter physics, as it affects the interpretation of the mass function parameters across diverse subhalo configurations.

**Evidence:** The inferred posteriors reveal a strong degeneracy between high-amplitude/steep-slope mass functions and low-amplitude/shallow-slope mass functions... We leave further explorations of this degeneracy to future work.
