title: ERB Modeling and Fluctuations
---

**Background:** Axion-like particles (ALPs) undergo radiative decay into photon pairs, a process stimulated by background radiation fields such as the Cosmic Microwave Background and the extragalactic radio background (ERB). Statistical cross-correlation of radio intensity mapping with galaxy surveys provides a potential avenue for detecting these signals at cosmological scales.

**Question / Future Work:** Current models of ALP dark matter intensity mapping depend on empirical radio luminosity functions and galaxy evolution models. There is a need to quantify model uncertainties stemming from these extrapolations and to evaluate the contribution of spatial ERB fluctuations to the angular power spectrum, which are currently neglected in the signal-to-noise forecasts.

**Why It Matters:** Since the stimulated decay rate is proportional to the background radiation intensity, uncertainties in ERB modeling directly propagate to the limits derived for ALP-photon coupling.

**Evidence:** Fluctuations in the ERB could, in principle, modulate the ALP DM signal and contribute to the angular power spectrum by introducing additional three-dimensional power spectrum components involving the ERB field.
