title: Quantum noise-aware normalization
---

**Background:** Quantum magnetometers are fundamentally limited by noise processes such as spin-projection noise and photon shot noise.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing a normalization procedure that incorporates the known quantum noise floor—such as by subtracting the expected Standard Quantum Limit (SQL) spectral density before performing channel normalization—could significantly sharpen the distinction between fundamental quantum-limited performance and technical artifacts.

**Why It Matters:** Distinguishing between fundamental quantum noise and technical faults is the primary challenge in quantum sensing diagnostic pipelines.

**Evidence:** For quantum sensor arrays, an extension that incorporates the known quantum noise floor into the normalisation stage—for instance by subtracting the expected SQL spectral density before normalising—could sharpen the distinction between quantum-limited and technically limited operation.
