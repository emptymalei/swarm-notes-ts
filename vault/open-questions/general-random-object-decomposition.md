title: Decomposition of general random objects
---

**Background:** Geometric frameworks for removing deterministic components from time series are generally developed for specific spaces like the sphere. Extending these to general random objects requires defining appropriate removal operations.

**Question / Future Work:** Generalizing the optimal transport-based removal operations to more complex random objects, such as networks, remains an open research direction for analyzing non-Euclidean time series with latent structure.

**Why It Matters:** This would broaden the scope of structural decomposition techniques to a wider variety of non-Euclidean data types prevalent in modern data analysis.

**Evidence:** While we focus on spherically embedded time series, the geometric interpretation of Euclidean subtraction and several optimal transport maps explored in Zhu and Müller (2026) can motivate other removal operations for general random objects such as networks.
