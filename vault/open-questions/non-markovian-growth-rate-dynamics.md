title: Characterizing non-Markovian growth-rate dynamics
---

**Background:** Stationary time-series data often exhibit growth-rate distributions with non-normal, leptokurtic behaviors, which complicates the use of standard models based on the assumption of normality.

**Question / Future Work:** The framework for selecting stochastic differential equations for growth-rate modeling is currently restricted to Markovian systems, leaving open the challenge of characterizing non-Markovian dynamics where memory effects dictate stationary distribution shape. This is particularly relevant for systems where variance does not reach the predicted saturation plateau, suggesting either non-stationary dynamics or memory-intensive processes.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing memory effects in growth-rate distributions is critical for moving beyond the Markovian limitations of existing stochastic modeling frameworks.

**Evidence:** Assuming that the stationarity tests are correct, we can imagine that the system is endowed with very strong correlations produced by memory effects (the Markovian approximation is not valid) or the variance is stuck in the growing phase without reaching the saturation value.
