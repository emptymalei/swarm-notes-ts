title: Bridging minimalist and semantic models
---

**Background:** Computational literary analysis spans from surface-level symbolic representations (like phoneme sequences) to high-dimensional semantic models (like contextual embeddings). The relationship between these two distinct tiers of representation remains a theoretical and empirical gap.

**Question / Future Work:** Determine whether minimalist, graphemic-level sequential patterns are sufficient to reconstruct or map onto deeper latent semantic and narrative structures in long-form creative texts. This investigates the potential for 'style-to-semantics' bridge modeling in computational humanities.

**Why It Matters:** Determines the descriptive and predictive limits of minimalist statistical modeling in understanding literary and complex creative complexity.

**Evidence:** These findings could be confirmed and even extended by adopting richer encodings ... to uncover latent semantic structures and longer-range narrative cues.
