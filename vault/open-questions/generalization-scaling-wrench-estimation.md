title: Scaling Sensorless Wrench Estimation
---

**Background:** Sensorless wrench estimation is often task-specific, and the potential of large-scale pretrained proprioceptive representations to generalize across diverse contact tasks and platforms remains a key research frontier.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigating scaling behaviors and representation learning strategies for proprioception-to-wrench pretraining to improve few-shot generalization and inference robustness across varied contact objects and robotic platforms.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the core question of whether foundation model paradigms can effectively transfer to specialized robotic sensing and interaction tasks.

**Evidence:** Further analyses of scaling behavior and representation learning strategies... would provide a more rigorous understanding of their effects on wrench estimation and forecasting.
