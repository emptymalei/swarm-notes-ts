title: Robust Simplicial Complex Construction
---

**Background:** Simplicial complexes are often constructed from graph-based datasets using algorithmic techniques such as clique identification, which can produce highly imbalanced topological distributions.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop methodologies for constructing simplicial complexes from real-world data that accurately capture higher-order interactions without introducing noise or disproportionate topological structures that degrade model performance.

**Why It Matters:** The efficacy of simplicial neural network architectures depends heavily on the quality of the underlying topological complex; poor construction can negate the theoretical advantages of higher-order modeling.

**Evidence:** These disproportionate structures introduce noise as many triangular relationships represent algorithmic artifacts rather than meaningful interactions, limiting performance gains despite competitive results.
