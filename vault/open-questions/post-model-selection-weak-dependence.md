title: Post-model-selection under dependence
---

**Background:** Adaptive post-model-selection inference involves conducting statistical tests after selecting features from high-dimensional datasets. Standard inference methods often fail in this context because the selection process introduces non-standard asymptotic distributions and selection bias.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved need for endogenously selected optimal covariate index methods that remain robust under weak dependence assumptions. While the current paper provides a pre-estimation test that avoids endogenous index selection, the authors note that exploring such selection mechanisms under weak dependence remains a challenge for future research.

**Why It Matters:** Developing post-model-selection inference that is valid for dependent time-series data is critical for accurate applications in fields like macroeconomics and finance, where traditional i.i.d. assumptions often do not hold.

**Evidence:** We do not explore an endogenously selected optimal covariate index l^n under weak dependence, leaving that for future work.
