title: Early Fusion for Relapse Detection
---

**Background:** Early fusion integrates multimodal data at the input or feature representation level, rather than at the decision score level.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to explore early fusion approaches that jointly model cardiac and sleep-circadian patterns within a unified Transformer-based network. The objective is to learn deeper cross-modal interactions that might further enhance the detection of psychotic relapse compared to the currently used late-fusion strategies.

**Why It Matters:** Current state-of-the-art results for this task rely on late-fusion of independent models; moving to early fusion could potentially uncover more complex, hidden correlations between physiological modalities that are critical for higher-fidelity clinical predictions.

**Evidence:** Future work will explore early fusion approaches that jointly model cardiac and time/sleep patterns within a unified network to learn cross-modal interactions that may further improve relapse detection.
