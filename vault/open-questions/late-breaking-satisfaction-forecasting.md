title: Horizon-Adaptive Satisfaction Forecasting
---

**Background:** Early-warning systems for learner satisfaction often rely on initial behavioral and textual signals, which may fail to capture dissatisfaction triggered by content or assessments released later in a course.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of horizon-adaptive models or systems that incorporate dynamic course-content metadata is necessary to improve the accuracy and robustness of satisfaction forecasting beyond the initial early-warning windows.

**Why It Matters:** This identifies a fundamental limitation in current early-warning paradigms that restrict predictions to fixed, narrow observation windows.

**Evidence:** Late-breaking dissatisfaction: dissatisfaction caused by assessments released after the horizon window cannot be predicted from early signals.
