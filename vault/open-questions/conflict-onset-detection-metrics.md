title: Conflict Onset Detection Metrics
---

**Background:** Conflict forecasting models commonly struggle to predict sudden onsets of violence, as standard error metrics often prioritize high-fatality areas and neglect the critical early warning signals needed by decision-makers.

**Question / Future Work:** Development of hurdle-based forecasting frameworks that use a dual-metric evaluation approach: one for continuous intensity estimation in active conflict zones, and a separate, high-reward metric specifically tuned for the detection of sudden conflict onsets in non-active areas.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses a fundamental limitation in the practical utility of conflict forecasting models, shifting evaluation from generic accuracy to impact-driven early warning metrics.

**Evidence:** However, a key expectation of forecasting models from decision-makers is to detect those early warning signals to ideally avoid conflict onsets. The solution could be to develop hurdle models that are assessed on both error in active conflict zones and a highly rewarding onset metric for non-active zones.
