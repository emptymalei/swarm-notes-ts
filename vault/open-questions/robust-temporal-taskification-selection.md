title: Optimal and Robust Taskification
---

**Background:** In streaming continual learning, continuous data streams are typically partitioned into discrete tasks to facilitate evaluation. Current benchmarking practices treat these temporal task boundaries as fixed experimental constants rather than variables that can fundamentally alter the perceived performance of learning algorithms.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop automated procedures for identifying or selecting an 'optimal' temporal taskification for a given stream, moving beyond the purely diagnostic framework currently established. Furthermore, the development of continual learning methods that are demonstrably robust to variations in temporal taskification remains an unresolved challenge.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing a methodology for selecting robust taskifications is critical to ensuring that benchmarking conclusions in streaming continual learning are intrinsic to the models being evaluated rather than artifacts of the chosen segmentation.
