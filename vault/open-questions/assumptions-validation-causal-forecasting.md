title: Assumptions in causal forecasting
---

**Background:** Causal forecasting models often rely on untestable assumptions about the underlying data-generating process, which can limit their reliability when applied to real-world, complex longitudinal datasets.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to rigorously validate the fundamental assumptions governing the data-generating process within real-world, multimodal settings to ensure the robustness of causal forecasts.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical because the validity of the causal identification depends on these structural assumptions; without empirical verification, the model's causal estimates in real-world environments could be biased or misleading.

**Evidence:** As with all causal models, the approach relies on assumptions about the data-generating process whose validity in real-world multimodal settings requires further study.
