title: Cross-institutional data fragmentation impact
---

**Background:** Disease trajectory prediction models using electronic health records (EHRs) are constrained by fragmented data across different healthcare systems, where events occurring outside a specific network remain unobserved.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved need to assess the impact of cross-institutional data fragmentation on the performance of disease trajectory models and to develop methods to mitigate the loss of predictive signal caused by unobserved events outside a primary health system.

**Why It Matters:** Foundation models for healthcare depend on large-scale data to generalize; fragmentation remains a fundamental barrier to achieving reliable long-horizon clinical predictions.

**Evidence:** events that occur outside the MGB network remain unobserved. This difference in observability may be particularly relevant for longer-term prediction.
