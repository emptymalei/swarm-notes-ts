title: Backward Inference in Chaotic Systems
---

**Background:** Dissipative dynamical systems exhibit exponential attenuation of information about initial conditions, rendering the inverse problem of backward temporal inference inherently ill-conditioned.

**Question / Future Work:** The stability of backward temporal inference in LAPIS-SHRED is currently restricted to dissipative systems; extending this framework to chaotic regimes with positive Lyapunov exponents requires addressing fundamentally different information-theoretic constraints on state reconstruction.

**Why It Matters:** Formalizing error bounds in chaotic regimes is essential for the reliability of inverse spatio-temporal modeling in physical sciences.
