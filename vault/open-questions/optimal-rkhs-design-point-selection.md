title: Optimal RKHS design point selection
---

**Background:** The current Functional Tucker Decomposition (FTD) framework relies on a fixed choice of design points, which are used to construct the Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS) model.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate and design general strategies for determining optimal sampling points (design points) when performing functional modeling using RKHS in tensor decompositions, which could lead to more stable and adaptable methods, potentially influencing data acquisition planning.

**Why It Matters:** Optimal selection of sampling points is critical for the stability and accuracy of RKHS-based models, and developing general strategies would provide a significant methodological advancement for functional data analysis.

**Evidence:** Another important open research question is the design point selection. Although this choice may be application‑dependent or constrained by measurement availability, investigating general strategies for determining optimal sampling points in RKHS models could yield more stable and adaptable methods. Such strategies may even merit consideration during the planning of data acquisition.
