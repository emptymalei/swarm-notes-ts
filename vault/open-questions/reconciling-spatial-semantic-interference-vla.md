title: Reconciling Spatial-Semantic Conflict in VLAs
---

**Background:** Autonomous driving systems are increasingly adopting Vision-Language-Action (VLA) architectures, which require the simultaneous integration of semantic reasoning with precise 3D spatial perception. However, integrating these disparate capabilities often leads to optimization interference within shared model parameters, degrading the overall performance of the unified system.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to develop more robust methods for decoupling or harmonizing the disparate requirements of spatial perception and semantic reasoning in unified VLA architectures, specifically addressing whether alternative architectural paradigms can facilitate effective multi-task learning without sacrificing reasoning capabilities.

**Why It Matters:** This is a foundational challenge in modern end-to-end autonomous driving research, as the current trade-off between perception accuracy and semantic reasoning limits the deployment of truly unified VLA systems.

**Evidence:** Consequently, although these methods differ in how spatial information is introduced, they remain unable to fully reconcile spatial perception with semantic reasoning, highlighting the need to decouple these objectives during optimization.
