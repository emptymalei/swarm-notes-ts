title: Identifying Reliable Leading Indicators
---

**Background:** Hospitalization forecasting in non-stationary healthcare environments remains sensitive to data noise and reporting delays, making it difficult to identify exogenous indicators that consistently lead changes in demand.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining which specific exogenous contextual signals (e.g., demographic shifts, search trends, or public health alerts) offer reliable, decision-relevant lead times for hospitalization demand remains a fundamental challenge for operational resource planning.

**Why It Matters:** Without identified and validated leading indicators, even advanced hybrid forecasting models may fail to provide the proactive warnings necessary for resource allocation during healthcare crises.

**Evidence:** This highlights a key limitation in the availability of reliable leading indicators, suggesting that the primary challenge lies not only in model design but also in identifying signals that truly anticipate hospitalization demand.
