title: Dynamic Acoustic Localization Bottlenecks
---

**Background:** In multi-party environments, current speaker localization methods often struggle with high background noise levels and the lack of user-specific listening intent. Head-tracking from wearable sensors serves as an emerging behavioral modality to infer the user's acoustic zone of interest.

**Question / Future Work:** The integration of behavioral and audio signals for real-time acoustic localization remains an open challenge, particularly in dynamic settings where conversation partners enter or leave the interaction space and where segmentation-based approaches fail to adapt. Researchers must address the limitations of static, short-segment assumptions by evolving architectures that can handle dynamic social trajectories and real-time multimodal fusion.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental bottleneck for deploying assistive hearing and AR systems in real-world, unconstrained social settings compared to the static environments used in current research.
