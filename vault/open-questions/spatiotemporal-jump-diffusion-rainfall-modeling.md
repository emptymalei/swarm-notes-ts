title: Spatio-temporal rainfall modeling extension
---

**Background:** Rainfall intensity is a complex geophysical process often modeled using stochastic differential equations, but capturing its full spatiotemporal dynamics remains a challenge for climate research.

**Question / Future Work:** Extending temporal mean-reverting jump-diffusion models to incorporate spatiotemporal correlations is necessary to investigate how extreme rainfall events propagate across geographical regions and interact with larger-scale atmospheric variability.

**Why It Matters:** Moving from purely temporal to spatiotemporal stochastic modeling is a critical bottleneck for regional climate forecasting and extreme event tracking.

**Evidence:** One natural extension is to incorporate spatio-temporal correlations to study how extreme rainfall events propagate across regions and interact with larger-scale atmospheric variability
