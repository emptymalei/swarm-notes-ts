title: Mitigating Negative Transfer in Multitask Learning
---

**Background:** Multitask learning in clinical settings aims to improve downstream tasks like policy optimization and outcome prediction by leveraging shared patient representations.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate the theoretical and empirical conditions under which multitask learning in multimodal clinical time-series results in negative transfer, and develop adaptive architectures to mitigate interference when scaling to a larger number of clinical outcomes.

**Why It Matters:** As clinical models move toward serving multiple diagnostic and prognostic endpoints simultaneously, managing task interference is essential to prevent performance degradation on critical safety-sensitive tasks.
