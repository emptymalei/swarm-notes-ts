title: Multi-user Network-aware V2G Management
---

**Background:** Bidirectional EV charging (V2G/V2H) offers potential for energy arbitrage and grid support but is constrained by battery degradation and user mobility requirements. Scaling these frameworks to multi-user settings and distribution networks is essential to assess their impact on grid resilience.

**Question / Future Work:** Extend single-user energy management strategies for bidirectional EV charging to multi-user environments and incorporate distribution network constraints to rigorously assess the aggregate grid reliability and infrastructure impact of V2G/V2H adoption.

**Why It Matters:** Scaling from single-user to multi-user and network-aware settings is critical to understanding the aggregate feasibility of V2G technology for power grid support.

**Evidence:** Future work will extend the framework to multi-user and distribution-network-aware settings to assess how bidirectional charging can support grid resilience under both normal operation and fault conditions.
