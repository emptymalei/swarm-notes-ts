title: Uncertainty Propagation in Boost Factors
---

**Background:** The subhalo boost factor, which quantifies the enhancement of the dark matter annihilation signal due to substructure, is currently computed using semi-analytic models that rely on deterministic prescriptions calibrated on simulations, typically omitting stochastic halo assembly histories.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to quantify how halo-to-halo scatter, uncertainties in environmental classification, and variations in individual assembly histories propagate through to the subhalo boost factor prediction to move beyond deterministic, mean-field estimates.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the variance and uncertainty in boost factor predictions is critical for the precision of observational forecasts and the reliability of constraints on particle dark matter models.

**Evidence:** In the present implementation, we do not propagate halo-to-halo scatter, uncertainties in the fitted environmental ratios, or classification uncertainties associated with the Cosmic Web assignment.
