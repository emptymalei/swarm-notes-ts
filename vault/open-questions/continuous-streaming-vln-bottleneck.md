title: Continuous Streaming VLN Bottleneck
---

**Background:** Vision-Language Navigation (VLN) agents often rely on stop-and-go decision-making patterns, where navigation planning is frequently coupled with intermittent halts for observation.

**Question / Future Work:** Current VLN architectures struggle with the transition from discrete, halt-based interaction to continuous, streaming navigation, which is necessary for fluid and efficient real-world human-robot interaction.

**Why It Matters:** The stop-and-wait bottleneck limits navigation efficiency, introduces artificial latency, and hinders the scaling of VLN to truly dynamic, fluid indoor settings.
