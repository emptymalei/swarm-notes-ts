title: Self-other perspective alignment
---

**Background:** Mirror Neuron System-inspired robotics aim to facilitate social cognition by enabling autonomous action prediction and imitation through observation. Integrating these systems requires bridging the gap between first-person and third-person perspectives.

**Question / Future Work:** The extent to which existing architectures can autonomously manage the shift between third-person and first-person perspectives remains unresolved. It is not yet clear how they can achieve perspective invariance without specific, explicit training, nor how they should refine these mappings through online interaction with real-world environments.

**Why It Matters:** This is a core challenge in social robotics, as natural human-robot interaction requires alignment of action representations across different observational viewpoints.
