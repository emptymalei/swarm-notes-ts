title: Causal drivers of algorithmic bias
---

**Background:** Recommendation algorithms mediate access to information, often creating differentiated environments based on user demographics like gender. It remains unclear whether such asymmetries stem from users' pre-existing preferences or the active, independent organizing role of recommendation mechanisms.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains unclear to what extent observed structural and allocative gender biases in recommendation systems are driven by users' pre-existing preferences and selective exposure versus the active, independent organizing role of platform recommendation algorithms. Identifying the relative contributions of these factors is essential for distinguishing between user-driven polarization and algorithmically enforced structural inequality.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental causal question in algorithmic fairness; if inequalities are purely user-driven, interventions must target user behavior, whereas algorithmically driven bias requires platform-level architectural redesigns.
