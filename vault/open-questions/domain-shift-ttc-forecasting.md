title: TTC Forecasting Domain Generalization
---

**Background:** Time-to-Collision (TTC) forecasting is a regression task that relies on interpreting complex spatiotemporal dynamics in video, including occlusions and varied traffic conditions. Achieving consistent generalization across diverse, real-world traffic datasets remains a significant challenge due to inherent domain shifts.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to resolve persistent domain shift challenges in video-based TTC forecasting, particularly regarding performance consistency across varied real-world deployment environments. Robust domain adaptation and generalization techniques are necessary to ensure the reliability of safety-critical collision prevention systems.

**Why It Matters:** TTC forecasting is safety-critical, and model degradation in new environments directly impacts the reliability of automated collision prevention systems.
