title: Multi-agent SBFD Resource Allocation
---

**Background:** Sub-Band Full Duplex (SBFD) architectures rely on adaptive uplink/downlink resource splits to manage traffic, but current resource allocation models typically operate within isolated single-cell environments.

**Question / Future Work:** The scalability and performance of predictive reinforcement learning-based SBFD resource allocation require evaluation in more complex scenarios involving multi-cell network topologies and multi-agent coordination, which is necessary to account for inter-cell interference and coordinated resource management in 6G networks.

**Why It Matters:** As SBFD moves toward practical deployment, the interaction between neighboring cells and the coordination of agents become critical to prevent system-wide performance degradation, making the multi-cell, multi-agent extension a necessary research frontier.
