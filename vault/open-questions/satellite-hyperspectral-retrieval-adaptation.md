title: Satellite Hyperspectral Retrieval Adaptation
---

**Background:** While near-surface hyperspectral remote sensing allows for accurate in situ monitoring, scaling such observations to spaceborne platforms is essential for global water quality monitoring. Satellite-based retrievals introduce significant additional challenges compared to in situ measurements, specifically regarding atmospheric correction and sensor-specific spectral variability.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is required to extend physics-aware retrieval frameworks to satellite-based hyperspectral imagery by explicitly integrating and mitigating atmospheric interference, sensor-specific spectral noise, and adjacency effects that degrade retrieval accuracy at the top-of-atmosphere level.

**Why It Matters:** Transitioning to satellite platforms is the primary bottleneck for achieving large-scale, high-spatiotemporal resolution water quality monitoring.

**Evidence:** Future work could focus on extending the framework to spaceborne multi/hyperspectral imagery... However, moving from in situ to satellite retrieval introduces additional error sources.
