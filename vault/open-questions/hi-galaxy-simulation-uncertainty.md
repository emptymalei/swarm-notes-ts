title: HI Galaxy Prediction Uncertainty
---

**Background:** Simulations of galaxy populations are essential for predicting the observables and clustering of neutral hydrogen (HI) in future large-scale radio surveys. However, current models for HI galaxy number counts and clustering exhibit significant discrepancies, complicating the interpretation of upcoming data.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a substantial theoretical uncertainty regarding the predicted HI galaxy number counts and bias across different simulation models (e.g., semi-analytic vs. hydrodynamical simulations). This discrepancy is particularly pronounced at high redshifts and lower flux density thresholds, and requires more robust simulation methods or multi-model comparisons to narrow the predicted range of observable properties for upcoming instruments like the SKA.

**Why It Matters:** Determining accurate predictions for HI galaxy distributions is critical for forecasting the scientific yield of large-scale radio survey missions and for interpreting observed signals in terms of cosmological parameters.

**Evidence:** The expected number counts and clustering properties of the galaxies are still quite poorly understood however.
