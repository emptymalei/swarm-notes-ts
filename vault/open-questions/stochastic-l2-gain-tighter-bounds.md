title: Tighter probabilistic L2-gain bounds
---

**Background:** Stochastic linear systems often exhibit unbounded L2-gain due to the presence of stochastic uncertainties in control inputs and exogenous disturbances.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate whether tighter probabilistic bounds for L2-gain stabilization can be obtained when more statistical information regarding the distribution of the disturbance noise is available, as the current framework relies on general bounds that may be conservative.

**Why It Matters:** The current bounds are conservative because they cater to all possible disturbance distributions. Tighter bounds would significantly improve control performance in practical applications where partial information about disturbance statistics is known.

**Evidence:** In the event when more statistical information of Δd is known, it would be interesting to investigate whether it is possible to obtain a tighter bound, yielding less conservative control design.
