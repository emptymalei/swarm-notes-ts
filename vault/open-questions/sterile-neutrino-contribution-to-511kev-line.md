title: Sterile Neutrino 511 keV Contribution
---

**Background:** The sterile neutrino decay channel into an active neutrino and an electron-positron pair produces final-state electron-positron pairs that may subsequently form a positronium bound state. This process potentially contributes to the 511 keV gamma-ray line emission observed from the Galactic center.

**Question / Future Work:** A detailed investigation is required to determine the contribution of sterile neutrino decay to the galactic 511 keV line excess, which necessitates advanced modeling of charged-particle propagation and energy loss within the complex environment of the Galactic center.

**Why It Matters:** Connecting dark matter decay to established astrophysical observations like the 511 keV line is crucial for constraining dark matter models or identifying potential new physics signals.

**Evidence:** The electron-positrons produced from the channel νs→ναe+e−, can lose energy and form a positronium bound state, which eventually can produce 511 keV line emission. ... A dedicated study of this signal would require a detailed treatment of charged-particle propagation and energy loss in the Galactic environment.
