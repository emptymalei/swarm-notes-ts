title: Non-perturbative primordial non-Gaussianity
---

**Background:** The polynomial expansion of primordial non-Gaussianity is commonly used to model departures from Gaussianity in the early Universe. The range of validity for these perturbative models in the context of induced gravitational waves is strongly model-dependent and requires further clarification.

**Question / Future Work:** Standard perturbative approaches to primordial non-Gaussianity often fail in regimes where the effects are large. A more general, non-perturbative framework is required to accurately model the induced gravitational wave spectrum and ensure reliable cosmological inference.

**Why It Matters:** This is important because standard perturbative approximations may miss significant signal features in regimes where non-Gaussianity is high, potentially leading to incorrect parameter constraints.

**Evidence:** the perturbativity condition is crucial for this relation to hold, and its range of validity is strongly model-dependent... We leave a more general study for future work.
