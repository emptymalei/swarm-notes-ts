title: Macroeconomic Covariate Integration
---

**Background:** The integration of macroeconomic covariates into latent-factor interest rate models remains an area of active development. While current latent-variable approaches effectively capture shape dynamics, they often lack explicit coupling with fundamental macroeconomic indicators.

**Question / Future Work:** The incorporation of external macroeconomic drivers (e.g., inflation indices, central bank policy rates) as exogenous forcing terms within the drift networks of Neural Stochastic Differential Equations remains a significant open challenge for improving long-horizon term structure forecasting.

**Why It Matters:** This is technically important because it bridges the gap between purely statistical generative models and economic structural models, providing a pathway toward more interpretable and policy-responsive term structure dynamics.

**Evidence:** Embedding these structural variables as external forcing terms in the SDE could further constrain long-horizon forecasting and improve the interpretability of the extracted risk premiums.
