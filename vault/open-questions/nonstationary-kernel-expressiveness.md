title: Flexible kernels for nonstationarity
---

**Background:** Stationary kernels are fundamental to the spectral representation and Markovian construction of Gaussian processes, but their assumption of shift-invariant correlation can be restrictive in dynamic, real-world systems.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop more flexible kernel parameterizations that can better handle high-dimensional, strongly nonstationary systems without sacrificing the analytical tractability and uncertainty quantification of current sequential Gaussian process models. Integrating neural feature maps or other adaptive representations into these frameworks remains a significant research challenge.

**Why It Matters:** Developing nonstationary kernels that maintain computational efficiency is critical for applying Gaussian processes to complex, real-world signal processing problems where signal properties evolve significantly over time.
