title: RSG Atmospheric Modeling Bottlenecks
---

**Background:** While 3D radiation-hydrodynamics simulations of red supergiants (RSGs) have provided new insights, existing model grids currently fail to replicate observed atmospheric structures and mass-loss mechanisms with high fidelity.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of more representative 3D radiative-hydrodynamics grids for RSGs is needed to address discrepancies between modeled and observed atmospheric extensions, pulsation properties, and the triggers for mass-loss events. Existing grids often rely on gray Rosseland opacities, which potentially limit the accurate modeling of molecular formation and dust-driven winds. Expanding these grids to include wavelength-dependent opacities and matching physical parameters more closely to observed stars remains a crucial path for future research.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental bottleneck in stellar astrophysics; bridging the gap between 3D model predictions and high-resolution interferometric data is essential to understanding the physics of RSG mass-loss, which is critical for stellar evolution models.

**Evidence:** The discrepancy in molecular bands of H2O may be due to the gray Rosseland opacity approximation in the models (Höfner and Freytag 2019).
