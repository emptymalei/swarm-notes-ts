title: Uniqueness of max-stable forecasts
---

**Background:** The prediction problem for stationary max-stable random fields involves minimizing a non-convex functional, and the uniqueness of the resulting forecast is not generally guaranteed.

**Question / Future Work:** The authors demonstrate that the solution to the prediction problem is not unique in general, and identifying the conditions under which a unique forecast exists—or characterizing the set of all optimal solutions—remains an unresolved problem.

**Why It Matters:** Non-uniqueness of the predictor poses significant challenges for practical applications, as different optimal weight vectors could lead to substantially different forecasts, necessitating further study on regularization or selection criteria.

**Evidence:** The uniqueness of the solution is in general not given.
