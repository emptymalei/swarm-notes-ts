title: Mechanisms of Skewness Dispersion
---

**Background:** Cross-sectional dispersion in firm-level realized skewness is a known predictor of future stock market returns, particularly around macroeconomic news announcements.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to isolate the relative contributions of rational (risk-based) versus behavioral (mispricing-based) channels in driving the predictive power of skewness dispersion in stock markets. Clarifying these mechanisms is essential for distinguishing between market efficiency theories and behavioral finance models.

**Why It Matters:** Distinguishing between risk-based and behavioral mechanisms is a fundamental challenge in asset pricing with significant implications for market efficiency and policy design.

**Evidence:** overall, this evidence supports a mechanism in which skewness dispersion captures heterogeneity in investor beliefs that is partially incorporated into prices ahead of major information releases and quickly resolved as information is revealed and processed... supporting the dual nature of skewness dispersion as both a risk-based and behavioral signal.
