title: Information Transmission Mechanism Ambiguity
---

**Background:** While prediction market signals have been shown to provide useful information for forecasting cryptocurrency volatility, the underlying mechanism of this information transmission remains poorly understood due to a lack of granular data on market participants. Establishing a clear causal link between prediction market sentiment and crypto-asset volatility requires differentiating between common participant pools, correlated information sets, and direct economic transmission channels.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to distinguish whether the observed relationship between prediction market repricing and cryptocurrency volatility is driven by shared participant pools, correlated information sets across markets, or direct transmission of macroeconomic shocks into crypto-asset pricing. Currently, the lack of participant-level data prevents a direct test of these mechanisms, leaving the exact nature of the information flow and its causality unresolved.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the mechanism is critical for determining whether prediction markets offer genuine predictive alpha or if the signals merely reflect market noise or shared speculative activity. This distinction affects the robustness and policy implications of using such markets for financial forecasting.
