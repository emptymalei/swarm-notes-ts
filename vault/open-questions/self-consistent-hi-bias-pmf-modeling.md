title: Self-consistent HI bias modeling for PMF studies
---

**Background:** The modeling of the neutral hydrogen (HI) bias in the post-reionization universe significantly influences the interpretation of 21-cm power spectrum measurements. Accurate bias modeling is essential for distinguishing between cosmological models at non-linear scales.

**Question / Future Work:** Standard HI bias functions are typically derived from Lambda-CDM simulations, yet primordial magnetic fields (PMF) fundamentally alter structure formation and the evolution of the baryon density field. A self-consistent determination of HI bias within a framework that accounts for PMF-induced perturbations is necessary to reduce systematic errors in cosmological parameter estimation.

**Why It Matters:** Using a bias model derived from standard CDM in a non-standard scenario introduces systematic errors in forecasts, as the bias is likely intrinsically modified by the physics being tested.

**Evidence:** more accurate analysis demands a dedicated high-resolution MHD simulation-based computation of the bias functions for the specific PMF-driven scenario under consideration.
