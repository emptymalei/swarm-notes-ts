title: Efficiency impact of lift synchronization
---

**Background:** Lift synchronization in multi-lift systems is often compared to bus-clustering, where specific scheduling policies interact to produce synchronized arrival patterns.

**Question / Future Work:** The formal link between identified synchronization states (in-phase vs. anti-phase) and quantifiable efficiency metrics like passenger waiting time remains unestablished, particularly in heterogeneous, multi-floor environments.

**Why It Matters:** Determining if synchronization is a symptom of inefficiency or a desirable state for throughput is critical for deploying adaptive control in high-density urban infrastructure.
