title: Efforts on accurate cluster center proxies
---

**Background:** High-precision gravitational redshift measurements require careful management of systematic uncertainties, particularly the choice of the cluster center.

**Question / Future Work:** Further efforts should be invested in exploring and comparing different prescriptions for accurately determining the cluster center, such as combining X-ray and optical observations, incorporating integral field spectroscopy, or adopting statistical priors on the center position, as mis-centering is the dominant systematic uncertainty in high-precision gravitational redshift studies.

**Why It Matters:** Cluster center mis-specification is identified as the only systematic effect capable of mimicking significant departures from General Relativity, making its accurate determination paramount for Stage-IV and Stage-V precision cosmology.

**Evidence:** Specifically, efforts should be invested into constructing an accurate proxy for cluster centres. Rosselli et al. (2023) found that the average of member positions can produce a more reliable estimate of a cluster’s minimum of the gravitational potential... Given these findings, and the fact that we find the centre mis-specification to be the only significant systematic effect for next-generation inferences of modified gravity from gravitational redshifts, it is paramount that future efforts be invested into (i) exploring different averaging prescriptions, (ii) determining a cluster’s centre from X-ray and optical observations jointly (Seppi et al., 2023), (iii) obtaining additional information with integral field spectroscopy, or from a statistical perspective, (iv) adopting a prior on the centre, rather than fixing it.
