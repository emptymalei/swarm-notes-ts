title: Dynamic ICD Extension Scaling

**Background:** Risk contribution metrics quantify individual asset contributions to total portfolio risk, but standard measures often aggregate isolated volatility and interaction effects. The Inherent and Correlation Decomposition (ICD) allows for the separation of these two sources of risk.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to extend the ICD framework to dynamic, multi-period settings and adaptive risk allocation strategies, particularly when employing non-stationary or high-dimensional covariance estimators in machine learning-based portfolio management.

**Why It Matters:** Ensuring that decomposed risk metrics remain valid and interpretable under adaptive strategies is a significant bottleneck for real-world deployment of automated portfolio management systems.

**Evidence:** Future research could extend the framework to dynamic risk allocation, multi-period settings, and machine-learning-based covariance estimation.
