title: Dynamic non-stationary forecasting adaptation
---

**Background:** Industrial time-series environments are characterized by non-stationary dynamics and regime changes that cause model performance to degrade over time.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved challenge in developing dynamic forecasting models that can adapt to nonstationarity without incurring the high computational costs of full re-training, specifically by leveraging structural priors from static multi-objective optimization frameworks.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the gap between static model selection (Pareto frontiers) and continuous adaptation is essential for long-term industrial ML viability.

**Evidence:** Real-world sintering production often exhibits pronounced regime changes and distribution drift, which can erode predictive performance over time.
