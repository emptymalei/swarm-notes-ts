title: Multivariate Probabilistic Data Assimilation
---

**Background:** Probabilistic densification methods, such as Neural Processes, typically utilize input observations to produce predictive distributions. Expanding the scope of these models beyond single-target variables (e.g., rainfall) could enhance their utility in broader meteorological applications.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research could extend probabilistic data assimilation frameworks by incorporating additional meteorological variables (e.g., temperature, humidity, wind) as both predictive targets and auxiliary inputs, moving towards a unified, multi-variable probabilistic weather prediction system.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the need for comprehensive, multivariate weather forecasting systems and leverages the existing architecture to handle more complex environmental modeling.
