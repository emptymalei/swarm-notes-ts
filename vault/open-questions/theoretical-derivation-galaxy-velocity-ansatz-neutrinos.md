title: Galaxy velocity field in neutrinos
---

**Background:** Theoretical studies of redshift-space distortions often assume that galaxies trace the velocity field of cold dark matter and baryons, an ansatz that lacks a full formal derivation in models with massive neutrinos.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to develop a self-consistent theoretical derivation of the galaxy velocity field ansatz in the presence of massive neutrinos and to investigate potential deviations from the current assumption that galaxies trace the velocity field of cold dark matter and baryons.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical for ensuring the accuracy of neutrino mass constraints derived from galaxy surveys, as the velocity field is a primary observable for redshift-space distortions.

**Evidence:** While a self consistent theoretical derivation of this result is still the subject of ongoing study, we adopt this ansatz in all that follows. We plan to investigate possible deviations from this approximation in a forthcoming work.
