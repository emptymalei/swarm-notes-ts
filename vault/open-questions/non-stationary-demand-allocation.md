title: Allocation Under Non-Stationary Demand
---

**Background:** Marketplaces often operate with non-stationary demand patterns such as seasonality, trends, and regime shifts. Existing models of demand allocation as a platform lever primarily focus on stationary demand processes.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is needed to extend the demand-allocation framework to non-stationary demand environments. Developing allocation policies that are robust to seasonality, trends, or regime shifts would better align the model with real-world planning environments and demonstrate the practical utility of intertemporal allocation in dynamic settings.

**Why It Matters:** Real-world marketplace demand is rarely stationary; thus, this extension is critical for bridging the gap between theoretical models and operational practice in e-commerce.

**Evidence:** Extending the analysis to nonstationary demand (seasonality, trends, regime shifts) and time-varying allocations would connect the mechanism to realistic planning environments.
