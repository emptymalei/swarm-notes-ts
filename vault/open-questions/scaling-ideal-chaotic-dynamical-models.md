title: Scaling Ideal Chaotic Models
---

**Background:** Data-driven modeling of chaotic systems using methods like Radial Function-based Regression (RfR) often relies on embedding observable time series into higher-dimensional spaces. While these models can reproduce short-term trajectories, they frequently fail to reconstruct the full dynamical structure, particularly negative Lyapunov exponents.

**Question / Future Work:** The construction of models that faithfully capture the negative Lyapunov exponents (the physically dominant dynamical structure) often requires a number of basis functions that grows exponentially with the system's dimensionality, posing a fundamental scalability bottleneck for complex physical systems.

**Why It Matters:** This identifies a structural limitation where trajectory-level accuracy is insufficient to guarantee dynamical fidelity, a key issue for physical-system modeling.
