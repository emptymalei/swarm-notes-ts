title: Offline-to-online transfer conditions
---

**Background:** Censoring-aware offline training objectives in operational decision-making are theoretically linked to online performance through a deployment penalty derived from completion mismatch.

**Question / Future Work:** While the authors establish that offline predictive quality translates to online performance under specific conditions, they acknowledge that censoring-aware offline training objectives may not inherently control completion mismatch without additional structural assumptions. Further research is required to characterize the minimal set of assumptions on data-generating processes that ensure this offline-to-online transfer for a wider variety of operational problems.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the necessary and sufficient conditions for offline-to-online transfer is crucial for the reliability of data-driven decision-making in the presence of censored feedback, which is ubiquitous in inventory management and revenue management.
