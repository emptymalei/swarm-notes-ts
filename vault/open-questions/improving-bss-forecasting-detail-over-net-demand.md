title: Relationship between forecasting detail and simulation accuracy
---

**Background:** The complexity of managing a Bike Sharing System (BSS) involves integrating demand forecasts with operational constraints like dynamic bike relocation to assess strategic decisions.

**Question / Future Work:** Further investigation is needed to understand the relationship between forecasting methodologies (which currently use net demand) and the simulation used to assess strategic and tactical decisions, particularly regarding how more detailed demand representations might change optimal strategies.

**Why It Matters:** Moving beyond net demand to Origin-Destination (OD) or trip-specific forecasting could significantly improve the realism of the simulation and the quality of the resulting strategic recommendations for fleet management and service reliability.

**Evidence:** Further investigating the relationship between forecasting and BSS simulation is an area of future research. In this work, user behavior and demand have been represented, for both the forecast and the simulation, by the net demand of bikes at each station. More detailed representations, such as, demand for a trip from a specific bike station to another, or from the actual origin to destination of the user, would allow for a more accurate estimation of the relationship between request forecasting and decision-making for bike sharing systems.
