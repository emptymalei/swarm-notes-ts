title: Local-global atmospheric context integration
---

**Background:** Data-driven tropical cyclone (TC) forecasting models often face trade-offs between processing high-resolution local imagery and incorporating large-scale atmospheric steering flows.

**Question / Future Work:** The integration of global boundary conditions from planetary-scale numerical models into high-resolution, localized deep learning models remains an unresolved challenge for long-range TC track forecasting accuracy.

**Why It Matters:** Identifying how to effectively bridge localized structural representation learning with global-scale atmospheric context is essential for scaling data-driven weather models to long-range forecasting.

**Evidence:** Future research will focus on integrating global boundary conditions from planetary-scale models into CycloneMAE, thereby merging local high-resolution information with the global meteorological context to construct a more versatile forecasting model.
