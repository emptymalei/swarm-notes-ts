title: Tighter probabilistic performance bounds
---

**Background:** The design approach for probabilistic finite ℒ₂-gain stabilization of stochastic systems currently assumes generic, worst-case distributions for input uncertainty and disturbance prediction error to guarantee a stable controller.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate whether tighter performance bounds and less conservative control designs can be obtained by incorporating additional statistical information or specific distribution assumptions about the input uncertainty and disturbance prediction error into the controller synthesis process.

**Why It Matters:** The current formulation is general and potentially conservative due to the lack of specific distribution knowledge; relaxing this could significantly improve performance in practical applications.

**Evidence:** In the event when more statistical information of Δd is known, it would be interesting to investigate whether it is possible to obtain a tighter bound, yielding less conservative control design.
