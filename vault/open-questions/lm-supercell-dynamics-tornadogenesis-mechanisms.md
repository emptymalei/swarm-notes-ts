title: LM Supercell Dynamics Mechanisms
---

**Background:** The physical mechanisms governing the development and maintenance of left-moving (LM) supercells, particularly their internal dynamics relative to right-moving (RM) counterparts and their infrequent tornadogenesis, remain incompletely understood.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research, including high-resolution numerical simulations, is required to test existing hypotheses regarding why left-moving supercells are generally weaker, shorter-lived, and less likely to produce tornadoes than right-moving supercells. Specifically, investigations should clarify how frequently these storms possess low-level mesoanticyclones, the extent to which their inflow is restricted to levels above the lifting condensation level (LCL), and the role that boundary interactions play in their development, longevity, and overall intensity.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding these dynamics is critical for improving the operational predictability of severe hazards associated with these under-studied convective storms.

**Evidence:** While this may be the case for some LMs, especially shorter-lived events, further numerical simulations are needed to test our hypotheses and understand the dynamics of LMs, how they physically differ from RMs, and why they predominantly produce hail and rarely produce tornadoes.
