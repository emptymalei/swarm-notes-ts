title: Causal Attribution in Control
---

**Background:** Interpreting the directional impact of decision variables on emissions in industrial combustion systems is often limited by post-hoc explainability methods that conflate correlation with causation.

**Question / Future Work:** Develop rigorous causal inference frameworks or domain-informed constraints that can distinguish between statistical attribution and direct physical causation for decision variables in complex industrial emission models. This is essential for moving beyond correlation to design robust, interpretable control systems that operators can trust to modify plant configurations.

**Why It Matters:** Moving from statistical correlation to causal understanding is essential for designing truly robust, interpretable control systems.

**Evidence:** SHAP-derived influences should be interpreted as statistical attributions conditioned on historically observed operating patterns rather than as direct causal relationships.
