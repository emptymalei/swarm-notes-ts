title: Adaptive post-model-selection inference
---

**Background:** Adaptive post-model-selection inference involves selecting an informative covariate index through a data-driven procedure before performing subsequent statistical tests. This often introduces non-standard limit distributions due to the endogenous nature of the selection.

**Question / Future Work:** The potential for endogenously selecting an optimal covariate index within a high-dimensional marginal screening framework under weak dependence remains a significant methodological challenge. Specifically, while max-tests avoid the need for such selection for valid screening, the development of reliable adaptive post-model-selection inference for weakly dependent data requires further theoretical investigation into the non-standard asymptotics resulting from such endogenous indices.

**Why It Matters:** Adaptive post-model-selection inference is a central problem in statistics, as practitioners frequently require both detection of significant predictors and valid post-selection inference, which currently remains computationally and theoretically complex for dependent, high-dimensional data.

**Evidence:** We do not explore an endogenously selected optimal covariate index l^n under weak dependence, leaving that for future work.
