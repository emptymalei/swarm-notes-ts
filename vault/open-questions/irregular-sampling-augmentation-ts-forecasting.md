title: Augmentation for Irregular Time-Series
---

**Background:** Clinical time-series data often feature irregularly sampled observations due to the episodic nature of clinical monitoring and interventions. Most predictive models for clinical time series are designed for regularly sampled sequences and struggle to process the irregular intervals inherent in real-world electronic health records.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing spectral residual augmentation techniques capable of handling irregularly sampled clinical time series is necessary to accommodate non-uniform intervals without loss of clinically critical information.

**Why It Matters:** Most real-world clinical data is irregularly sampled; therefore, extending robust augmentation techniques to these settings is essential for practical clinical deployment.
