title: Quantifying FRB Lensing Decoherence
---

**Background:** Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs) are increasingly used to probe dark matter, including Primordial Black Holes (PBHs), through gravitational lensing effects. However, the interactions between FRB signals and the intervening intergalactic medium can induce decoherence that degrades lensing signals.

**Question / Future Work:** The impact of decoherence effects, caused by the interaction of FRBs with the intergalactic medium, on the lensing signal of PBHs remains to be rigorously quantified. Addressing these effects is necessary to refine the sensitivity of future constraints on the PBH mass function, particularly in the high-mass regime where these effects are expected to be more significant.

**Why It Matters:** As current FRB lensing constraints are optimistic and do not account for physical propagation effects that can mask lensing, properly modeling decoherence is critical to provide realistic forecasts for upcoming observatories like LOFAR2.0, FAST Core Array, and BINGO.
