title: Diffusion Forecast Interpretability
---

**Background:** Deep generative models for forecasting often prioritize predictive accuracy at the expense of mechanistic transparency, complicating their adoption in policy-driven environments.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved need to bridge diffusion-based generative models with mechanistic interpretability, enabling practitioners to understand the causal or epidemiological drivers behind specific probabilistic trajectories.

**Why It Matters:** Providing mechanistic clarity is essential for decision-makers to justify public health interventions based on machine-generated forecasts.

**Evidence:** Moreover, interpretability remains a challenge: as a deep generative model, Influpaint does not provide mechanistic explanations for its predictions.
