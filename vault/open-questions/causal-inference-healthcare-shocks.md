title: Causal Inference for Healthcare Shocks
---

**Background:** Statistical time-series models for count-based healthcare activity, such as organ transplantation, often encounter difficulty in attributing observed shifts to specific systemic shocks versus latent structural changes. Developing robust, causal-inference frameworks that move beyond descriptive intervention proxies remains an important methodological challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is needed to extend count time-series models to incorporate explicit causal designs for regime identification. While current protocols often utilize intervention dummies to represent shocks like the COVID-19 pandemic, these proxies do not provide causal attribution, necessitating methods that can rigorously identify the underlying drivers of structural breaks.

**Why It Matters:** Moving from descriptive intervention models to explicit causal identification is critical for informing policy and clinical practice in post-disaster healthcare recovery.
