title: Macroeconomic Volatility Transmission Heterogeneity
---

**Background:** Macroeconomic models often struggle to integrate common level and volatility factors while capturing the heterogeneous ways uncertainty impacts different types of economies.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to determine whether the observed differences in volatility contributions between advanced and developing economies are structural or driven by specific, context-dependent transmission mechanisms in joint level-volatility factor models. Understanding the mechanism behind this heterogeneity is essential for improving predictive accuracy in diverse macroeconomic environments.

**Why It Matters:** This is a core challenge in understanding global vs. regional economic shocks and the reliability of density forecasting in international macroeconomics.
