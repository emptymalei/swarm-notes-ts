title: Capturing Abrupt Transition Dynamics
---

**Background:** Climate scenarios are high-dimensional projections, yet current summary metrics often rely on simplified, smooth parametric functions that struggle to represent non-linear transitions or sudden policy shifts.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing metrics that can formally identify and quantify structural breaks, abrupt policy changes, or delayed transition effects within climate scenarios remains a significant challenge for scenario analysis.

**Why It Matters:** Climate policy analysis often relies on scenarios with non-linear or delayed transitions; failing to capture these dynamics limits the utility of summary metrics in high-stakes policy modeling.

**Evidence:** the smooth decarbonization metric cannot capture acute changes in trajectores. For instance, in the Delayed Transition scenario by NGFS... This detailed feature cannot be explicitly reflected by the decarbonization speed.
