title: Robustness against exogenous shocks
---

**Background:** Dynamical Network Marker (DNM) theory identifies precursors to critical transitions in high-dimensional systems by detecting indicators like critical slowing down in interacting elements. Real financial markets are open systems subject to continuous exogenous shocks, which can interfere with the closed-system assumptions of the DNM framework.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing a DNM-based early-warning indicator that remains robust against exogenous market shocks is a significant open problem. Current indicators are sensitive to external influences that deviate from the standard model's assumptions, necessitating further research into constructing indicators that are effectively shielded from, or account for, these persistent external factors.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental challenge for the practical application of DNM theory in financial contexts, as the presence of exogenous noise is a primary obstacle to achieving reliable and precise early warnings.

**Evidence:** Real financial markets are open systems subject to continual external shocks, deviating from the DNM assumption of closed interactions among participants with limited random noise. These exogenous factors significantly affect the DNM indicator and are unavoidable in practice. Thus, developing a DNM-based early-warning indicator that remains robust against such external influences is also an important direction for future study.
