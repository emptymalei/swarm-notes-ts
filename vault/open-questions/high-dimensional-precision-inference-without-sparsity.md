title: High-dimensional Precision Inference Limits
---

**Background:** Simultaneous inference for high-dimensional covariance and precision matrices is typically contingent on structural assumptions such as sparsity, which can be difficult to verify in practice.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining whether non-asymptotic Gaussian approximation and bootstrap validity results can be established for precision matrix estimation in high-dimensional (p ≥ n) regimes without relying on sparsity or other rigid structural assumptions remains an open challenge. Current methods for precision matrix inference in high-dimensional settings primarily rely on regularized estimators that mandate specific structural priors.

**Why It Matters:** Provides a path toward assumption-free statistical inference in high-dimensional settings, which is essential for complex, non-structured data.
