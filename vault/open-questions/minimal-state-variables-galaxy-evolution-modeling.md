title: Minimal state variables in galaxy formation
---

**Background:** Galaxy formation is typically modeled either through computationally expensive hydrodynamic simulations or semi-analytic models (SAMs) with uncertain governing equations. There is currently no consensus on the minimal set of state variables required to characterize the evolution of galaxies and their circumgalactic medium.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to determine the minimal, necessary, and sufficient set of independent state variables required to characterize galaxy evolution and the thermodynamic state of the circumgalactic medium. Identifying these variables is essential for constructing robust, predictive dynamical frameworks that allow for clear model comparison.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental theoretical bottleneck that limits the predictive power of current physics-informed modeling efforts in astrophysics.
