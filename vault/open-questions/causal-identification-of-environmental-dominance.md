title: Causal identification of environmental dominance
---

**Background:** Individual success outcomes depend on both entity-specific capability and environmental opportunity, with empirical evidence suggesting a significant variance dominance of the latter over the former. Distinguishing the precise contribution of capability versus environmental factors remains challenging due to the confounding effects of sorting and selection bias.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop and apply causal-mobility designs to the global pooled-individual distribution of income outcomes to rigorously separate the causal effect of environment from the individual-level selection (e.g., sorting of individuals into better environments based on capability). The current empirical decomposition provides upper bounds on the dominance ratio, but a clean identification requires micro-data that is not currently integrated into the cross-country/decile variance framework.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing whether environmental dominance is an outcome of causal structural factors or individual selection bias is fundamental to understanding the nature of inequality and designing effective policy interventions.
