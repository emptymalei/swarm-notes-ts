title: Multi-dimensional Climate Scenario Metrics
---

**Background:** Integrated Assessment Models (IAMs) produce multidimensional projections, yet current summary statistics are typically restricted to single-variable metrics like carbon intensity.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an ongoing need for integrated, multi-dimensional metrics that consolidate disparate scenario variables—such as land use, economic indicators, and technological capacity—while maintaining the interpretability and parsimony required for comparative policy analysis.

**Why It Matters:** A comprehensive understanding of transition pathways requires aggregating information across domains to better represent the full scope of climate mitigation efforts.

**Evidence:** The decarbonization speed is defined to measure the improvement of carbon intensity... Nevertheless, this metric leaves other scenario variables unpresented.
