title: Split-ensemble QRC optimization limits
---

**Background:** Near-term quantum computers generate noisy measurement records consisting of finite samples (shots), which are typically processed into expectation values for classical machine learning. This process forces a trade-off between the precision of feature estimation and the total number of available training examples within a fixed measurement budget.

**Question / Future Work:** The authors identify the need for exploring adaptive group sizes and broader applications for split-ensemble training. Specifically, they suggest future work should investigate how to dynamically optimize group sizes based on task-specific characteristics, as well as testing the method's effectiveness with non-linear readouts, higher-dimensional observable sets, and more complex hybrid generative model architectures.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the limits and versatility of shot-record organization is critical for optimizing near-term quantum learning without increasing the hardware execution budget.
