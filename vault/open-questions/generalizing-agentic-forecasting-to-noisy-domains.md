title: Generalizing Agentic Forecasting Performance
---

**Background:** Large language models (LLMs) used for time series forecasting struggle to balance general-purpose reasoning capabilities with high-precision numerical performance.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to determine how to better distill generalized reasoning rules into these frameworks without overfitting to specific dataset characteristics, and how to improve the robustness of agentic tool selection in the face of noisy or non-informative diagnostic signals.

**Why It Matters:** The inability to maintain performance across all temporal domains, particularly when diagnostic evidence provides conflicting or non-predictive signals, represents a fundamental bottleneck for deploying agentic forecasting systems in heterogeneous real-world infrastructures.
