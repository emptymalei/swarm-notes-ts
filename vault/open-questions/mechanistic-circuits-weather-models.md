title: Mechanistic Circuits in AI Weather Models
---

**Background:** AI weather models are increasingly used for global forecasting, but their internal decision-making processes remain opaque \"black boxes\" that lack a comprehensive mechanistic understanding. Establishing a rigorous framework for identifying and mapping interpretable features within their latent spaces is essential for building scientific trust and operational reliability.

**Question / Future Work:** A key unresolved challenge is the systematic construction of a dictionary of interpretable features and understanding how they evolve across processor steps to form functional \"circuits\" that drive predictive outputs. Moving beyond qualitative associations to quantitative validation of these causal pathways remains an open research bottleneck.

**Why It Matters:** Without identifying functional circuits, individual feature identification remains largely anecdotal; validating these pathways is necessary to bridge the gap between ML activations and physical causality.

**Evidence:** Ultimately contributing to a more comprehensive understanding of how AI weather models generate their predictions.
