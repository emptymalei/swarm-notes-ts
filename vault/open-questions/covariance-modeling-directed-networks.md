title: Covariance Modeling on Directed Networks
---

**Background:** Spatio-temporal modeling of environmental processes is currently limited by the assumption of Euclidean distance metrics, which fail to capture connectivity constraints imposed by physical barriers and directional flows.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing a general framework for covariance modeling on directed networks that accommodates nonstationarity, anisotropy, and complex boundary behavior without requiring symmetric differential operators remains a significant open challenge in spatial statistics.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental bottleneck for geostatistical modeling of flow-driven domains like marine and river environments, where Euclidean-based models produce physically invalid correlations.
