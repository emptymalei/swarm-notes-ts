title: Robust modeling of PBH formation
---

**Background:** The formation of primordial black holes (PBHs) is sensitive to the collapse efficiency parameter, which varies based on the perturbation profile and background cosmological dynamics. Currently, the impact of these modeling variations on the precision of cosmological parameter constraints derived from gravitational wave observations is not fully constrained.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop more robust, precision-based modeling of PBH collapse dynamics—accounting for variations in the equation of state at horizon reentry and non-sphericity—to refine the resulting uncertainties in the Hubble parameter derived from multi-band gravitational wave signals.

**Why It Matters:** The uncertainty in the collapse efficiency acts as a potential systematic bottleneck for using primordial black holes as precise cosmological probes. Refining this parameter is essential for achieving high-precision Hubble constant measurements.
