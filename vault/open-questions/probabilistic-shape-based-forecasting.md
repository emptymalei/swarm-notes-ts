title: Probabilistic Shape-Based Forecasting
---

**Background:** Conflict forecasting models traditionally use point estimates for fatalities, but there is an increasing shift toward probabilistic forecasting to better account for uncertainty.

**Question / Future Work:** Adapting shape-based models to generate full probability distributions for future conflict risk, rather than deterministic scenario outputs, represents a critical unresolved direction. This requires determining appropriate statistical distributions and managing the trade-off between the precision of the threshold used for pattern similarity and the number of historical sequences required to estimate valid probability densities.

**Why It Matters:** Probabilistic outputs are essential for decision-making in high-stakes environments, as they provide an explicit measure of the risk and uncertainty inherent in conflict predictions.

**Evidence:** The ShapeFinder can be adapted in this direction by shifting from scenario generation to estimating a predictive distribution for each cell-month... Further research is needed to determine both the appropriate statistical distribution... and the optimal balance between the number of similar cases required and the distance thresholds.
