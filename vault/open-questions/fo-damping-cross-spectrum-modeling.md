title: Refining Cross-Spectrum FoG Modeling
---

**Background:** The Ly$\\alpha$ forest power spectrum is significantly affected by non-linear astrophysical effects, particularly the Finger-of-God (FoG) damping caused by random peculiar velocities within virialized structures. Accurate modeling of these effects is essential to avoid degeneracies with cosmological signals.

**Question / Future Work:** A systematic, robust, and physically motivated modeling approach for the Finger-of-God (FoG) damping in the Ly$\\alpha$–21-cm cross-power spectrum is required, as current analyses often rely on approximations derived from the auto-spectrum. Incorporating specific intra-halo kinematics for both tracers would improve the reliability of constraints on cosmological parameters, including neutrino interaction strengths.

**Why It Matters:** The cross-spectrum is a key observable for breaking degeneracies, and the lack of a tailored FoG model limits the precision and robustness of parameter extraction, especially as the signal-to-noise ratio improves with next-generation surveys.
