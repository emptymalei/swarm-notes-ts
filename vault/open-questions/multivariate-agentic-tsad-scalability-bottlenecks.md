title: Multivariate Agentic TSAD Bottlenecks
---

**Background:** Current time-series anomaly detection (TSAD) systems often produce anomaly scores but lack mechanisms for providing structured, interpretable explanations and diagnostic evidence. Current approaches commonly treat TSAD as a single inference task, which limits the ability to provide detailed, evidence-based reasoning required for complex diagnostic tasks.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to scale multi-agent, evidence-based anomaly detection frameworks to multivariate time-series data and to minimize the latency and computational cost associated with multi-agent LLM systems. Addressing these barriers, alongside the challenge of creating synthetic databases that cover diverse real-world failure modes and developing robust deployment-oriented calibration strategies, is essential for practical application.

**Why It Matters:** These issues are central to the scalability and practical reliability of agentic TSAD frameworks in industrial or critical monitoring settings.
