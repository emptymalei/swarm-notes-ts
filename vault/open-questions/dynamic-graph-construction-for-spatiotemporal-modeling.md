title: Dynamic Graph Construction
---

**Background:** Static graph structures are often used to represent spatial relationships, yet atmospheric transport mechanisms and pollutant dispersion patterns frequently evolve over time and seasons.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research should develop dynamic or adaptive graph construction mechanisms that capture shifting spatial dependencies between monitoring stations rather than relying on fixed distance-based proximity.

**Why It Matters:** Fixed proximity graphs often fail to capture the time-varying nature of atmospheric connectivity, representing a significant limitation in urban modeling.

**Evidence:** Dynamic or causally informed graph structures could further improve the model’s ability to represent changing pollutant transport mechanisms across seasons and cities.
