title: Robust Inference for Hurst Estimators
---

**Background:** Statistical analysis of multifractional processes often relies on estimators for the time-varying Hurst function, which characterizes the local Hölder regularity of the process. Currently, robust methods for quantifying the uncertainty of these estimators are limited.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to develop robust statistical inference frameworks for Hurst function estimators, specifically focusing on the construction of reliable confidence intervals and formal hypothesis tests.

**Why It Matters:** The absence of formal statistical testing and confidence interval estimation limits the ability to draw rigorous conclusions about the time-varying roughness of observed processes in practical applications.

**Evidence:** New statistical functions may be based on methodological developments in constructing confidence intervals and statistical tests for Hurst function estimates.
