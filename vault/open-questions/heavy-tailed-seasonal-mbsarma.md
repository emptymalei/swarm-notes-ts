title: Heavy-tailed and seasonal extensions
---

**Background:** The Birnbaum-Saunders distribution is widely used to model positive, skewed time series, but existing multivariate models often lack flexibility in capturing heavy-tailed behavior or seasonal patterns.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing heavy-tailed extensions of the multivariate Birnbaum-Saunders model using alternative kernels, such as the Student-t distribution, remains a key direction for improving modeling robustness. Additionally, incorporating explicit seasonal components into the multivariate ARMA framework is identified as a necessary step for more accurate environmental and financial forecasting.

**Why It Matters:** The current Gaussian kernel limits the model's ability to account for extreme values, which are critical in pollution episodes and financial risk management, and seasonal modeling is essential for time-varying data.

**Evidence:** As part of future research, it is of interest to propose heavy-tailed variants based on the BS-t kernel and seasonal MBSARMA formulations.
