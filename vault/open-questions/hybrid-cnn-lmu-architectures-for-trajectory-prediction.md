title: Hybrid CNN-LMU Trajectory Architectures
---

**Background:** Legendre Memory Units (LMUs) use continuous-time state-space representations grounded in Legendre polynomials to process sequential data. Hybrid architectures combining CNNs with LMU-based recurrent structures have shown potential in movement forecasting tasks.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate the potential of a hybrid CNN-LMU architecture, where the CNN extracts spatial or contextual features and the LMU processes temporal dynamics, to improve upon current LSTM-based hybrid models in chaotic, dynamic environments.

**Why It Matters:** The LMU's principled, continuous-time framework for long-term dependency capture may offer better stability than traditional LSTM-based hybrid models in chaotic, high-interaction environments.
