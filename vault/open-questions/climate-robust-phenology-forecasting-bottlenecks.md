title: Robustness in Climate-Phenology Forecasting
---

**Background:** Deep learning models for plant phenology often struggle with distribution shifts caused by climate change, as they are typically trained on historical climate data and fail to generalize when future meteorological conditions deviate significantly from the training distribution.

**Question / Future Work:** While domain adaptation techniques can help bridge the gap between historical training data and future projections, climate change presents a complex, continuous domain shift involving both covariate and label shifts that are not fully addressed by existing unsupervised domain adaptation methods designed for binary domain classification. Further research is needed to explore methods for handling continuous temporal domains and integrating diverse ecological drivers into data-driven phenology forecasting models.

**Why It Matters:** Climate change induces non-stationary, multi-faceted shifts in ecological data that conventional machine learning benchmarks rarely capture, making this a critical frontier for robust predictive modeling.

**Evidence:** Our findings encourage further exploration of this problem in order to achieve even stronger data-driven performance with for example the inclusion of additional drivers, or exploring Fourier-based temporal features for improved robustness.
