title: Aggregating Diverse DNM Indicators for Market Stability Forecasting
---

**Background:** Financial markets are complex, high-dimensional systems where diverse participant types interact through various trading activities, and different data representations capture distinct aspects of market dynamics. Integrating these heterogeneous data sources could improve the predictive performance and stability of early-warning markers.

**Question / Future Work:** Systematic methods for aggregating disparate time-series types—such as trading volumes, network centrality, and point process statistics—are needed to enhance the predictive power of DNM markers. Research is required to determine whether ensemble-based or other collective intelligence approaches can more reliably synthesize these distinct features into a single, unified, and more robust indicator of market instability.

**Why It Matters:** The study notes that individual time-series types show varying response timings; aggregating these is hypothesized to leverage collective intelligence to improve signal consistency and accuracy.

**Evidence:** Overall, the rise of SD_I,d_focal prior to several d_focal events across diverse time-series types supports the effectiveness of the DNM indicator... although systematic comparison among time-series types—or potential aggregation across them—remains an important direction for future work.
