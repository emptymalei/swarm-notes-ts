title: Distributed probabilistic L2-stabilization
---

**Background:** Distributed control of large-scale interconnected systems requires managing the propagation of uncertainties through the interconnection network. While data-driven probabilistic L2-gain stabilization has been developed for centralized systems, the extension to decentralized or distributed architectures remains an open challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** The data-driven probabilistic L2-gain stabilization framework needs to be extended to distributed control architectures for interconnected systems, including the development of distributed estimation algorithms that account for uncertainty propagation through the network.

**Why It Matters:** This extension is critical for scaling data-driven control methods to complex, large-scale industrial processes where centralized control is infeasible.

**Evidence:** An immediate future work is to extend this approach to distributed control of interconnected systems. The uncertainties for each subsystem propagate in the interconnection network and indirectly affect other subsystems, which means that the estimation algorithm also needs to be extended to a distributed version.
