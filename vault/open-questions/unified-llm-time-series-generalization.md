title: Unified Time-Series Generalization
---

**Background:** Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly adapted for multivariate time-series analysis through parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) methods, yet generalizable architectures capable of seamless cross-task transfer remain limited.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved challenge in developing universally applicable pre-trained LLM-based models that can effectively adapt to diverse temporal tasks—such as forecasting, classification, and anomaly detection—across varying domains without requiring extensive task-specific fine-tuning.

**Why It Matters:** Achieving a truly unified, cross-domain temporal foundation model would significantly reduce the computational and data labeling burdens currently required to deploy LLMs across the varied landscape of industrial and scientific time-series tasks.

**Evidence:** Advancing general time series analysis requires more flexible models capable of adapting to a wide range of tasks without extensive fine-tuning. An ideal model should be capable of handling diverse time-series applications and transferring knowledge across domains. Developing such universally applicable models remains a significant challenge.
