title: Causal Policy Counterfactuals in SDEs
---

**Background:** Modeling socioeconomic systems often requires accounting for external factors like policy changes or environmental shocks that influence dynamical trajectories. Current latent SDE approaches typically rely primarily on autonomous dynamics driven by noise.

**Question / Future Work:** Incorporate exogenous control variables or causal constraints into the latent SDE framework to allow for policy-counterfactual simulations and to explicitly model the impact of external interventions on socioeconomic evolution.

**Why It Matters:** Adding exogenous controls is essential for moving from purely descriptive modeling to predictive policy evaluation, which is a major open bottleneck in continuous-time time-series forecasting.

**Evidence:** Future work will focus on extending the framework to include ... exogenous control variables, and hierarchical spatial dependencies across districts. Additionally, integrating causal constraints and exploring policy-counterfactual simulations within the V-NSDE framework represent promising directions.
