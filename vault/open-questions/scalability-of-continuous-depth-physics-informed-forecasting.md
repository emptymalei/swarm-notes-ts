title: Scalability of continuous-depth forecasting
---

**Background:** Neural architectures for forecasting that utilize continuous-depth NODEs and physics-informed constraints are currently evaluated primarily at moderate spatial resolutions.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains unclear whether the benefits of continuous-depth physics-informed architectures for forecasting scale to high-resolution datasets and how they perform against large-scale, pre-trained forecasting models.

**Why It Matters:** Determining the scalability of continuous-time, physics-informed architectures is critical to establishing their utility for real-world, high-resolution operational weather forecasting.

**Evidence:** whether the observed gains persist at higher resolutions and against pretrained or larger-scale forecasters remains an open question.
