title: Spatio-temporal rainfall stochastic modeling
---

**Background:** Rainfall is a multiscale phenomenon influenced by complex topography and regional atmospheric dynamics, often described by stochastic processes to model intensity variability and intermittent occurrence. While various models exist to represent these dynamics, the lack of a universal distribution or robust framework to simulate rainfall across different environmental conditions remains a significant challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Extending point-process stochastic models like mean-reverting jump-diffusion to incorporate spatial dependencies and non-stationary stochastic forcing is critical for capturing long-term climate-induced changes in rainfall extremes. This research direction focuses on bridging the gap between localized temporal simulation and regional, multiscale climate impact modeling.

**Why It Matters:** This question addresses a fundamental limitation in current temporal stochastic models regarding their generalization to regional spatiotemporal variability and non-stationary environmental shifts.

**Evidence:** One natural extension is to incorporate spatio-temporal correlations to study how extreme rainfall events propagate across regions and interact with larger-scale atmospheric variability... Another direction is to explore parameterized stochastic forcing to investigate the emergence of extreme events and multifractal scaling under slowly varying environmental conditions.
