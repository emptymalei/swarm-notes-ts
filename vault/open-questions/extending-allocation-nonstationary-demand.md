title: Extending allocation to non-stationary demand
---

**Background:** Marketplaces often operate in dynamic, non-stationary environments where demand patterns such as seasonality, trends, and regime shifts are present. Current models for demand-allocation mechanisms largely rely on the assumption of stationary demand processes.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is required to extend the current demand-allocation framework to non-stationary demand environments. Developing allocation rules that adapt to seasonality, long-term trends, and sudden regime shifts would bridge the gap between idealized stationary models and the operational realities faced by digital marketplaces.

**Why It Matters:** Real-world demand is rarely stationary, and allocation policies that fail to account for time-varying patterns may be sub-optimal or fail to provide the intended inventory-shaping effects.
