title: Model Failure Tail Dependence
---

**Background:** Machine learning model failure extrapolation methods currently rely on specific tail-shape assumptions that may be violated by the presence of rare, high-risk failure modes.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to understand how failure tail shape depends on model size, training data, and post-training procedures, as well as to develop a taxonomy of tail shapes based on task distribution and risk score categories.

**Why It Matters:** This is crucial for scaling pre-deployment safety assessments to larger models and diverse real-world deployment domains where failure tail behavior may not be well-behaved under current assumptions.

**Evidence:** how failure-tail shape depends on model size, training data, and post-training procedure is open.
