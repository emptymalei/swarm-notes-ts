title: Improving Federated Inference Alignment
---

**Background:** Federated inference frameworks typically rely on the availability of common input data or shared encoder architectures to achieve collaboration across independently deployed models. Achieving this cooperation in heterogeneous environments without shared inputs or encoder parameters remains a significant challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to develop more robust ensemble strategies and alignment techniques that can minimize the performance gap between consensus-embedding-based federated inference and idealized cooperative inference. Enhancing the quality and semantic separability of the consensus embedding space is identified as the central bottleneck for improving framework performance across diverse tasks.

**Why It Matters:** This is identified as the primary performance bottleneck. Improving these components is essential for the practical viability of the proposed framework, especially for modality-specific tasks where current generic ensemble strategies show limitations.
