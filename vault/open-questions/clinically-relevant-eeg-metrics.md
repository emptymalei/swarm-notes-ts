title: Clinically Relevant EEG Benchmarking
---

**Background:** Foundation models for EEG are often evaluated using generic performance metrics that frequently fail to capture clinical utility and practical diagnostic requirements.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop and standardize metrics that align model performance with domain-specific clinical utility, such as event-level reliability in epilepsy or hypnogram-derived accuracy in sleep staging, rather than relying solely on aggregate accuracy metrics.

**Why It Matters:** This question is crucial for bridging the gap between algorithmic performance in benchmarks and actual effectiveness in clinical settings.

**Evidence:** Aggregate metrics such as AUROC or accuracy can hide failure modes that determine real-world usability, so each domain demands bespoke, clinically grounded evaluation.
