title: Theoretical limits of FCNs
---

**Background:** Fully convolutional networks (FCNs) are widely used for time series analysis, yet their asymptotic behavior is often studied under restrictive assumptions such as short-range dependent input processes. Theoretical characterizations currently remain underdeveloped for non-stationary processes or complex architectures.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the asymptotic behavior of FCNs under non-stationary or long-range dependent input conditions remains a significant challenge for time series theory. Further extension is required to understand how these properties evolve during non-linear training dynamics rather than just fixed-parameter analysis.

**Why It Matters:** Essential for robust time series forecasting where data often exhibits non-stationarity and long-range dependencies, bridging the gap between empirical success and formal statistical guarantees.

**Evidence:** Obtaining an unconditional statement is much harder.
