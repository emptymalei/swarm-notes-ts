title: Morphologically Informed Neighbor Selection
---

**Background:** Binary stellar evolution simulations involve complex, coupled interactions that produce irregularly sampled temporal data with discontinuous morphological features. Developing robust interpolation methods that preserve these physical relationships across varying initial conditions remains a significant challenge in computational astrophysics.

**Question / Future Work:** Neighbor selection often fails in regions of the parameter space where physical outcomes are highly sensitive to initial conditions. There is an unresolved need for alternative weighting schemes or morphological similarity metrics that can inform the selection of neighbors to avoid interpolating across structurally distinct evolutionary paths.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental bottleneck for interpolation accuracy in sparse or highly non-linear regions of the parameter space, directly impacting the reliability of large-scale population synthesis studies.
