title: Accelerating 3D Atomic Reconstruction Inference
---

**Background:** Reconstructing 3D atomic structures from single 2D projection images is a highly ill-posed inverse problem that currently requires iterative optimization loops, such as simulated annealing coupled with molecular dynamics, to ensure physical plausibility.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop faster inference methods, such as machine-learning-based surrogates, that can match the physical fidelity of current iterative simulated annealing and molecular dynamics frameworks while significantly reducing computational time for large-scale dynamic datasets.

**Why It Matters:** Computational efficiency is the primary bottleneck for scaling this 3D reconstruction approach to large-field-of-view dynamic studies or high-throughput material characterization tasks.
