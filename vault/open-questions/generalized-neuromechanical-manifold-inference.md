title: Generalized Neuromechanical Manifold Inference
---

**Background:** Neuromechanics aims to model the complex relationship between neural activity and physical locomotion using low-dimensional dynamical systems. Despite progress in identifying these systems in model organisms, a universal framework that reliably maps neural activity to behavior across diverse species and environmental contexts remains unrealized.

**Question / Future Work:** A significant open challenge is the development of a generalized, data-driven approach that can automatically infer the appropriate dimensionality and manifold structure of neuromechanical dynamics without imposing restrictive, manually specified geometric constraints, particularly when dealing with noisy or partial neural recordings.

**Why It Matters:** Removing the need for manual constraint selection is essential for scaling neuromechanical analysis to novel species where the underlying dynamical manifold is not known a priori.
