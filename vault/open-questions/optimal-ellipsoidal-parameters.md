title: Optimal Ellipsoidal Decomposition Parameters
---

**Background:** The ellipsoidal decomposition framework decomposes a target distribution on a Euclidean space into an infinite mixture of component distributions defined on a nested sequence of compact ellipsoidal sets.

**Question / Future Work:** The framework currently lacks a general, automated procedure for selecting the radii and the total number of ellipsoidal regions, often relying on heuristic guidelines. Developing a rigorous theoretical basis for these parameter choices is essential to improve the method's computational efficiency and convergence properties.

**Why It Matters:** Determining optimal parameters is critical for balancing the coalescence time of perfect sampling against the number of regions, which directly impacts computational efficiency.

**Evidence:** The framework requires choosing radii and the total number of regions. While heuristic guidelines exist, theoretical results on optimal choices would be valuable.
