title: Fidelity-Decision Value Thresholds
---

**Background:** Energy system capacity expansion models typically rely on coarse spatial and temporal resolution to maintain computational tractability, which can mask critical planning dynamics. Systematic evaluation of the decision value versus the computational cost of higher-resolution modeling is currently lacking.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an open research gap in determining the 'fidelity threshold'—the specific spatio-temporal resolution required for different energy infrastructure planning contexts—above which additional modeling detail provides only marginal improvement in decision quality versus significant increases in computational burden. Future work is needed to systematically compare high-resolution and aggregated models across diverse decision-making scenarios to quantify this trade-off.

**Why It Matters:** Defining these thresholds is critical for moving beyond 'more fidelity is always better' towards computationally efficient models tailored to specific decision needs, thereby bridging the gap between academic model complexity and practical utility for policymakers and system operators.
