title: Asymptotic Distribution of FLOC-based Measures
---

**Background:** Cyclostationary time series models are conventionally analyzed using the autocovariance function, which is mathematically invalid for infinite-variance heavy-tailed processes. While fractional lower-order covariance (FLOC) provides an alternative dependence measure for such processes, the derivation of asymptotic properties for estimators based on these measures remains underdeveloped.

**Question / Future Work:** The proposed methodology relies on Monte Carlo simulations for validation and determining critical regions or confidence intervals. A theoretical derivation of the asymptotic distribution for the sample periodic fractional lower-order autocorrelation function (peFLOACF) and periodic fractional lower-order partial autocorrelation function (peFLOPACF) is needed to establish formal statistical inference properties and refine hypothesis testing.

**Why It Matters:** Analytical asymptotic results would eliminate the reliance on computationally expensive simulation-based methods for setting significance thresholds and confidence intervals, providing more robust theoretical guarantees for the proposed tests and order identification procedures.
