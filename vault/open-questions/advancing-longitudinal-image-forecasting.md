title: Advancing Longitudinal Image Forecasting
---

**Background:** Existing image-on-image regression models for longitudinal neuroimaging data generally rely on linear assumptions or do not effectively incorporate patch-based spatial context, limiting their utility for complex biomarker dynamics.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to develop more robust and scalable nonlinear image-on-image regression frameworks that can seamlessly integrate multi-modal imaging data while accounting for complex longitudinal dynamics, potentially moving beyond the currently proposed patch-to-voxel mapping for even higher-dimensional data structures.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing these limitations is critical for developing prognostic models that can reliably forecast future neurobiological changes in neurodegenerative diseases like Alzheimer's, as current methods fail to capture the complex, nonlinear evolution of biomarkers over time.

**Evidence:** While there is an increasing literature on IIR models, there are several unmet needs... applications of IIR methods to longitudinal image forecasting using imaging data collected over multiple visits are limited... most methods assume linear dependencies... the adoption of GPs to tackle non-linearity in TOT regression models is limited... the patch-to-voxel concept has not yet been incorporated into TOT regression frameworks.
