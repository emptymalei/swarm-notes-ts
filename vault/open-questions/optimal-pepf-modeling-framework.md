title: Optimal Modeling Framework for PEPF
---

**Background:** Probabilistic electricity price forecasting (PEPF) aims to quantify uncertainty in market outcomes to improve operational and economic decision-making. Recent developments have introduced foundation models (TSFMs) alongside traditional, task-specific deep learning methods for this task.

**Question / Future Work:** There is no consensus on the optimal modeling framework for PEPF, as both foundation models and task-specific architectures offer distinct advantages in performance, flexibility, and computational efficiency. Future research is required to evaluate emerging foundation models and optimize loss functions specifically for generating well-calibrated, reliable predictive distributions in renewable-integrated electricity systems.

**Why It Matters:** This is a central benchmarking question for the field, as it determines whether the massive pre-training paradigm of foundation models provides a net benefit over specialized, often more interpretable or efficient, domain-specific models in energy forecasting contexts.
