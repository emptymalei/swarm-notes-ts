title: Scalability of Digital Twin Frameworks
---

**Background:** Digital twin implementations for data center energy management are currently challenged by the limited ability to generalize results from small-scale, constrained, or partially simulated environments to large-scale, heterogeneous, and real-world data center infrastructures.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to transition digital twin frameworks from controlled, small-scale testbeds to complex, large-scale, and heterogeneous data center environments, specifically validating the scalability of predictive models and energy optimization strategies across diverse operational conditions.

**Why It Matters:** Validating performance in production-scale environments is critical to determine the practical viability and reliability of digital twin-based energy optimization beyond academic or lab settings.
