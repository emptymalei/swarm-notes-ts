title: Long-Horizon Supply Chain Forecasting
---

**Background:** Supply chain disruption forecasting requires reasoning about rare, high-impact events from noisy, unstructured data, often suffering from temporal gaps between data availability and event realization.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work needs to extend current binary, short-horizon forecasting models toward longer time horizons and continuous outcome variables to capture persistence and cumulative risk, while ensuring robustness against regime shifts.

**Why It Matters:** This represents a critical performance bottleneck in moving from simple detection to reliable long-term risk assessment in supply chains.

**Evidence:** Our formulation also focuses on one-month-ahead binary events, which abstracts from richer dynamics such as persistence and multi-period risk accumulation. Extending the framework to longer horizons or continuous outcomes would provide a more complete characterization of supply chain risk.
