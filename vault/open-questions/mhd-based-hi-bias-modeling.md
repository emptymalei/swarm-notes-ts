title: MHD-based HI bias modeling
---

**Background:** Neutral hydrogen (HI) bias at small, nonlinear cosmological scales is typically approximated using fits from standard hydrodynamical simulations, which may not capture complex physics at high redshifts or in non-standard cosmological models.

**Question / Future Work:** Future cosmological forecasts require high-resolution magnetohydrodynamical (MHD) simulations that self-consistently compute bias functions for non-standard scenarios, such as primordial magnetic field-induced perturbations, to replace reliance on simplified LCDM-based fitting functions.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental bottleneck for precision cosmology as we move beyond standard LCDM assumptions in interpreting non-linear tracers.

**Evidence:** In addition, over the course of this study, ΛCDM-fitted nonlinear bias functions have been used to theoretically model the Lyα and 21 cm spectra as biased tracers of the matter spectrum at small scales, whereas a more accurate analysis demands a dedicated high-resolution MHD simulation-based computation of the bias functions for the specific PMF-driven scenario under consideration.
