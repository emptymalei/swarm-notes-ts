title: Scaling Validation for Climate Datasets
---

**Background:** Climate-specialized agents increasingly rely on tool-augmented pipelines for multi-step reasoning, yet the robustness of these agents is hindered by the lack of large-scale, region-specific, and multimodal datasets for training and evaluation.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a critical need to scale human-in-the-loop validation for climate-grounded datasets to reduce label noise and grounding errors, which currently limit the reliability of evidence-based policy synthesis.

**Why It Matters:** Scaling validation is essential for moving climate agent research from semi-automated prototypes to reliable, operational decision-support systems.

**Evidence:** First, coverage and validation of GCA-DS are semi validated: although the dataset is large and semi-automatically curated, only a subset is human-verified, and automated QA generation can introduce subtle label or grounding errors.
