title: Distributed probabilistic L2-gain stabilization
---

**Background:** Data-driven control of interconnected systems requires coordination between subsystems to ensure stability and performance.

**Question / Future Work:** Extend the developed data-driven probabilistic L2-gain stabilization approach to distributed control of interconnected systems, particularly focusing on how estimation error covariances propagate through the interconnection network and how to manage them in a distributed manner.

**Why It Matters:** Large-scale dynamical systems are frequently controlled in a distributed fashion. Extending this framework is essential for maintaining probabilistic stability guarantees in complex, networked control environments.

**Evidence:** An immediate future work is to extend this approach to distributed control of interconnected systems. The uncertainties for each subsystem propagate in the interconnection network and indirectly affect other subsystems, which means that the estimation algorithm also needs to be extended to a distributed version.
