title: Scaling JJ Reservoir Systems
---

**Background:** Reservoir computing architectures based on Josephson junctions (JJs) utilize nonlinear superconducting dynamics for temporal information processing, and recent approaches have explored single-junction configurations without explicit delay-based feedback.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains to be investigated how scaling up to networks of multiple Josephson junctions, or integrating explicit delay-based mechanisms, influences the memory capacity and computational complexity of superconducting reservoir computers compared to a single-junction configuration.

**Why It Matters:** Determining the scaling laws and necessary architectural augmentations is critical for the transition of superconducting neuromorphic hardware from single-device proof-of-concept to complex, production-scale information processing.

**Evidence:** Furthermore, networks of multiple Josephson junctions may provide increased dynamical complexity and improved nonlinear processing capabilities.
