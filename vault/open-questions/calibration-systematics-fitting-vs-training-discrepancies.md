title: Supernova calibration systematic discrepancies
---

**Background:** Calibration systematics in Type Ia supernova light-curve models impact cosmological parameter inference differently depending on whether they originate from the training phase or the light-curve fitting phase.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains unclear why calibration-induced effects on the Dark Energy Figure of Merit demonstrate non-linear behaviors and counter-intuitive trends when comparing training-only, fitting-only, and combined training-and-fitting scenarios. Further investigation is required to identify the root cause of these discrepancies, which are significant for meeting the sub-percent level calibration requirements of next-generation cosmology surveys.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding these calibration-induced systematics is vital for next-generation dark energy experiments that require sub-percent level control of systematic uncertainties.

**Evidence:** we naively expect that Train+Fit should always have smaller FoM compared to Fit, we see the opposite trend for some shift values, particularly for shift > 5.
