title: Edge Deployment of Transformers
---

**Background:** Transformer architectures often require substantial memory and computational resources, limiting their feasibility for real-time applications on resource-constrained hardware.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is required to develop lightweight versions of attention-based forecasting models suitable for deployment on edge devices and rural power stations. This involves investigating techniques such as model pruning, quantization, efficient attention approximations, and specialized regularization to reduce memory and computational overhead.

**Why It Matters:** This is a significant barrier to the real-world adoption of high-performance transformer models in decentralized energy management.

**Evidence:** the computational and memory demands of the employed architecture pose challenges for deployment on resource-constrained (or edge) devices.
