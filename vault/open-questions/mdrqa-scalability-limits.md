title: Scaling MdRQA for complexity
---

**Background:** Multidimensional Recurrence Quantification Analysis (MdRQA) offers a powerful framework for capturing collective dynamics in multivariate systems but faces significant computational and dimensionality-related challenges when applied to high-dimensional pose trajectories.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research must address the computational scaling limits and feature-selection strategies for applying MdRQA to high-dimensional pose data, specifically identifying how to prioritize informative anatomical subsets or latent representations that preserve the integrity of collective system-level coordination without requiring exhaustive or computationally prohibitive analysis.

**Why It Matters:** The current lack of principled, automated feature-selection methods for high-dimensional MdRQA creates a barrier for analyzing complex multi-agent or whole-body interactions where collective patterns are non-reducible to pairwise couplings.
