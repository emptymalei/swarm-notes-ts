title: Adaptive thresholding for complex dependence
---

**Background:** While adaptive thresholding methods for covariance matrix estimation have been extended to weakly dependent time series by incorporating long-run variance estimators, the current theoretical results are established for relatively simple dependent processes. The performance of these methods under more complex, general dependence structures, such as those with non-linear dynamics or more intricate memory decay properties, is not yet fully understood.

**Question / Future Work:** The theoretical performance and support recovery consistency of adaptive thresholding estimators for covariance matrices in high-dimensional time series remain to be rigorously established for general, complex dependence structures beyond the currently studied weakly dependent processes.

**Why It Matters:** Real-world high-dimensional data frequently exhibit complex temporal dependencies. Proving the robustness of these estimators in more general settings is essential for their widespread applicability.
