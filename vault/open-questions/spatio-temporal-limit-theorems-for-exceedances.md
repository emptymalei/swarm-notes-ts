title: Spatio-temporal limit theorems for exceedances
---

**Background:** The statistical modeling of spatio-temporal extreme events typically relies on limit theorems for point processes of exceedances. While asymptotic behavior is well-established for univariate and purely spatial or purely temporal cases, comprehensive frameworks for general spatio-temporal processes remain a significant area of development.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to extend existing limit results for spatio-temporal point processes to account for more complex, non-stationary or long-range dependent structures. Further research is required to analyze how different forms of spatio-temporal dependence, beyond the mixing and local dependence conditions currently assumed, affect the convergence of exceedance processes.

**Why It Matters:** Developing these theoretical foundations is essential for improving risk assessment and modeling of climate-related extremes, which are inherently complex and spatio-temporal in nature.

**Evidence:** However, limit results for such spatio-temporal point processes are still missing in the literature.
