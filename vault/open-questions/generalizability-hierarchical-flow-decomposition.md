title: Generalizability of Hierarchical Forecasting
---

**Background:** Traffic forecasting models often vary in performance across different urban environments due to differences in road geometry and traffic demand patterns.

**Question / Future Work:** Evaluating the generalizability of hierarchical flow decomposition frameworks across diverse urban corridors with varying intersection geometries and traffic demand profiles is essential for broader practical deployment.

**Why It Matters:** The lack of validated generalizability across diverse topologies remains a significant barrier to the widespread adoption of specialized traffic forecasting models.

**Evidence:** Evaluation on additional corridors with varying geometric configurations is a natural next step to assess generalizability further.
