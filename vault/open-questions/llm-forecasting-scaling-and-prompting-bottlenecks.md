title: LLM Forecasting Scaling and Prompting Bottlenecks
---

**Background:** Large language models (LLMs) are increasingly integrated into time series forecasting to leverage semantic priors, but current approaches often rely on synchronous fusion strategies that may induce semantic-perceptual dissonance. The effectiveness of LLM-based guidance depends on how abstract textual semantics are fused with fine-grained numerical temporal dynamics.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is needed to determine how scaling TimeSAF to larger foundation models impacts performance, and to develop more sophisticated methods for converting time-series data into rich, unstructured semantic prompts rather than relying on current rule-based templates.

**Why It Matters:** The scalability and the method of prompt engineering are fundamental bottlenecks for the broader adoption and effectiveness of LLM-empowered forecasting models.
