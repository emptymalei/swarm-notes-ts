title: Advancing Observation-native Earth Models
---

**Background:** Atmospheric and Earth system modeling traditionally relies on projecting heterogeneous, multi-source raw observational data onto predefined spatial grids, a process that inherently discards significant information and introduces computational bottlenecks.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed to integrate formal uncertainty quantification and expand the latent space to accommodate diverse instrument modalities (e.g., seismic, phased-array) while improving interpretability of the latent dynamics.

**Why It Matters:** Identifies specific technical bottlenecks regarding uncertainty, modal generalization, and scientific interpretability essential for evolving these simulators into foundational scientific discovery tools.
