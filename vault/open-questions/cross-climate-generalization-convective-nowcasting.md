title: Cross-Climate Nowcasting Generalization
---

**Background:** Precipitation nowcasting models often rely on short-term radar extrapolation, which struggles to maintain accuracy beyond 30-60 minutes due to the complexity of underlying atmospheric thermodynamic and microphysical processes.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains an open challenge to determine the extent to which physics-informed, multi-modal fusion models trained on regional convective systems can generalize across diverse climatic regimes and varying topographies without overfitting to local sensor characteristics.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the limits of cross-climate generalization is crucial for the deployment of machine learning-based weather forecasting tools in global operational environments.

**Evidence:** Future work will extend our evaluation to diverse climatic zones (e.g., the SEVIR benchmark in the USA [57]) to empirically verify this cross-domain robustness.
