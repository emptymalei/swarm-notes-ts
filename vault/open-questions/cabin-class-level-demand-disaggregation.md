title: Cabin Class Demand Disaggregation
---

**Background:** Airline demand forecasting is typically performed on aggregate load factors across all service classes. Increasing the granularity of demand forecasting by separating predictions by cabin class is essential for precision seat inventory management.

**Question / Future Work:** A research challenge exists in disaggregating total passenger load factor predictions into class-specific (e.g., economy, business, first) demand forecasts, which exhibit distinct stochastic and behavioral patterns. Developing frameworks that model these class-specific dependencies without sacrificing the global constraints of total capacity is an open problem.

**Why It Matters:** Class-specific modeling is critical for revenue management and optimizing seat inventory in multi-class cabin environments.
