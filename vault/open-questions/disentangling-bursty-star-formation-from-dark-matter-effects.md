title: Disentangling Bursty Star Formation Effects
---

**Background:** Spectroscopic observations show that star formation in galaxies at z ~ 7–12 is highly stochastic, with significant variation in star formation rates over short timescales.

**Question / Future Work:** The bursty nature of star formation leads to inflated ultraviolet luminosity scatter, which mimics the effects of dark matter physics on the luminosity function, creating a potential observational degeneracy. Future work is required to distinguish this astrophysical burstiness from dark matter-induced effects, potentially by utilizing higher-order statistics such as the 21 cm bispectrum or more granular galaxy clustering metrics.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing this degeneracy is crucial for preventing the misinterpretation of astrophysical stochasticity as evidence for, or constraints against, dark matter models.

**Evidence:** This burstiness inflates the effective σUV and broadens the UVLF, mimicking one of the two channels through which the profile likelihood absorbs the SIDM signal... Separating astrophysical burstiness from SIDM-induced effects may require the 21 cm bispectrum or galaxy clustering statistics.
