title: Pseudo-label Imprecision in World Models
---

**Background:** Autonomous driving world models often rely on pseudo-labels derived from pretrained foundation models, which may exhibit inherent imprecision when applied to driving-specific environmental tasks. The dependency on these imperfect labels poses a fundamental bottleneck for further improvements in planning performance.

**Question / Future Work:** The performance of perception-free driving world models remains constrained by the precision and reliability of the pseudo depth and semantic labels generated by foundational models used for self-supervision. Research into more robust or self-correcting methods for generating these labels without human annotation is required to close the performance gap between perception-free and perception-based systems.

**Why It Matters:** This identifies a foundational limitation for the scalability of perception-free autonomous driving, highlighting a bottleneck in existing self-supervised world model training.

**Evidence:** However, our model does not yet surpass SOTA perception-based models, primarily due to challenges arising from the inherent imprecision of the pseudo depth and semantic labels generated by pretrained foundational models.
