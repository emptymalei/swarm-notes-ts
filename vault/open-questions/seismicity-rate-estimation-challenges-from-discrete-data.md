title: Unobservable Seismicity Rate Estimation
---

**Background:** The seismicity rate in underground reservoirs, a key parameter for managing induced seismic risks, is not directly observable and must be estimated from discrete, stochastic event catalogs.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing robust methodologies to accurately infer the underlying, continuous seismicity rate from sparse or stochastic earthquake event data remains a fundamental challenge for real-time control. This inference is particularly sensitive to the temporal and spatial resolution of the seismic monitoring network and the stochastic nature of fault reactivation.

**Why It Matters:** Effective closed-loop control of seismic risks requires a reliable, real-time estimate of the state variable (seismicity rate). Without an accurate estimation method, feedback controllers cannot be effectively tuned or deployed in industrial settings.
