title: Refining Supernova Light Curve Noise
---

**Background:** Photometric error estimation in time-domain astronomy often relies on image metadata that omits contributions from template images in difference-based photometry, leading to systemic noise discrepancies.

**Question / Future Work:** The discrepancy between simulated flux noise and real-world difference-image photometry remains a bottleneck for high-fidelity survey modeling. Future work is required to identify and incorporate the missing error contributions from template images to ensure simulation-based inference aligns with observed data.

**Why It Matters:** Accurate noise estimation is critical for characterizing systematic biases and for reliable cosmological inference in time-domain surveys; discrepancies between simulations and real data hinder the validation of analysis pipelines.
