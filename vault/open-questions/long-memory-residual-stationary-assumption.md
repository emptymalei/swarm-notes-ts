title: Long-memory residual time series
---

**Background:** Spherically embedded time series are often assumed to be stationary in existing methodologies, but empirical data frequently exhibit non-stationary behaviors such as long-memory processes in the residuals.

**Question / Future Work:** The current framework requires the final residual time series of the decomposition to be stationary. Extending this to handle long-memory residual processes remains an open challenge.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing non-stationarity beyond simple trend and periodicity is necessary for broader applicability to real-world time series.

**Evidence:** While we require that the final residual time series is stationary, sometime the residual process may have long-memory, and extensions to deal with long-memory residuals can be explored.
