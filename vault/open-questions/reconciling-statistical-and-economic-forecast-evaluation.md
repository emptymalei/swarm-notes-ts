title: Reconciling Statistical and Economic Evaluation
---

**Background:** Economic performance in decision-focused forecasting (e.g., battery arbitrage) often acts as a proxy for model quality, yet this metric can diverge significantly from strictly proper statistical scoring rules.

**Question / Future Work:** It is unresolved how to systematically reconcile statistical forecast evaluation with decision quality in application-specific contexts, particularly when decision-based performance measures fail to serve as strictly proper scoring rules. Further research is required to create evaluation diagnostics that bridge the gap between statistical accuracy and realized economic value.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental barrier to establishing reliable, end-to-end forecasting workflows where the objective is to maximize utility rather than purely minimizing loss.

**Evidence:** How reliable is the economic evaluation of forecasting models though (simplified) application studies – and how do improvements in statistical forecast quality for stochastic programs relate to the decision-quality and economic performance?
