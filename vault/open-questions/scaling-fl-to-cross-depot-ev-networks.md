title: Cross-depot federated learning scalability
---

**Background:** EV charging infrastructures are inherently distributed, leading to privacy and operational constraints that necessitate collaborative learning models without centralized raw data access. While federated learning (FL) is applicable to intra-depot settings, its efficacy at scale across diverse, geographically separated charging sites remains to be characterized.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to evaluate the performance of federated learning frameworks when extended from a single-depot (intra-depot) setting to a multi-depot (cross-depot) setting, assessing how data heterogeneity, communication overhead, and client participation models behave when scaling across disparate charging infrastructures.

**Why It Matters:** Scaling federated learning from single sites to wider, multi-depot networks is essential for building real-world, industry-scale predictive systems that remain privacy-compliant.
