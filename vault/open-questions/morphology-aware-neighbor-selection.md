title: Morphology-Aware Neighbor Selection
---

**Background:** Astrophysical simulation grids are often sparse in parameter space, making the selection of neighbor tracks for interpolation challenging when neighboring systems exhibit significant morphological differences.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need for robust neighbor selection mechanisms in high-dimensional astrophysical parameter spaces, particularly when existing interpolation methods require a fixed number of neighbors and sparse regions can lead to the selection of morphologically dissimilar tracks that produce artifacts. Future research should investigate weighting schemes that accommodate variable numbers of neighbors or incorporate morphological similarity metrics directly into the selection process.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental bottleneck for improving the accuracy and reliability of interpolation in sparse regions of astrophysical model grids.
