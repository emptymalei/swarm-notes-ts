title: Molecular Modeling in Evolved Stars
---

**Background:** Red supergiant (RSG) and asymptotic giant branch (AGB) stars exhibit extended atmospheres that are vital for mass-loss processes, yet current theoretical models struggle to replicate the atmospheric structure and shock-driven trigger mechanisms observed in long-term interferometric time-series data. Current radiation-hydrodynamics models often rely on opacity approximations that may misrepresent molecular species formation and resulting density profiles in the extended atmosphere.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop and validate 3D radiation-hydrodynamics models of RSG and AGB stars that incorporate wavelength-dependent opacities to accurately capture the formation and spatial distribution of molecular species in the extended atmosphere. Resolving the discrepancy between modeled and observed molecular layer extensions is essential for understanding how convective shocks and pulsation-mode switching drive stellar mass-loss.

**Why It Matters:** The discrepancy between synthetic and observed molecular layer extensions suggests that current astrophysical models lack critical physical components necessary for accurately characterizing the initiation of stellar mass loss.

**Evidence:** This difference could be due to the gray Rosseland opacity approximation in the CO5BOLD model, which may result in a higher temperature in the upper atmosphere and thus prevent formation of some molecular species and dust.
