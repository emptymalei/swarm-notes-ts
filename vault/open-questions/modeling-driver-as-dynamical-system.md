title: Modeling Driver as Dynamical System
---

**Background:** In autonomous driving, world models typically focus on forecasting external environmental dynamics, whereas in-cabin intelligence often treats the driver as a passive object for recognition tasks.

**Question / Future Work:** Integrating multi-modal driver dynamics—the continuous evolution of physical posture and internal cognitive state—into a unified, traffic-conditioned latent world model remains an open challenge, particularly for maintaining accuracy over long-horizon, safety-critical shared-control transitions where human reaction patterns are inherently multi-modal.

**Why It Matters:** Transitioning from static recognition to dynamic world modeling for human drivers is critical for anticipating safety-critical reactions in shared-control systems.

**Evidence:** in-cabin intelligence remains strictly recognition-oriented and lacks multi-step rollout capabilities for driver dynamics.
