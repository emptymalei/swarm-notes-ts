title: DRL-HMP Training Scalability Bottleneck
---

**Background:** Deep reinforcement learning (DRL) frameworks for forecasting multivariate Hidden Markov Processes often rely on multi-stage training schemes to manage state-space complexity and training stability. These frameworks face a computational bottleneck in high-dimensional settings as the training time scales significantly with the number of variables.

**Question / Future Work:** Addressing the computational scaling challenges when coordinating a large number of variables is necessary to improve the practicality of DRL-based state-aware forecasting frameworks in time-sensitive or resource-constrained large-scale settings.

**Why It Matters:** As these frameworks are applied to increasingly high-dimensional real-world data, training efficiency becomes a primary limiting factor for widespread deployment.
