title: Integrating Simplified Climate Metrics
---

**Background:** Integrated Assessment Models (IAMs) provide complex, high-dimensional projections for climate scenarios, often making it difficult to analyze and compare mitigation strategies across different models.

**Question / Future Work:** A comprehensive framework is required to account for the variables and policy nuances left unrepresented by simplified, one-dimensional metrics such as decarbonization speed. Current approaches prioritize carbon intensity as a summary statistic, but this omits significant information regarding upstream socioeconomic drivers and downstream climate outcomes, necessitating methods that reconcile these reduced metrics with the high-dimensional reality of IAM datasets.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the relationship between reduced metrics and high-dimensional model output is critical for accurately communicating the policy implications of climate scenarios to stakeholders who need to grasp both the overall trend and the specific drivers of a transition.
