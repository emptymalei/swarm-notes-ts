title: Isolating transient multifractal signatures
---

**Background:** Multifractal Detrended Fluctuation Analysis (MFDFA) is frequently applied to characterize the scaling properties of non-stationary signals.

**Question / Future Work:** Current MFDFA techniques often struggle to distinguish between transient physical events (e.g., black hole mergers) and persistent background instrumental noise, as long analysis windows tend to dilute the signal of the transient. Developing methods to isolate the multifractal signature of short-duration transient events from background noise remains a critical open problem.

**Why It Matters:** This is a core limitation in applying multifractal analysis to real-world transient-driven data like gravitational waves.

**Evidence:** it requires shorter, localized windows or alternative multiscale approaches to effectively trigger on short-duration transients.
