title: Limitations of Fisher matrix forecasts
---

**Background:** Fisher matrix forecasts provide a standard method for estimating the sensitivity of future experiments to cosmological parameters, but they are subject to limitations such as the assumption of Gaussian likelihoods and simplified noise models.

**Question / Future Work:** Current Fisher matrix forecasts for neutrino self-interactions rely on assumptions that neglect non-Gaussian covariances, realistic foreground contaminations, and complex instrumental systematic effects. MCMC analyses incorporating these realistic observational factors are needed for robust survey planning.

**Why It Matters:** Fisher matrix forecasts often yield overly optimistic bounds, making their validation through realistic simulations essential for reliable scientific interpretation.

**Evidence:** Our forecasts rely on the Fisher matrix formalism and therefore represent optimistic lower bounds on the achievable uncertainties; a full MCMC analysis incorporating realistic foreground models, beam systematics, and non-Gaussian covariances would improve the picture considerably.
