title: Generalizing Clinical Timeline Reconstruction
---

**Background:** Clinical timeline reconstruction requires accurate temporal ordering and absolute timestamp assignment for patient events, yet narrative text often provides rich semantic context while lacking temporal precision, whereas structured EHR data provides precise anchors but lacks event coverage.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the generalizability of multimodal scaffolded timeline reconstruction across different clinical conditions and evaluating the utility of the central-anchor decomposition method in settings where structured temporal evidence is absent remains an important research frontier.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the generalizability and modularity of the proposed multistep reconstruction pipeline is critical for healthcare informatics where documentation styles vary across clinical settings.

**Evidence:** It will also be important to study generalization beyond sepsis... Another key question is how the framework should adapt when multimodal data is unavailable.
