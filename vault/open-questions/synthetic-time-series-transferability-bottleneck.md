title: Synthetic Time Series Transferability
---

**Background:** Synthetic time series generation is increasingly explored for model training, but findings often indicate inconsistent or negative transfer to real-world tasks.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining which structural properties of synthetic generators ensure positive transfer across diverse forecasting architectures and datasets, avoiding the harmful distribution mismatches often observed with naive augmentation.

**Why It Matters:** Vital for transitioning from empirical trial-and-error to systematic, reliable synthesis methods in time series forecasting.

**Evidence:** The effect is sharply architecture-conditional... Averaged across all architectures, augmentation hurts in 67% of trials.
