title: Emissions Forecasting Generalization Failure
---

**Background:** Carbon emission prediction at the entity level often suffers from fragmented data sources, inconsistent schemas across regions, and a lack of standardized evaluation protocols.

**Question / Future Work:** Current entity-level emission models exhibit catastrophic performance drops during cross-city transfer and demonstrate an inherent ceiling when relying on simplistic sector-factor lookup methods. Investigating why current foundation models and multimodal architectures fail to bridge this regional generalization gap remains an open research priority.

**Why It Matters:** These failure modes represent the most significant bottlenecks to deploying predictive emission models across diverse geographic and structural contexts.
