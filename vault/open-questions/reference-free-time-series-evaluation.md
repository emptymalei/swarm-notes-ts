title: Reliable Time-Series Explanation Evaluation
---

**Background:** Large language models (LLMs) are increasingly used to generate natural language explanations for time-series data, yet automated methods for verifying the factual faithfulness of these explanations to the underlying numerical signals remain limited. Existing evaluation frameworks often rely on reference-based metrics or classical methods incapable of processing free-form textual reasoning.

**Question / Future Work:** The core challenge is developing a robust, general-purpose, reference-free evaluation framework that confirms LLM-generated interpretations of time-series data are both numerically accurate and faithful to the input signal. Future research must address evaluation robustness on complex patterns and explore hybrid systems that integrate model-based assessment with symbolic or formal verification methods.

**Why It Matters:** Ensuring the reliability of LLMs in high-stakes time-series domains like finance, climate, and healthcare is critical, as automated reasoning must be demonstrably grounded in the underlying data.
