title: Multivariate Spatiotemporal Regionalization Frameworks
---

**Background:** Regionalization frameworks typically partition spatial units based on univariate indicators, limiting their ability to model complex systems characterized by multiple interacting variables.

**Question / Future Work:** Extending information-theoretic regionalization to multivariate datasets remains a significant methodological hurdle for capturing holistic spatiotemporal interactions.

**Why It Matters:** Holistic spatial analysis in environmental and social sciences requires integrating diverse, interacting indicators rather than relying on univariate signals.
