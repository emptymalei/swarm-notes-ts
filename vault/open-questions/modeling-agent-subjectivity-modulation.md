title: Modeling Agent Subjectivity Modulation
---

**Background:** Trajectory prediction models often struggle to explicitly learn and represent the subjective intentions and anisotropic social interactions of agents.

**Question / Future Work:** A critical bottleneck exists in explicitly modeling and incorporating the subjective nature of individual agents (subjectivities) into trajectory forecasting, specifically regarding how such latent subjectivities can be used as control signals to dynamically modulate prediction models.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing this is fundamental to improving the realism and interpretability of multi-agent trajectory prediction, particularly in complex social scenarios where individual behavior deviates from generic crowd dynamics.

**Evidence:** it remains difficult to explicitly learn and forecast these subjectivities, let alone further modulate models’ predictions through a specific ego’s subjectivity.
