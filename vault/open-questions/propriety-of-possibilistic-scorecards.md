title: Propriety of Possibilistic Scorecards
---

**Background:** Proper scoring rules encourage honest probability forecasting by penalizing hedging and ensuring that the expected score is maximized by predicting the true distribution. Verification of possibilistic forecasts, which relax the additivity constraint of probabilities, currently lacks such formal guarantees for its diagnostic scorecard metrics.

**Question / Future Work:** Establishing formal propriety for possibilistic scorecards—ensuring that the proposed metrics incentivize truthful reporting rather than tactical hedging—remains an unresolved problem. Further work is required to determine if these descriptive diagnostic metrics can be grounded in or augmented by a formal proper scoring rule.

**Why It Matters:** Propriety is a fundamental requirement in forecast verification to ensure metrics incentivize truthful reporting rather than manipulation. Extending this to the possibilistic domain is essential for it to become a robust optimization standard.

**Evidence:** For the native scorecard, propriety remains an open question: the five metrics are descriptive diagnostics, not a single loss function to buttress optimisation.
