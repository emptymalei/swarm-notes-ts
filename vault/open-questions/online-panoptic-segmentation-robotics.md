title: Online Panoptic Segmentation for Robotics
---

**Background:** Panoptic segmentation aims to provide both semantic and instance-level scene understanding simultaneously by classifying pixels or points and grouping them into distinct entities. While offline methods can process aggregated data, developing online, computationally efficient versions that integrate directly into real-time SLAM and mapping frameworks is an active research goal.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of single-scan panoptic segmentation capable of real-time performance on mobile robotic platforms is a significant unresolved task, specifically regarding the integration of this segmentation capability into onboard mapping frameworks to generate persistent, semantic, and instance-aware maps during execution.

**Why It Matters:** Onboard semantic and instance-aware mapping is necessary for robots to perform intelligent, site-specific decision-making in real-time, such as identifying specific tree species or health conditions during navigation.
