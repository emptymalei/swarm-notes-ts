title: Robustness to Demand Shocks
---

**Background:** Supply chain forecasting and optimization are increasingly integrated into unified architectures that co-train predictive and prescriptive modules using shared loss functions. Future systems require improved robustness against unpredictable, extreme demand anomalies and enhanced scalability to industrial-scale multi-tier networks.

**Question / Future Work:** Current joint forecasting-optimization models exhibit performance degradation during sudden, consecutive demand shocks that remain underrepresented in training data. Further research is required to integrate adaptive constraints or reinforcement learning feedback mechanisms capable of managing extreme events and multi-echelon supply chain complexities.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing rare-event robustness and scalability to complex, multi-echelon networks is essential for moving these hybrid frameworks from academic benchmarks to reliable, industrial-scale deployment.
