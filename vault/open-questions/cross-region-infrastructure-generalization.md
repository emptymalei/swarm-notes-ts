title: Cross-region infrastructure generalization
---

**Background:** Predictive models for power outages are typically trained on individual service territories, which prevents them from leveraging shared information across geographically distinct regions.

**Question / Future Work:** Current infrastructure models often treat data from different service territories independently, limiting their portability and robustness. Developing domain generalization techniques that allow models to learn from heterogeneous regional data is essential for scaling predictive infrastructure systems.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental challenge for scaling predictive infrastructure models across diverse geographic regions with varying characteristics.
