title: Reducing Actuation Noise in Shape-Changing Interfaces
---

**Background:** Physical shape-changing interfaces often suffer from mechanical noise and vibration during actuation, which can degrade the overall user experience in quiet or professional environments.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop quieter, smoother, and more robust actuation mechanisms for shape-changing interfaces to minimize the disruption caused by mechanical noise, which users identified as a negative factor in their interaction experience. Future work should investigate alternative actuation technologies or mechanical designs that reduce audible noise and vibration without compromising the responsiveness or aesthetic appeal of the interface.

**Why It Matters:** Noise is a significant barrier to the adoption of shape-changing interfaces in shared professional environments (like offices), and addressing this is crucial for the transition of these interfaces from lab prototypes to real-world deployment.
