title: Solar cycle predictability bottlenecks
---

**Background:** The solar cycle is primarily predicted using the polar magnetic field as a precursor, but these predictions frequently lack consistency and accuracy, particularly when performed before the solar minimum. Accurate early prediction is complicated by the stochastic nature of active region properties and potential nonlinearities in the dynamo mechanism.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to improve solar cycle forecasting by refining data assimilation for dynamo models, specifically incorporating meridional flow variations and robust polar magnetic field data. Additionally, it remains unresolved how to determine the physical limit for prediction timing and how stochastic solar features influence predictive skill.

**Why It Matters:** Reliable solar cycle prediction is vital for mitigating space weather impacts on modern technology, and the current inability to achieve consistent predictions highlights a major bottleneck in understanding solar dynamo mechanisms.

**Evidence:** a significant deviation of the means of all predictions for both cycles 24 and 25 with respect to reality implies that we need to improve our predictive skills.
