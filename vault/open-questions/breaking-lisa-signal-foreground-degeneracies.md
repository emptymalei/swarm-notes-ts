title: Breaking LISA Signal-Foreground Degeneracies
---

**Background:** The sensitivity of space-based gravitational-wave detectors like LISA to anisotropic signals is coupled with complex instrument noise and galactic foregrounds. Separating these components remains a foundational challenge for high-precision cosmology.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigation is required into how kinematic dipole anisotropies can be utilized to formally break degeneracies between cosmological gravitational-wave backgrounds, foreground signals, and instrumental noise in high-contamination data environments.

**Why It Matters:** Degeneracy resolution is the primary utility of studying the kinematic dipole, and understanding its effectiveness under realistic, high-noise scenarios is essential for future missions.

**Evidence:** We also explore the potential of the kinematic dipole to break degeneracies, particularly in the presence of strong galactic foregrounds or noise features that closely mimic the primordial signal.
