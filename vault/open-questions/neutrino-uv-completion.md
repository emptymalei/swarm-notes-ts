title: UV Completion of Neutrino Interactions
---

**Background:** The effective four-fermion parameterization of neutrino self-interactions is a standard approximation used in cosmological simulations that may not capture the full complexity of realistic ultraviolet completions.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigating ultraviolet-complete models of neutrino self-interactions, particularly those with non-trivial flavor structures, is essential for reconciling cosmological constraints with laboratory observations. Current flavor-universal effective models may not be fully consistent with established constraints from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN), supernova cooling, and CMB data.

**Why It Matters:** Identifying viable UV-complete models is crucial for ensuring the physical validity of cosmological forecasts and reconciling them with broader particle physics constraints.

**Evidence:** flavor-universal interactions of this form face tight constraints from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN), supernova cooling, and CMB polarization data [43], implying that viable ultraviolet completions likely require non-trivial flavor structures.
