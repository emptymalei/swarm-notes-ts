title: Multi-relational sheaf diffusion extension
---

**Background:** Cellular sheaf theory provides a framework for defining learnable restriction maps on graphs to govern information flow and represent higher-order, asymmetric dependencies. Extending this framework to more complex topological structures beyond simple graphs is a recognized research objective.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate the extension of sheaf-theoretic diffusion models to multi-relational graphs or hypergraphs where nodes and edges exhibit diverse types and concurrent interaction modalities that simple restriction maps may not fully capture.

**Why It Matters:** Moving beyond single-relation graphs is necessary to apply these models to complex, heterogeneous network structures found in real-world systems.
