title: Visual Communication of Risk
---

**Background:** Public safety communication often relies on balancing the objective communication of complex environmental risks with the need for clear decision-support for populations with varying domain and visual literacies.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is required to determine how visual interventions, such as hazard icons, influence decision-making among diverse audiences without falling into reductive abstractions or exclusionary design conventions. This includes formalizing the trade-offs between rhetorical goals (e.g., behavior modification) and the descriptive necessity of maintaining high-fidelity data communication in safety-critical settings.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the intersection of cognitive load, visual communication, and safety-critical decision-making is essential to prevent unintended consequences in real-world risk management.

**Evidence:** we highlight remaining questions around visualization design and design ethics that warrant further study in the domain of how best to communicate cold weather hazards in ways that are mindful of the diversity of literacies and experiences of visitors.
