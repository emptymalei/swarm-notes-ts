title: Real-time Wrench Forecasting Deployment
---

**Background:** Forecasting models for robotic interactions are traditionally evaluated offline, yet their performance and stability in high-frequency closed-loop control settings remain largely uncharacterized.

**Question / Future Work:** Assessing the performance and reliability of short-term wrench forecasting models during real-time deployment, specifically focusing on delay-aware estimation and predictive control stability.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing the gap between offline accuracy and real-time control utility is essential for the industrial adoption of predictive sensing.

**Evidence:** investigating the use of forecasting models in real-world deployment would further clarify their practical value in delay-aware wrench estimation and predictive control settings.
