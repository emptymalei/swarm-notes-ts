title: Multivariate Extreme Value Modeling
---

**Background:** The current framework focuses on univariate extreme value modeling, yet many risk management scenarios involve multiple interacting series or spatially distributed observations. Expanding this methodology to a multivariate context remains a significant challenge due to the complexities of modeling cross-series dependencies and tail correlations.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop a multivariate extension of the marked Hawkes point process model for nonstationary extreme values, accounting for dependencies in occurrence times and mark distributions across different series or geographical regions.

**Why It Matters:** Multivariate modeling is essential for realistic risk assessment in complex interconnected systems like global financial markets or disaster networks.

**Evidence:** A multivariate extension would also be valuable in applications such as financial risk or natural hazards, where extremes in different series or regions may interact.
