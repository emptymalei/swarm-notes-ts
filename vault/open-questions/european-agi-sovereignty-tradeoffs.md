title: European AGI Sovereignty Trade-offs
---

**Background:** The emergence of AGI—defined as AI systems matching or exceeding human performance across most economically useful cognitive tasks—could potentially occur within the next two decades, with substantial uncertainty regarding both its timing and geopolitical consequences. Europe currently faces significant structural disadvantages in compute infrastructure, talent retention, and strategic policy coordination, risking a forced dependence on foreign AI systems.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to systematically evaluate how Europe can capture the economic benefits of AGI while maintaining its sovereign control, specifically regarding the optimal division between domestic development and strategic procurement of AI capabilities. This involves clarifying which application areas (e.g., defense, critical infrastructure, general commercial use) require sovereign control, the necessary infrastructure buildout strategies, and how to mitigate risks of external coercion or dependence on foreign AI stacks.

**Why It Matters:** This question is central to the tension between relying on global efficiency and maintaining strategic autonomy, which is a fundamental bottleneck for European policy in the current AI landscape.
