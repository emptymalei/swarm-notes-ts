title: Scaling State-to-Flow Air Traffic Modeling
---

**Background:** Air traffic flow prediction is commonly approached as a time series forecasting task based on historical macroscopic aggregations, which may overlook microscopic aircraft kinematics and variable-cardinality dynamics. Integrating these microscopic states directly into predictive models introduces challenges in handling unordered, variable-length sets while maintaining permutation invariance.

**Question / Future Work:** Extending state-to-flow models from local terminal airspace to multi-node network topologies while incorporating exogenous latent variables like dynamic weather constraints remains a significant challenge for air traffic management.

**Why It Matters:** This identifies the primary bottleneck in moving from specialized local airspace controllers to global, multi-scale traffic management systems.
