title: Phase reduction for multi-frequency turbulence
---

**Background:** Phase-reduction analysis is a established mathematical framework for simplifying the dynamics of periodic systems by projecting them onto a phase variable, but it faces challenges when applied to complex, non-periodic, or multi-frequency systems.

**Question / Future Work:** There remains a need to establish a comprehensive and robust phase-reduction framework specifically tailored for turbulent flows characterized by multiple dominant, interacting frequencies. Developing generalized methods to define and extract phase and amplitude variables in these high-dimensional, chaotic environments is critical for future advancements in perturbation dynamics and flow control.

**Why It Matters:** This is a central bottleneck for using reduced-order oscillator models in complex turbulent engineering applications. Addressing it is necessary to move beyond current limitations that restrict phase-based control and analysis to simplified, quasi-periodic flows.
