title: Robust HAP Downlink Beamforming
---

**Background:** High-altitude platform (HAP) downlink communications often rely on narrow beams that are highly susceptible to performance degradation from platform attitude fluctuations. While proactive beam steering can mitigate this, forecast errors inherently introduce pointing uncertainty that complicates the robust design of beamforming under quality-of-service (QoS) constraints.

**Question / Future Work:** The joint design of multivariate HAP attitude forecasting and robust beamforming remains largely unexplored, particularly the translation of forecast-error-induced pointing uncertainty into multiuser QoS-aware beamforming decisions. Future research is needed to develop frameworks that explicitly propagate predictive attitude uncertainty into online resource allocation and beamforming optimization without incurring prohibitive computational overhead.

**Why It Matters:** Reliable non-terrestrial network performance depends on maintaining precise beam alignment despite turbulence; addressing the coupling between predictive models and robust optimization is critical for practical 6G deployments.
