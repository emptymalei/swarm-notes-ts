title: Hybrid Feature Integration Strategies
---

**Background:** Hybrid time series classifiers often rely on simple concatenation of heterogeneous features, which may not capture optimal interactions between complementary representations.

**Question / Future Work:** The challenge lies in designing sophisticated, non-additive mechanisms to integrate distinct feature extractors (e.g., dictionary-based and interval-based) to maximize predictive performance while minimizing computational overhead.

**Why It Matters:** Advances in this area could significantly improve the efficiency and performance of hybrid forecasting and classification architectures.

**Evidence:** our Hydrant quality improvement hypothesis (H1) is only partially confirmed and likely requires more refined approaches for effectively combining both approaches.
