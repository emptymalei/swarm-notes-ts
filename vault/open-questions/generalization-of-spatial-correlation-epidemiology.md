title: Generalizing spatial correlation analysis
---

**Background:** The analysis of epidemiological time series requires handling non-stationary signals influenced by both biological dynamics and varying societal interventions. Current methods in post-pandemic analysis often lack established frameworks for quantitatively characterizing how these socio-epidemiological factors manifest in spatial correlation patterns.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to formalize a generalized methodology for mapping the temporal evolution of spatial correlations across diverse geopolitical regions. Future work should investigate whether the observed recurrent regimes of correlation in pandemic data are universal features of infectious disease spread or specific to the sociopolitical context of a region, potentially enabling a predictive framework for identifying shifts in pandemic phases.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing a transferable framework allows for comparative epidemiological studies across different countries and diseases, which is currently limited by the reliance on compartmental models that prioritize biological parameters over collective regional behaviors.

**Evidence:** Moreover, our methodology could be in principle applied to the analysis of other epidemiological datasets, offering a powerful tool for post-pandemic analysis and preparedness planning.
