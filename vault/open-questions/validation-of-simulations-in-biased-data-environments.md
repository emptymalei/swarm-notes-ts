title: Validating Simulations in Biased Data Environments
---

**Background:** Societal-scale simulations of human populations frequently rely on historical data that encodes existing discriminatory patterns, complicating the validation of models and potentially reinforcing systemic inequities.

**Question / Future Work:** There is no widely accepted or standard ground truth against which to validate simulations of marginalized populations, as historical datasets are often products of the very discriminatory policies these simulations might be used to examine. Research is needed to determine how to effectively conduct validation for such simulations without relying exclusively on potentially biased historical data, particularly when evaluating their impact on marginalized groups.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental methodological and ethical bottleneck; without a viable way to validate simulations of marginalized populations, their use in policy risks codifying inequality as 'neutral' technical findings.

**Evidence:** At the same time, there is no independent ground truth against which to validate a simulation of marginalized population behavior, because the historical data that would serve as the “ground truth” is itself a product of discriminatory policy (Mayson, 2019). This feedback loop makes this issue unresolvable through data collection alone.
