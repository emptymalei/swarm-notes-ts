title: Preserving non-negativity in densities
---

**Background:** The generalized logit transformation of probability density mixtures into a linear Euclidean space can lead to inverse-transformed functions that violate non-negativity constraints.

**Question / Future Work:** The potential for inverse-transformed density functions to take negative values challenges the geometric and statistical interpretability of the model. Determining whether alternative mappings or constraints can preserve non-negativity without sacrificing computational or inferential tractability remains an open problem.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental theoretical constraint on the proposed transformation-based approach that directly impacts its validity for density estimation.
