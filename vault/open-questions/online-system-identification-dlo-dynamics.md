title: Online Material Property Adaptation
---

**Background:** Robotic manipulation of deformable linear objects (DLOs) requires models that generalize across diverse material properties, such as stiffness, mass, and surface friction. Current data-driven dynamics models are typically trained on fixed environments, limiting their adaptability to new physical scenarios.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved challenge in developing online system identification methods that allow latent dynamics models to adapt to varying material properties of DLOs in real-time without full retraining. Bridging this gap is crucial for deploying DLO manipulation systems in novel or dynamic real-world environments.

**Why It Matters:** The lack of adaptation to changing physical properties is a primary bottleneck in moving from simulated benchmarks to robust, real-world robotic interaction.
