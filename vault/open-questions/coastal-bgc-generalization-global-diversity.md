title: Global Generalization of BGC Retrieval
---

**Background:** Coastal biogeochemical parameter retrieval using remote sensing is frequently limited by regional variations in bio-optical properties, which necessitate the development of robust, generalizable algorithms.

**Question / Future Work:** The current evaluation of physics-aware meta-learning frameworks for BGC retrieval is often restricted to limited regional datasets. Further validation in diverse international coastal and inland optical water types is necessary to confirm the model's generalizability and ability to handle broader ranges of bio-optical variability.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing the global transferability of BGC retrieval models is essential for developing standardized, cost-effective water quality monitoring tools that can operate effectively across disparate geographic regions.
