title: Phase-reduction for turbulent flows
---

**Background:** Phase-reduction analysis is traditionally limited to periodic systems with a limit cycle, making its application to chaotic, multi-frequency turbulent flows inherently difficult. Conventional frameworks often struggle to characterize phase variables in non-periodic, high-dimensional dynamical regimes.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to establish a formal and generalized phase-reduction framework for turbulent systems that contain multiple dominant frequencies. Defining robust phase variables that remain interpretable and predictive in these chaotic regimes remains an open challenge.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental bottleneck in applying non-linear dynamical systems theory to complex fluid flows and other multi-scale chaotic physical systems.

**Evidence:** Furthermore, characterizing the phase variable of multi-frequency turbulent flows is even challenging within existing phase-reduction methods.
