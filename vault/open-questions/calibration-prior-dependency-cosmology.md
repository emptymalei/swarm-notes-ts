title: Prior dependency in supernova cosmology
---

**Background:** The Bayesian fitting framework used to determine cosmological parameters incorporates instrumental calibration parameters and supernova model parameters as nuisance parameters, where the final inferred cosmology can be sensitive to the choice of priors for these parameters. It remains unclear if there exists a 'point of stability' where the posterior cosmology becomes independent of the chosen calibration priors.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate the extent to which cosmological parameter inference depends on the prior specifications for instrumental calibration and model nuisance parameters in hierarchical Bayesian frameworks. It is critical to determine whether results are robust to prior selection or whether they remain inherently prior-dependent despite self-calibration efforts.

**Why It Matters:** This represents a fundamental bottleneck in achieving high-precision (e.g., 5-sigma) cosmology with Type Ia supernovae, challenging the robustness of hierarchical pipelines.

**Evidence:** A potential “point of stability” towards which the posterior uncertainty choice would converge ... does not seem to exist for any reasonable priors above 1 mmag.
