title: Scalable Multi-Person CSI Sensing
---

**Background:** Wireless sensing systems based on Channel State Information (CSI) often struggle to maintain performance as the number of individuals in a monitored space increases, due to signal interference and overlapping motion patterns.

**Question / Future Work:** The performance of current language-driven wireless sensing frameworks degrades significantly with increasing interaction density, indicating a need for more robust signal decomposition and semantic grounding techniques that can accurately isolate and interpret individual activities within complex, multi-person environments.

**Why It Matters:** Scalability in multi-person environments is a critical bottleneck for deploying real-world wireless sensing systems, particularly those attempting to leverage LLMs for fine-grained semantic understanding.

**Evidence:** First, performance degrades as scene complexity increases in multi-person scenarios. Second, interaction reasoning remains challenging even in simpler settings, indicating that fine-grained spatial relationships and interaction cues are hard to recover from CSI signals.
