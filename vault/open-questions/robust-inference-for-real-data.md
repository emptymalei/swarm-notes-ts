title: Robust Inference for Real Data
---

**Background:** Neural ratio estimators (NREs) are simulation-based inference methods that rely on the assumption of a perfectly known data-generating process to produce valid posterior constraints. Real-world applications often face systematic discrepancies between simulation models and observational data, which can introduce significant biases in inferred physical parameters.

**Question / Future Work:** Bridging the gap between simulation-based forecasts and real-world scientific discovery requires developing inference methodologies that explicitly incorporate observational systematics, detector noise, and modeling uncertainties. This remains a primary bottleneck for applying NREs to large-scale astronomical surveys.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental challenge for the field of simulation-based inference in astrophysics, particularly when moving from idealized forecasts to real-world data analysis.

**Evidence:** While this analysis assumes perfect knowledge of the data-generating process and cannot be directly applied to data analysis, it quantifies constraints achievable with LSST alone and motivates the development of robust inference methods for real survey data.
