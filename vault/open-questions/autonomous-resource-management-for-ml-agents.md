title: Autonomous Resource Management for ML Agents
---

**Background:** AI model development often requires significant computational resources including distributed training, multi-GPU configurations, and extended time budgets. Current autonomous systems for model development are typically evaluated under strictly limited resource constraints.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to enable autonomous agents to reason about, optimize, and manage large-scale computational infrastructure, such as distributed training configurations and dynamic compute scheduling, for production-grade model development.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the gap between sandbox evaluation and large-scale industrial training is essential for the practical utility of autonomous AI development systems.

**Evidence:** real-world AI development tasks... often require substantially more computational resources... adapting modeling strategies based on available compute, automatically selecting distributed training configurations... would improve its applicability to large-scale, production-grade AI development.
