title: Optimal Feature Selection for PEPF
---

**Background:** Effective probabilistic electricity price forecasting (PEPF) requires balancing predictive accuracy with computational efficiency, particularly when comparing task-specific models and foundation models. The sensitivity of these models to varying feature sets and data regimes remains an active area of investigation.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved need to determine which specific combinations of exogenous feature groups (e.g., generation mix, load, cross-border flows) optimize forecast quality across different market conditions, as empirical results demonstrate that performance improvement from additional covariates is highly model-dependent. A comprehensive evaluation across all possible feature combinations and diverse market scenarios is required to establish generalizable guidelines for feature selection in PEPF.

**Why It Matters:** Developing systematic guidelines for feature selection is critical for balancing the computational costs of including high-dimensional exogenous data against the marginal gains in forecast accuracy for large-scale energy systems.
