title: Federated Collaborative Continuum Learning
---

**Background:** Future computing architectures are increasingly relying on distributed, multi-agent frameworks for workload placement across the Edge-Cloud continuum to balance latency, energy efficiency, and resource utilization. Many current approaches in this domain are limited by a lack of coordination mechanisms that can maintain system scalability while simultaneously ensuring privacy and global optimization in decentralized settings.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an explicit need for future research into federated or collaborative learning mechanisms that allow distributed agents within the computing continuum to share knowledge and improve their collective performance without compromising system scalability or the privacy of individual edge node data.

**Why It Matters:** This is a critical bottleneck for deploying large-scale decentralized systems where individual agents need global intelligence but face constraints regarding communication overhead, data privacy, and the complexity of non-stationary environments.
