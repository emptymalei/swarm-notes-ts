title: Simulation-to-Experiment Style Transfer
---

**Background:** Hybrid datasets combining simulation and experimental data are increasingly used for training deep learning models, but domain shift between simulated physics and real-world sensor noise remains a persistent barrier.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigating robust simulation-to-experiment style transfer or domain adaptation techniques is required to transform idealized simulated process data into realistic, noise-consistent representations for industrial anomaly detection.

**Why It Matters:** This is essential for the practical deployment of simulation-trained anomaly detection systems in real-world industrial environments where perfectly labeled, noise-free data is often unavailable.

**Evidence:** Fully exploiting this potential, however, requires bridging the gap between simulated data and experimental data that contain noise, sensor errors, failures, and various inconsistencies.
