title: Scalability of Constrained Adversarial Attacks
---

**Background:** Time-series forecasting models in streaming environments often operate under bounded-buffer constraints, limiting the availability of full history for both inference and potential adversarial analysis. Developing adversarial strategies that respect these operational constraints is critical for evaluating the robustness of real-world cyber-physical systems.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work must investigate the scalability of selective adversarial attacks under increasingly strict memory budgets and faster sampling rates. Furthermore, the cross-domain generalizability of uncertainty-aware attack strategies remains an open question, particularly regarding their transferability from stationary systems to environments with dynamic, non-stationary regimes.

**Why It Matters:** It addresses the gap between theoretical adversarial robustness and the practical, compute-limited constraints of real-time industrial forecasting deployments.
