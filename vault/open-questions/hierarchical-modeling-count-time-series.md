title: Hierarchical Count Time Series
---

**Background:** Count time-series models often rely on aggregate data, which can obscure finer-grained dynamics such as within-week variability or center-level heterogeneity.

**Question / Future Work:** Extending count time-series modeling to hierarchical frameworks that explicitly incorporate center-level heterogeneity and within-site variability remains a significant challenge for granular operational forecasting.

**Why It Matters:** Hierarchical models are essential for capturing latent sources of variance in medical and operational domains that aggregate models routinely overlook.

**Evidence:** The analysis is based on aggregated weekly counts, which may mask within-week variability and centre-level heterogeneity.
