title: Augmentation for Irregular Data
---

**Background:** Clinical data often lacks consistent observation intervals, whereas many forecasting architectures assume regular sampling or predefined imputation grids. Integrating augmentation methods with natively asynchronous data remains a persistent hurdle in clinical AI.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing augmentation strategies that are invariant or natively robust to irregularly sampled clinical temporal data without requiring pre-interpolation, which often loses structure, is a significant research challenge.

**Why It Matters:** Standardizing the robustness of clinical models requires moving beyond regularized grid assumptions toward handling the inherent sparsity and irregular cadence of ICU monitoring.
