title: Endogenizing demographic and productivity dynamics
---

**Background:** The design of social security systems involves balancing fiscal sustainability with intergenerational welfare, particularly under demographic transitions where fertility rates decline and the elderly population increases. Current theoretical approaches rely on exogenous demographic and productivity projections to determine contribution and replacement rates.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a lack of endogeneity in how social security design affects demographic and productivity dynamics. Future work is required to incorporate feedback loops where the social security system itself influences the fertility rates, labor supply, and productivity growth, rather than treating these variables as purely exogenous.

**Why It Matters:** Treating demographic and economic variables as exogenous limits the applicability of the model to real-world policy reform, as the introduction of significant pension changes may alter household behavior, fertility choices, and human capital investment.
