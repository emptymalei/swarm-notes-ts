title: Robustness for Non-LG Emissions
---

**Background:** The infinite hidden Markov model (iHMM) typically relies on conjugate linear-Gaussian (LG) emissions to allow for closed-form recursive Bayesian updates. Extending robustness guarantees to non-conjugate or non-linear observation models involves overcoming the difficulty of deriving bounded posterior influence functions without the mathematical convenience of the LG structure.

**Question / Future Work:** Generalizing robustness guarantees, specifically bounded posterior influence functions (PIFs), to non-linear or non-Gaussian emission models remains an unresolved challenge. Developing theoretical frameworks for models without such closed-form updates is necessary for broader applicability.

**Why It Matters:** Crucial for the practical utility of robust Bayesian modeling in non-linear or non-Gaussian systems like GARCH or chaotic dynamics.
