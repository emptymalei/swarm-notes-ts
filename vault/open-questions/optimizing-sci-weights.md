title: Optimizing SCI component weights
---

**Background:** The Signal Credibility Index (SCI) relies on integrating persistence, consensus, and breadth components, but its current formulation weights these parameters equally. Determining the optimal weighting for these factors remains an open empirical challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to determine whether the components of the Signal Credibility Index (SCI) should be differentially weighted based on domain-specific contexts, such as financial versus political markets. Since the relevant behavioral response times differ across domains, an empirical investigation using panel data across multiple shocks is necessary to calibrate these weights optimally.

**Why It Matters:** Differential weighting is essential for the SCI to be robustly applicable across different socio-economic domains with varying feedback loop speeds.
