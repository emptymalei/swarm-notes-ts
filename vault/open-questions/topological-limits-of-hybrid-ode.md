title: Topological limits of hybrid ODEs
---

**Background:** Neural Ordinary Differential Equations rely on Lipschitz continuity constraints, which fundamentally restrict their ability to represent discontinuous trajectories in dynamic physical systems.

**Question / Future Work:** While the proposed hybrid continuous-discrete framework introduces a mechanism to break this constraint via discrete compensation, it remains an open question how to rigorously characterize the topological boundaries of such hybrid dynamical systems. Future research is needed to determine the limits of piecewise-continuous approaches when modeling extremely non-linear spatiotemporal fluctuations.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the fundamental expressivity limits of piecewise-continuous Neural ODE architectures is critical for defining the scope of their applicability in complex physical system modeling.
