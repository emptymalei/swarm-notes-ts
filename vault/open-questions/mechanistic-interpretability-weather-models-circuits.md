title: Mechanistic circuits in weather models
---

**Background:** Mechanistic interpretability aims to reverse-engineer neural networks by identifying features and the circuits that connect them within a model's latent space. While this approach has been extensively studied in large language models and vision models, its application to complex AI weather forecasting models remains in its early stages.

**Question / Future Work:** Establishing a systematic dictionary of interpretable latent features and mapping how these features evolve across processor steps to form functional circuits that drive accurate weather predictions is a key requirement for scientific validity.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding these internal circuits is critical for building trust in operational weather forecasting and for potentially uncovering new physical insights modeled by AI.

**Evidence:** a comprehensive understanding of how predictions are made, and what connections are learned, is missing. ... lead to the identification of circuits, ultimately contributing to a more comprehensive understanding of how AI weather models generate their predictions.
