title: Self-supervised learning for WHAR
---

**Background:** The effectiveness of wearable human activity recognition models is frequently constrained by the requirement for large, manually labeled datasets for supervised training.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing self-supervised pre-training strategies that leverage large-scale unlabeled sensor data while maintaining compatibility with efficient fusion architectures like Cascaded Fusion Blocks remains an open challenge for scaling activity recognition to diverse real-world applications.

**Why It Matters:** Reducing reliance on labeled data is critical for scaling WHAR to diverse populations where annotated data is scarce.

**Evidence:** integrating self-supervised pre-training with the proposed MoM and CFB modules could reduce reliance on labeled data
