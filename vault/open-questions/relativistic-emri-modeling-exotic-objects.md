title: Relativistic EMRI Dynamics Modeling
---

**Background:** Extreme-mass-ratio inspirals (EMRIs) are binary systems where a stellar-mass compact object orbits a massive black hole, serving as high-precision probes of gravity in the strong-field regime. Modeling the orbital dynamics of exotic horizonless compact objects often relies on effective approximations due to the lack of exact, analytic metric solutions for these structures.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of self-consistent EMRI waveform models that account for relativistic orbital effects in the vicinity of non-Kerr exotic compact objects remains an open challenge. Future work is needed to derive metrics for perturbed stationary and axisymmetric spacetimes that can describe these horizonless geometries, particularly in high-spin regimes where current analytic approximations fail.

**Why It Matters:** Developing full relativistic geodesic models is essential for distinguishing quantum-gravitational horizon structure from general relativistic effects, which is a fundamental requirement for the high-fidelity testing of the fuzzball paradigm with instruments like LISA.
