title: Low-mass old-age evolutionary models
---

**Background:** Planetary atmospheric evolutionary models for low-mass substellar objects currently lack consistent coverage for masses below 2-4 Jupiter masses at system ages greater than 5 billion years.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of comprehensive planetary evolutionary tracks covering the low-mass and high-age regime is required to fully exploit the sensitivity of mid-infrared time-series imaging observations. Without these models, it is impossible to accurately translate detection limits into reliable mass constraints for the oldest M-dwarf planetary systems.

**Why It Matters:** This is a critical bottleneck for interpreting high-contrast imaging data of mature planetary systems and limits the ability to perform statistical surveys of the wide-orbit gas giant population around M-dwarfs.

**Evidence:** One limitation of our work is the lack of planetary evolutionary models with masses below 4 M_Jup at ages older than 5 Gyr.
