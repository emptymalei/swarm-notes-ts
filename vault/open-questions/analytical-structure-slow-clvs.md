title: Analytical Structure of Slow CLVs
---

**Background:** Fast-slow dynamical systems are characterized by multiple time scales where the separation of fast and slow manifolds is typically assumed, but analytical methods for characterizing slow Covariant Lyapunov Vectors (CLVs) in these systems remain underdeveloped.

**Question / Future Work:** Further analytical insight into the geometric and dynamical structure of slow CLVs requires additional assumptions regarding the functional form of the vector fields and the properties of their Jacobians beyond the current general characterizations.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the behavior of slow CLVs more generally is essential for broader application to complex multiscale systems where normal hyperbolicity assumptions may be violated.

**Evidence:** Beyond this general geometric characterisation, further analytical insight into the structure of the slow CLVs requires additional assumptions on the functional form of f and g and on the properties of their Jacobians.
