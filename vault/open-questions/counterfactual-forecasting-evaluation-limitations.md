title: Counterfactual Forecasting Evaluation Challenges
---

**Background:** Counterfactual time series forecasting aims to predict future sequences under hypothetical, stochastic conditions that have no direct ground-truth correspondence. The inherent lack of factual future sequences for these scenarios creates significant difficulties for evaluating forecasting quality.

**Question / Future Work:** Establishing rigorous, model-agnostic benchmarks for counterfactual time series forecasting is a major open problem. Future work must bridge the gap between semantic consistency metrics and actual accuracy when ground truth is systematically unavailable.

**Why It Matters:** Without evaluation protocols for counterfactual outcomes, it is impossible to validate model performance in hypothetical decision-support scenarios.

**Evidence:** The lack of ground-truth for counterfactual settings presents a fundamental obstacle to evaluate forecasting quality.
