title: Collaborative Marsupial Forestry Exploration
---

**Background:** Marsupial robotic systems consist of an aerial vehicle carried by a ground platform to combine their respective mobility and endurance advantages. Real-time coordination and map-sharing strategies for these heterogeneous teams remain a primary technical challenge for complex environment exploration.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to implement and validate co-planning and map-sharing methods for marsupial robotic systems in forested environments, focusing on the autonomous coordination of deployment from the ground platform based on traversability and the synchronization of shared mapping between heterogeneous agents.

**Why It Matters:** Marsupial systems are critical for overcoming the limited endurance of drones and the limited traversability of legged robots in dense forestry; establishing robust collaborative frameworks is essential for scaling these deployments.
