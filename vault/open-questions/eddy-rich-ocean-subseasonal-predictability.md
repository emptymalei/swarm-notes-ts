title: Eddy-rich ocean subseasonal predictability
---

**Background:** Subseasonal-to-seasonal (S2S) prediction systems exhibit sensitivity to the interplay between improved physical realism from ocean-atmosphere coupling and potential degradations arising from coupled systematic model biases. The extent to which these coupled biases limit the predictive benefit of increasing ocean model resolution to the eddy-resolving scale remains constrained by the current reliance on single-member experiments and deterministic metrics.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved need to rigorously evaluate whether the lack of substantial atmospheric response to eddy-rich ocean coupling in subseasonal forecasts is a physical limit of the system or an artifact of the current experimental design. Future research should leverage ensemble-based coupled reforecasts and probabilistic evaluation metrics (e.g., continuous ranked probability score) to overcome the 'double penalty' effect associated with deterministic metrics and to better isolate the signal of mesoscale ocean features from internal atmospheric noise at subseasonal lead times.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical for determining whether the high computational cost of eddy-rich ocean configurations is justified for subseasonal forecast systems, as the current results suggest only marginal atmospheric impacts.

**Evidence:** Nevertheless, it remains possible that modest improvements, not detectable in these single-member experiments, could emerge with ensemble reforecasts and/or a larger sample of start dates.
