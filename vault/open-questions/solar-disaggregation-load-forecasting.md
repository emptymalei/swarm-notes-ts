title: Solar Disaggregation in Load Forecasting
---

**Background:** In households equipped with solar photovoltaic systems, smart meters typically record net electricity consumption, which conflates household load demand with solar power generation. This aggregation makes it difficult for forecasting models to accurately predict the underlying electricity demand distinct from the highly weather-dependent solar output.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is required to develop dual-output forecasting architectures capable of independently predicting household grid consumption and solar generation. This disaggregation would resolve the confounding effect where weather-based models implicitly forecast solar generation while attempting to predict total household load.

**Why It Matters:** Accurate disaggregation is vital for the effective management of behind-the-meter resources, enabling more precise demand response and grid-level optimization.
