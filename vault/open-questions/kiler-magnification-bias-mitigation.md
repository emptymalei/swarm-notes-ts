title: Mitigating KiLeR Magnification Bias
---

**Background:** Kinematic Lensing Ratio (KiLeR) uses rotation-based intrinsic shape inference to provide a purely geometric probe of dark energy, potentially bypassing systematic bottlenecks in traditional weak lensing like photometric redshift errors and intrinsic alignments. While KiLeR effectively isolates geometric information, it remains susceptible to magnification bias, which reintroduces dependence on the lens mass distribution.

**Question / Future Work:** The impact of magnification bias, defined as the difference between shear and reduced shear, on KiLeR needs to be fully quantified and mitigated. While preliminary strategies like radial scale cuts or joint magnification modeling have been proposed, their performance, optimal implementation, and impact on the overall cosmological constraining power of KiLeR in realistic survey settings remain to be systematically explored and finalized.

**Why It Matters:** Magnification bias is a primary systematic that threatens the 'purely geometric' advantage of the KiLeR probe. Developing robust mitigation strategies is essential to ensuring that KiLeR meets its promised potential as a precision dark energy probe for Stage-IV surveys.
