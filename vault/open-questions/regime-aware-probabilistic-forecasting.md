title: Regime-aware and probabilistic forecasting
---

**Background:** Electricity price forecasting models often rely on autoregressive patterns that are susceptible to failure during sudden, non-linear market regime shifts. Identifying structural mechanisms that lead to these forecast failures remains an open challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research should focus on developing regime-aware or probabilistic models that explicitly characterize the conditions under which forecast errors increase, particularly to distinguish between predictable autoregressive patterns and exogenous-driven extreme events. Bridging this gap is essential for moving beyond average accuracy improvements in volatile market environments.

**Why It Matters:** Current models often perform well on average but fail systematically during extreme, identifiable market conditions, which significantly limits their utility for operational risk management.
