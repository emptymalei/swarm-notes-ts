title: Efficiency of Multi-Agent TSAD
---

**Background:** Time-series anomaly detection (TSAD) systems currently face practical limitations regarding computational overhead and scalability due to the reliance on multiple agentic LLM calls per time-series window.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to optimize the cost and latency of multi-agent LLM frameworks for TSAD, including exploring adaptive filtering to reduce agent invocations and investigating more efficient deployment strategies.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing these bottlenecks is essential for transitioning from research-grade prototypes to production-level anomaly detection systems where responsiveness and resource efficiency are critical.
