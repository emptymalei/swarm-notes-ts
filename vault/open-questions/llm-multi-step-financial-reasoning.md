title: Multi-step Reasoning in Financial Agents
---

**Background:** Large language models (LLMs) often struggle with complex, multi-step reasoning tasks in domains requiring precise numerical computation, such as financial time-series analysis. While tool-augmented frameworks allow agents to delegate these computations to specialized functions, handling sequences of interdependent queries remains an open challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Current agentic frameworks often utilize single-step workflows that struggle with complex queries requiring reasoning chains across multiple tools. Extending these systems to handle multi-step reasoning—for instance, chaining data retrieval with conditional analysis—is necessary to move beyond isolated computational tasks in financial applications.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing compositionality and multi-step reasoning is essential for scaling LLM agents from simple question-answering systems to autonomous financial analysts capable of handling sophisticated, multi-faceted inquiries.
