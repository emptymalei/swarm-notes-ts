title: Directly constraining subhalo mass function normalization
---

**Background:** The normalization of the subhalo mass function is a critical parameter in dark matter modeling that remains subject to significant theoretical uncertainty, with current predictions from N-body simulations and semi-analytic models differing by approximately a factor of two.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to directly constrain the normalization of the subhalo mass function using larger samples of lenses (e.g., ~200+ quadruply imaged quasars) to resolve existing discrepancies between theoretical models and simulation predictions, thereby reducing systematic uncertainty in dark matter property measurements.

**Why It Matters:** The normalization of the subhalo mass function is a critical nuisance parameter that currently limits the precision of inferences about the minimum halo mass and the nature of dark matter in strong lensing studies.
