title: Climate-dependent sovereign dependence identification
---

**Background:** Sovereign credit rating migrations are typically modeled as discrete-event count time series, and systemic credit risk is governed by the dependence structure between these sovereign entities. Understanding the influence of exogenous factors like climate change on this dependence remains a significant challenge due to data aggregation and model identification constraints.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is needed to determine whether aggregate sovereign rating migrations demonstrate climate-dependent tail dependence and to develop estimation techniques that can robustly identify these effects. Specifically, it remains an open question whether higher-frequency climate proxies or alternative model specifications can overcome the current limitations in distinguishing marginal climate impacts from systematic dependence shifts in systemic risk models.

**Why It Matters:** Identifying if and how climate risks influence the dependence structure of sovereign migrations is essential for accurate systemic stress testing and understanding the evolution of tail risk in global financial markets.

**Evidence:** Alternatively, the effective climate-enriched sample may be too short, and annual aggregation too coarse, to permit reliable identification of time-varying dependence effects.
