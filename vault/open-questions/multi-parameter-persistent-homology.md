title: Multi-parameter Persistent Homology
---

**Background:** Persistence landscapes used in time-series analysis are often generated via single-parameter persistent homology, which restricts the captured geometric information to specific resolution scales.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing multi-parameter persistent homology that filtrates along multiple dimensions (e.g., scale and volatility) is necessary to capture complex manifold geometries that current single-parameter summaries overlook.

**Why It Matters:** Multi-parameter persistence potentially offers a more expressive characterization of population manifolds critical for forecasting performance in volatile regimes.
