title: Utility of Non-Separable KLE
---

**Background:** The Karhunen-Loève expansion is traditionally constructed for stochastic elements taking values in separable Hilbert spaces, which limits its direct applicability to non-separable spaces often encountered in broader theoretical or computational contexts.

**Question / Future Work:** While the existence of the expansion for non-separable Hilbert spaces is established, the practical computational utility and convergence properties of the expansion in these generalized settings remain an area for further investigation. Identifying specific, non-separable Hilbert space applications where the generalization provides distinct advantages over standard separable approaches is necessary to justify the theoretical extension.

**Why It Matters:** Determining whether the generalized expansion offers tangible benefits for specific non-separable Hilbert space problems is essential to justify the theoretical extension beyond its current abstract formulation.

**Evidence:** We include this content on non-separable spaces anyways for the interested reader and for potential undiscovered applications.
