title: Rigorous geometric-CSAD bridge proof
---

**Background:** Formal verification of the link between discrete geometric metrics and macroscopic financial statistics remains a significant theoretical challenge. Existing mean-field derivations often rely on simplifying assumptions regarding the concentration of agent interactions and the nature of price impacts.

**Question / Future Work:** Establish a rigorous proof of the mean-field bridge between graph-theoretic curvature and CSAD, specifically addressing the remainder bounds in the optimal-transport mapping between near-degenerate kernels. This would provide the necessary theoretical grounding to transition from heuristic diagnostic to a proven financial metric.

**Why It Matters:** This is essential to move the proposed geometric framework from a useful heuristic to a rigorously grounded financial diagnostic.

**Evidence:** We state this as a scaling identity because Step 2 of the derivation invokes a closed-form W1 between two near-degenerate kernels whose remainder bound we do not establish here; a fully rigorous proof is left to follow-on work.
