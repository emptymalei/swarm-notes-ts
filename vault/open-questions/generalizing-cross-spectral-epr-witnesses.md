title: Generalizing Cross-Spectral EPR Witnesses
---

**Background:** Many reduced-order models of complex nonequilibrium systems suffer from information loss when latent degrees of freedom are coarse-grained, leading to the disappearance of time-irreversibility signatures in scalar observations.

**Question / Future Work:** Extending the cross-spectral detectability framework beyond one-way coupled Ornstein–Uhlenbeck systems to account for fully bidirectional, nonlinear, or non-Gaussian coupling requires a more general analytical bridge between spectral structure and entropy production.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical for validating the cross-spectral witness as a reliable indicator of system dissipation in realistic, complex physical systems where one-way coupling is an oversimplification.

**Evidence:** The result does not imply generic multivariate superiority... The qualitative cross-spectral witness survives bidirectional coupling... full analytic extensions... are natural next steps.
