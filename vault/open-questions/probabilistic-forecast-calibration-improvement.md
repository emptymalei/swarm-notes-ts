title: Probabilistic Forecast Calibration Improvement
---

**Background:** Probabilistic forecasting aims to estimate the distribution of future observations rather than providing a single point prediction, which is critical for quantifying uncertainty. Calibration of these probabilistic estimates is a key requirement for reliable decision-making in time series applications.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is needed to improve the calibration of probabilistic forecasts generated through tabular foundational models adapted for multivariate time series. This involves identifying the specific causes of performance deficits in current probabilistic forecasting frameworks and developing targeted mitigation strategies.

**Why It Matters:** Uncertainty quantification is vital for real-world deployment of time series models, and existing tabular PFN adaptations currently struggle to produce well-calibrated probabilistic outputs for multivariate data.
