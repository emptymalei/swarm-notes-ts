title: Robust uncertainty quantification development
---

**Background:** Predictive models for geophysical systems like sea ice must balance the inclusion of predictable, stationary modes with the handling of non-stationary, high-frequency transients. Current model-based approaches, including those using Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD), often suffer from under-dispersive uncertainty estimates that fail to capture the variability inherent in seasonal transitions.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing methods to generate robust, well-calibrated uncertainty quantification for DMD-based predictive frameworks is essential to move from research to operational climate forecasting. Future efforts must address the limitations of existing bagging-based ensembles to better characterize the risks associated with rapid seasonal transitions and extreme events.

**Why It Matters:** Reliable uncertainty quantification is essential for operational climate forecasting where risk management and decision-making are paramount.
