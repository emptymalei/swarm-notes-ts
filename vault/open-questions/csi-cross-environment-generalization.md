title: Environment-Agnostic Wireless Sensing
---

**Background:** Wireless sensing relies on diverse hardware configurations and deployment environments, which can significantly alter the received CSI signal characteristics.

**Question / Future Work:** Current models often rely on datasets collected under specific, controlled sensing configurations. Developing methods that are robust to variations in hardware layouts, room dimensions, and environmental conditions remains a significant hurdle for practical, wide-scale adoption of zero-shot wireless sensing.

**Why It Matters:** Generalization across hardware and environments is essential for moving wireless sensing from controlled laboratory experiments to universal, plug-and-play applications.

**Evidence:** In addition, our evaluation is based on a dataset collected under a specific sensing configuration and limited environments, so robustness across different hardware layouts, rooms, and deployment conditions requires further study.
