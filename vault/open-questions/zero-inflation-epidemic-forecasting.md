title: Addressing zero-inflation in forecasting
---

**Background:** Multivariate epidemic forecasting often relies on methods that assume dense data, which struggles with the high proportion of zeros often found in reported infectious disease incidence cases.

**Question / Future Work:** Existing forecasting models frequently experience numerical instability and difficulty in capturing outbreak intensity when dealing with zero-inflated epidemic time series, necessitating the development of robust, specialized architectures for these sparse regimes.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing zero-inflation is critical for moving beyond simple univariate models to reliable multivariate forecasting in public health.
