title: Mechanism of anti-solar rotation
---

**Background:** Anti-solar differential rotation, where the pole rotates faster than the equator, has been observed in several subgiant components of RS CVn-type binary systems. The underlying physical mechanism for this phenomenon remains poorly constrained by the limited number of observational detections.

**Question / Future Work:** Determine the specific physical mechanisms responsible for anti-solar differential rotation in active subgiant stars. Further investigations are needed to increase the sample size of detected anti-solar rotation in these stars to clarify the correlation between stellar parameters (such as Rossby numbers) and the observed rotational shear.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding this rotational pattern is critical for refining stellar dynamo theories and the influence of rotational shear on magnetic field generation in evolved, magnetically active stars.

**Evidence:** However, the samples are still insufficient so far, and more detections of anti-solar differential rotation are required so as to clarify its mechanism.
