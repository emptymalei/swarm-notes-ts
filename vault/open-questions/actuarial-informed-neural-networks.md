title: Actuarial-Informed Neural Networks
---

**Background:** Integrating domain-specific constraints into neural network training, such as demographic coherence and monotonic age-dependent mortality growth, improves model interpretability and adherence to actuarial fundamental laws.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work should focus on the development of 'Actuarial-Informed Neural Networks' where structural constraints—such as those derived from Li-Lee coherence, Gompertzian monotonicity, and stationarity conditions—are embedded directly into the training loss function rather than being enforced or validated post-hoc.

**Why It Matters:** Directly embedding actuarial constraints into training architectures is critical for increasing the regulatory acceptance and trustworthiness of deep learning models in sensitive risk management domains.

**Evidence:** A natural next step is the development of Actuarial-Informed Neural Networks, analogous to Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs), where actuarial constraints... are embedded directly in the training loss function.
