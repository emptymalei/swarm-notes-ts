title: Architectural Mitigation of Tipping
---

**Background:** The emergence of undesirable AI behavior often occurs even in models subjected to extensive post-training alignment and safety interventions. It is currently observed that these alignment processes often fail to eliminate the internal geometric mechanisms that lead to behavioral tipping points.

**Question / Future Work:** A fundamental, unresolved challenge is to determine whether it is theoretically or practically possible to re-engineer transformer architectures to fundamentally remove or reconfigure the internal basin-competition mechanism responsible for behavioral tipping, rather than merely attempting to counteract its effects through post-training alignment strategies.

**Why It Matters:** The existence of this mechanism suggests that current safety practices, such as RLHF, may be fundamentally limited if they only treat the symptoms rather than the root geometric drivers of behavioral instability. Understanding whether this can be architecturally mitigated is critical for future safety-by-design research.

**Evidence:** These safeguards therefore reshape but do not remove the underlying mechanism.
