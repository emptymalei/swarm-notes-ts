title: Limb Flare Synthesis Limitations
---

**Background:** Machine learning models for synthesizing Soft X-ray (SXR) solar flux from Extreme Ultraviolet (EUV) imagery frequently encounter reduced predictive accuracy for flares occurring near the solar limb.

**Question / Future Work:** The identification of whether limb-specific performance degradation stems from fundamental physical factors, such as optical depth in coronal loops, or from observational constraints like data cropping, remains a critical bottleneck for reliable whole-disk solar monitoring.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing this is necessary to ensure consistent space weather forecasting performance regardless of the solar flare's location relative to the observer's line of sight.
