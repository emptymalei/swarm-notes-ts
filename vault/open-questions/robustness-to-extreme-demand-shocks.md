title: Robustness to extreme demand shocks
---

**Background:** Integrating deep learning-based forecasting with downstream optimization in supply chains frequently relies on sequential, decoupled processes, which can hinder the ability of predictive models to align with operational constraints. Although differentiable, joint training of these modules has been proposed, adapting this architecture to highly volatile, multi-echelon environments remains a significant challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Addressing system failure during sudden, consecutive demand shocks where predictive under-estimation leads to stock shortages, specifically investigating synthetic rare-event training data augmentation and adaptive constraint mechanisms to improve decision robustness in edge cases.

**Why It Matters:** Failure cases during demand shocks are critical bottlenecks for the deployment of predictive-prescriptive systems in real-world supply chains where resilience against tail-end risks is essential.
