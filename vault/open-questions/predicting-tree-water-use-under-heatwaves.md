title: Predicting tree water-use during heatwaves
---

**Background:** Tree water-use models often struggle to maintain accuracy during extreme climatic events, as physiological shifts in water extraction behavior may violate the assumptions of models trained under typical environmental conditions.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop methods for predicting shifts in tree water-use behavior specifically associated with extreme weather events like heatwaves. Existing models struggle to capture these shifts, and there is a challenge in determining whether it is possible to reliably predict these regime changes given that the effect of extreme weather on trees is often gradual and modulated by complex species-specific hydraulic strategies.

**Why It Matters:** Improving the robustness of water-use forecasts during climate extremes is essential for sustainable irrigation management and ecosystem conservation under changing climate scenarios.
