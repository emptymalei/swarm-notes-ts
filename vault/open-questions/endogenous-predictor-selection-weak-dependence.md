title: Optimal predictor index selection
---

**Background:** The selection of an optimal predictor index in a high-dimensional marginal regression framework is typically performed endogenously, yet the theoretical implications of this selection under weak dependence remain an open challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop a rigorous asymptotic theory for the endogenously selected optimal covariate index in a marginal regression setting that accounts for the complexities introduced by weakly dependent and non-stationary data. This involves understanding the behavior of this selection process when the number of covariates diverges, which is essential for advancing post-model-selection inference in time series and panel data contexts.

**Why It Matters:** Adaptive post-model-selection inference is a central challenge in high-dimensional statistics, and extending existing methods to handle temporal or cross-sectional dependence is crucial for their application in fields like macroeconomics and finance.

**Evidence:** We do not explore an endogenously selected optimal covariate index l^n under weak dependence, leaving that for future work.
