title: Improving DER capacity estimation methods
---

**Background:** Estimating the installed capacity of Distributed Energy Resources (DERs) from aggregated measurements at substation or feeder levels is essential for distribution network visibility, yet existing methods face accuracy limitations.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to improve the performance and robustness of data-driven and model-based estimation techniques for inferring aggregated DER installed capacities (such as PV, EVs, and Heat Pumps) from historical measurement data. Future efforts should focus on developing more accurate estimation algorithms that can handle varying levels of data granularity and noise in distribution network measurements.

**Why It Matters:** Accurate estimation of DER metadata is the foundational step for all downstream operational and planning applications; insufficient estimation accuracy directly limits the effectiveness of forecasting, congestion management, and hosting capacity assessments.
