title: LLM Emergency Mobility Generalization
---

**Background:** Large language models are increasingly applied to simulate human-centric decision-making processes, such as household trip generation, which involve complex, multi-stage cognitive interactions and social dependencies.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains an open question whether LLM-based multi-agent frameworks, designed for routine urban mobility, can generalize to predict household decision-making under high-stakes, extreme conditions such as natural hazard evacuations or disaster response planning, where behavioral constraints and volatility increase significantly.

**Why It Matters:** This tests the robustness and scalability of LLM-based behavioral simulation beyond stable, routine datasets, which is critical for life-critical urban systems.
