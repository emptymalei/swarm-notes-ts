title: Robust Ranking Normalization Methods
---

**Background:** Ranking models based on normalized performance metrics across diverse datasets is sensitive to the specific normalization technique employed, such as rank-based, min-max, or z-scoring.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to systematically investigate how different performance normalization strategies (e.g., min-max scaling, z-scoring, or rank-based methods) affect leaderboard stability, particularly regarding the sensitivity to ties and the preservation of performance magnitude differences.

**Why It Matters:** Determining robust normalization methods is crucial for ensuring that leaderboard rankings accurately reflect model performance differences rather than artifacts of the ranking methodology.
