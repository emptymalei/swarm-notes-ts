title: Loss Functions for Calibrated PEPF
---

**Background:** Probabilistic forecasts are essential for quantifying risk in electricity markets, but the choice of loss functions and training objectives significantly influences the calibration and reliability of the resulting predictive distributions. Evaluating the effectiveness of various objectives beyond standard likelihood-based metrics is necessary for robust decision-making.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is needed to identify and design loss functions specifically tailored for producing well-calibrated probabilistic electricity price forecasts. Investigating how different objectives affect uncertainty quantification in renewable-dominated systems—where extreme price volatility is frequent—remains an open challenge for improving the reliability of downstream operational decisions.

**Why It Matters:** Proper calibration is essential for economic risk management in electricity markets, and current standardized loss functions may not fully capture the complexities of market-driven price volatility.
