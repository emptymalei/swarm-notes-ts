title: Controller Placement in SDN-LEO
---

**Background:** The deployment of central controllers in software-defined satellite networks involves balancing the conflicting requirements of global topological visibility, computational resource availability, and propagation latency.

**Question / Future Work:** Establishing an optimal controller placement strategy is critical, as ground-based controllers face intermittent connectivity, while space-based controllers are limited by Size, Weight, and Power (SWaP) constraints and high propagation latency. Future research is needed to develop sophisticated distributed control planes, mechanisms for dynamic controller delegation, and robust protocols for maintaining state consistency in split-control architectures.

**Why It Matters:** The controller placement determines the feasibility of real-time network orchestration and the overall resilience of the software-defined architecture in mega-constellations.
