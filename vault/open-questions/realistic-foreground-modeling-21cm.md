title: Foregrounds in 21cm surveys
---

**Background:** 21-cm intensity mapping is heavily obscured by foregrounds that are orders of magnitude stronger than the cosmological signal, requiring sophisticated mitigation that current forecasting often idealizes.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed to develop realistic, non-idealized foreground modeling and mitigation frameworks for 21-cm intensity mapping to validate the feasibility of using 21-cm auto-spectra for high-precision cosmological parameter estimation.

**Why It Matters:** This remains the primary systematic bottleneck for the utilization of 21-cm data in cosmology.

**Evidence:** As already highlighted, the presence of strong foregrounds in the 21 cm sector has been neglected in this study, which must be taken into account for a more realistic analysis of the 21 cm auto-spectrum and its capacity of providing constraints on the PMF sector.
