title: LSST Artifact Characterization Bottlenecks
---

**Background:** Large-scale photometric surveys, such as those conducted by the Vera C. Rubin Observatory, face significant challenges with instrument-specific artifacts, including sensor saturation, blending, and spectral-leakage. Efficient identification and removal of these artifacts are essential for ensuring the purity and completeness of astrophysical source catalogs.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing robust, automated pipelines to flag, deblend, or accurately measure saturated sources is a persistent bottleneck in survey science. Improved methods are needed to characterize these artifacts and maintain photometric accuracy for faint sources located near bright objects.

**Why It Matters:** Accurate handling of saturated source artifacts is vital for the scientific validity of deep, wide-field sky surveys.

**Evidence:** Similarly, there currently is no mechanism to perform astrometric or photometric measurements on the original saturated sources within the LSST pipelines.
