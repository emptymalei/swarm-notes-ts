title: Bridging station to grid valuation gap
---

**Background:** Large-scale participatory weather sensing networks increasingly require incentive mechanisms to allocate rewards based on data utility rather than mere availability, yet establishing a model-informed value signal remains an open problem.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved need for validation of attribution signals to verify the station-to-grid link, where raw local observations are assimilated into gridded fields. This gap complicates the translation of model-derived utility to physical ground stations operating under real-world data constraints.

**Why It Matters:** This is the core bottleneck to applying model-derived valuation in real-world infrastructure; without bridging this gap, the proxy may overstate or misinterpret the actual utility of sparse physical ground stations.
