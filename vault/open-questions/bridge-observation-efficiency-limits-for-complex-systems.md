title: Bridge observation efficiency limits for complex systems
---

**Background:** Radial velocity (RV) monitoring of stellar motion is a critical tool for detecting long-period giant exoplanets, yet observational gaps caused by instrument retirement and subsequent zeropoint offsets significantly degrade long-term sensitivity. Ongoing research seeks to determine how complex planetary architectures, such as non-zero orbital eccentricities and multi-planet systems, influence the efficacy of sparse bridge-observation strategies in overcoming these sensitivity losses.

**Question / Future Work:** Future investigations should extend existing bridge-observation recovery experiments to incorporate non-zero planetary eccentricities, multi-planet system configurations, and rigorous model-selection frameworks. Establishing how these factors modulate the reported signal enhancement is necessary for optimizing survey design and data integration when transitioning between historical and future instrumentation.

**Why It Matters:** This research is essential for generalizing the efficacy of sparse observational strategies from idealized single-planet scenarios to the more complex, realistic populations encountered in long-term astronomical surveys.

**Evidence:** Experiments that relax our single, circular-planet model might yield lower values of Q: Injecting planets with non-zero eccentricity, Injecting multiple planets, Running fits for numbers of planets other than the number of planets injected.
