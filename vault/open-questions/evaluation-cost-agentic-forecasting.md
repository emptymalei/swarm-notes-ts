title: Evaluation cost agentic forecasting
---

**Background:** Agentic time series forecasting frameworks increasingly leverage large-scale foundation models to integrate multimodal context with numerical sequences.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a significant bottleneck in establishing statistically rigorous and reproducible evaluation protocols for agentic time series forecasting due to the prohibitive computational costs of high-parameter LLM inference. Standard methodologies like repeated trials and uncertainty quantification are often infeasible in these settings, leaving performance metrics vulnerable to noise or single-run bias.

**Why It Matters:** This question highlights a critical systemic limitation that hinders the transition of agentic forecasting from prototype to production-grade reliable systems.
