title: Theoretical Framework for QRC Capacity
---

**Background:** Quantum reservoir computing (QRC) has demonstrated potential for processing temporal data, yet a comprehensive understanding of the relationship between quantum correlations and computational capacity remains elusive.

**Question / Future Work:** A formal, rigorous theoretical framework is required to explicitly link specific quantum mechanical properties, such as entanglement and squeezing, to the computational performance of QRC systems, moving beyond observed empirical correlations.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing a theoretical foundation would transition QRC from an empirical paradigm to one with predictive design principles, allowing for the optimization of quantum resources to maximize computational power.

**Evidence:** Nevertheless, a rigorous theoretical framework that links quantum correlations to computational capacity remains an open challenge, and we hope this work stimulates further theoretical investigations in this direction.
