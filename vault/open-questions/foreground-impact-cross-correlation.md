title: Foreground Impact on Cross-Correlation
---

**Background:** Post-reionization 21-cm intensity mapping is a promising cosmological probe, but it is highly susceptible to contamination from Galactic synchrotron emission, terrestrial radio frequency interference, and other extraterrestrial foregrounds that are several orders of magnitude stronger than the cosmological signal.

**Question / Future Work:** The impact of realistic, complex, and non-Gaussian foregrounds on the signal-to-noise ratio of the 21-cm–Ly$\\alpha$ cross-correlation requires rigorous investigation. While the statistical independence of these tracers helps mitigate individual systematic effects, the residual foreground leakage into the cross-spectrum remains a critical source of systematic uncertainty that could significantly degrade forecasted constraints.

**Why It Matters:** This is the primary bottleneck for the practical realization of 21-cm intensity mapping, and understanding the survival of the cross-correlation signal in the presence of these foregrounds is essential for reliable survey planning.
