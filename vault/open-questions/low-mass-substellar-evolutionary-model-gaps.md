title: Low-mass substellar model gaps
---

**Background:** Substellar evolutionary models are required to translate brightness limits into mass and temperature for directly imaged exoplanets, yet coverage for mature, low-mass systems remains sparse.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a significant gap in planetary evolutionary models for masses below 2-4 Jupiter masses in systems older than 5 Gyr. This limitation hinders the characterization of cold, old, and low-mass substellar companions observed in high-contrast imaging datasets.

**Why It Matters:** Without these models, detection probability maps are incomplete, leaving a critical portion of the exoplanetary population inaccessible to direct imaging analysis.

**Evidence:** One limitation of our work is the lack of planetary evolutionary models with masses below 4 M_Jup at ages older than 5 Gyr. Such models are essential for fully exploiting the sensitivity of time-series observations to old, cold substellar companions, and their development should be prioritized by the community.
