title: Region-Stratified Evaluation Tracks
---

**Background:** Monitoring infrastructure is characterized by global disparities, where regions with mature sensing systems are more densely represented and have lower missingness compared to under-instrumented regions.

**Question / Future Work:** Current evaluation protocols do not account for the inherent regional imbalances in data quality and density. Future research should develop region-stratified evaluation tracks to better assess model performance and fairness across geographically and socio-economically diverse monitoring environments.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing spatial bias is critical for ensuring that air-quality forecasting models perform reliably across the globe rather than only in data-rich urban centers.

**Evidence:** We therefore view spatial imbalance as an expected property of real monitoring infrastructure rather than a removable artifact of the dataset... and propose region-stratified evaluation as a future benchmark track.
