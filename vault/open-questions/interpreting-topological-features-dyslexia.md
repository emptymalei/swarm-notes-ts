title: Interpretable Topological Dyslexia Features
---

**Background:** Topological data analysis provides a framework for extracting stable, coordinate-invariant features from time series by applying filtrations to quantify geometric structures. While this approach has shown promise in identifying patterns in fixation sequences for dyslexia detection, the interpretation of these topological signatures in relation to cognitive reading models and clinical practice remains limited.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is required to bridge the gap between abstract topological signatures and cognitive or clinical theories of reading. Determining which persistent homology features map to specific reading impairments is necessary for interpretable clinical diagnostic tools.

**Why It Matters:** Translating abstract topological features into clinically or cognitively meaningful insights is essential for the adoption of TDA-based diagnostic tools.
