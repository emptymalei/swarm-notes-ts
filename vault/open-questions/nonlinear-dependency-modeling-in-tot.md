title: Nonlinear dependency modeling in TOT
---

**Background:** Longitudinal imaging data modeling often assumes linear relationships, which frequently fails to characterize the complex, nonlinear trajectories of neurodegenerative disease progression. While Gaussian process (GP) priors have been used to mitigate this, their integration into high-dimensional tensor-on-tensor (TOT) regression frameworks remains computationally and methodologically challenging.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop more scalable and robust Bayesian semi-parametric frameworks for TOT regression that can handle non-linear dependencies in high-dimensional imaging time series. Future research should address the tension between capturing voxel-level spatial heterogeneity and maintaining the computational efficiency required for longitudinal neuroimaging.

**Why It Matters:** This question addresses the critical bottleneck of scaling flexible, non-linear Bayesian modeling to high-dimensional tensors, which is central to advancing longitudinal image analysis.
