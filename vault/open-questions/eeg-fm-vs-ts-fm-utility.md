title: Necessity of Specialized EEG Architectures
---

**Background:** EEG-specific foundation models are compared against generic time-series models to assess the necessity of specialized neurophysiological pretraining and architectural design.

**Question / Future Work:** It is currently unclear whether specialized EEG foundation models provide distinct advantages over general-purpose temporal foundation models, raising questions about whether architectural innovations or domain-specific pretraining provide tangible, consistent improvements over generic foundation model architectures.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses a fundamental efficiency and design question: whether the field should invest in domain-specific EEG models or focus on improving general-purpose temporal models.

**Evidence:** EEG-FMs do not consistently outperform generic TS-FMs, raising the question of how much of the reported gain stems from EEG-specific architectural choices or pretraining on neurophysiological data.
