title: Personalization and Multimodal Fusion
---

**Background:** Wrist-worn device gesture recognition typically relies on inertial sensing, yet the integration of physiological signals like photoplethysmography (PPG) remains limited in its real-world effectiveness. Subject-independent generalization and personalization are also critical hurdles in deploying these models in natural settings.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is needed to explore advanced multimodal fusion techniques that more effectively integrate PPG with inertial sensors for gesture recognition, and to investigate personalization techniques that can adapt models to individual user behavior while maintaining privacy and addressing the limitations of current subject-independent evaluation protocols.

**Why It Matters:** The paper identifies that while PPG adds significant value, the exact fusion dynamics and personalization strategies for in-the-wild gesture recognition remain under-optimized, creating a bottleneck for real-world adoption.
