title: Integrating LLMs into Decentralized Governance
---

**Background:** Decentralized prediction markets often utilize optimistic oracles, which rely on human-driven voting to resolve disputes when market outcomes are contested. Research has explored whether AI models can assist or replace these human processes in evaluating factual outcomes.

**Question / Future Work:** While current research indicates that LLMs can achieve high agreement with human-based oracle decisions during the post-dispute resolution phase, it remains unclear how to systematically design and integrate LLM-based adjudication systems to ensure they remain robust against potential adversarial manipulation or systemic bias in real-world governance. Future work is needed to establish frameworks for LLM-assisted adjudication that move beyond proof-of-concept alignment metrics toward secure, auditable, and incentive-compatible governance integration.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical for transitioning from experimental validation to actual implementation in high-stakes financial environments where trust and security are paramount.

**Evidence:** These results affirm that LLMs are capable of approximating UMA arbitration outcomes, though persistent model-specific reliability gaps highlight the need for careful validation before deploying LLM-assisted adjudication in decentralized governance settings.
