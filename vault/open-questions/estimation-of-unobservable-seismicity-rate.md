title: Estimating Unobservable Seismicity Rate
---

**Background:** Seismicity rate (SR) is a statistical construct representing the expected frequency of events in a region rather than a directly observable physical quantity.

**Question / Future Work:** Closed-loop control strategies for subsurface reservoirs rely on regulating the seismicity rate (SR), but in practice, there is no direct physical measurement of the underlying SR. Developing robust methods to estimate the instantaneous SR from discrete, sparse, or noisy earthquake catalogs remains a critical challenge for industrial feedback control.

**Why It Matters:** This represents a fundamental bottleneck for implementing feedback control systems in seismic mitigation, as the control law requires accurate feedback of the system's latent state.

**Evidence:** First and most important, SR is not a directly measurable quantity in practice.
