title: Event-State Causal Coupling Modeling
---

**Background:** Artificial intelligence innovation and public response are typically studied as independent phenomena using separate methodologies, failing to adequately link continuous-time event streams like patent publications with aggregate discrete-time indicators.

**Question / Future Work:** How can joint modeling frameworks effectively capture the directional influence between continuous-time point process event streams and discrete-time aggregate state-based series, especially when the causal coupling is non-reciprocal?

**Why It Matters:** Characterizing the directional interplay between technological output and latent social response is a fundamental challenge for quantitative social science and innovation forecasting.

**Evidence:** Neither family couples a point process event stream with a separate aggregate time series through learnable directional structure.
