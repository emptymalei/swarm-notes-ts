title: Multi-wave Epidemic Forecasting Benchmarks
---

**Background:** Infectious disease forecasting often involves multiple, interconnected waves driven by factors like policy changes, spatial heterogeneity, and viral evolution, which traditional benchmarks often overlook.

**Question / Future Work:** Existing benchmarks focus on isolated single-wave outbreaks; there is an open need to develop evaluation protocols and models that account for multi-wave dynamics, spatial coupling, and mechanistic constraints to better simulate real-world pandemic progression.

**Why It Matters:** Moving beyond isolated outbreaks is essential for developing predictive tools capable of handling the non-stationary, multi-wave nature of long-term epidemiological crises.

**Evidence:** We acknowledge that such an approach inherently neglects multi-wave dynamics that may arise from policy changes, spatial heterogeneity and viral evolution.
