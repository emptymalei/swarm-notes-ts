title: Real-time polyspectral estimation trade-offs
---

**Background:** The translation of robust spectral connectivity metrics into adaptive, closed-loop control systems requires high temporal resolution to track rapid dynamics. Obtaining reliable estimates from short data segments currently presents a significant statistical bottleneck for high-order spectral indices.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the optimal statistical trade-off between data window length, signal-to-noise ratio, and spectral estimation precision is necessary to enable the use of these robust indices for near real-time connectivity tracking.

**Why It Matters:** Temporal resolution is a primary hurdle for transitioning these theoretical metrics into actionable tools for adaptive, personalized interventions like multi-site TMS.

**Evidence:** A rigorous statistical characterization, evaluating the trade-off between data length, signal-to-noise ratio, and spectral estimation techniques, is necessary to determine the most robust analytical approach for tracking connectivity states in near real-time.
