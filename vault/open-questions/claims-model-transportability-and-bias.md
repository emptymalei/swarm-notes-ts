title: Claims Model Transportability Limits
---

**Background:** Administrative claims data are primarily generated for billing purposes and often contain systematic biases related to payer-specific coding, reimbursement, and coverage rules.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigating the transportability of claims-based foundation models across heterogeneous payer systems and populations requires addressing the confounding effects of plan-specific design, such as cost-sharing and negotiated pricing, and the inherent lack of granular clinical outcomes found in EHRs.

**Why It Matters:** Ensuring equitable model utility across different insurance environments is critical for using claims data as a reliable substrate for clinical and public health informatics.

**Evidence:** Consequently, predicted expenditure may reflect both underlying health needs and plan-specific patterns of access and reimbursement, limiting transportability to uninsured populations or payer systems with different payment rules.
