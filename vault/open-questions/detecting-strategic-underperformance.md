title: Detecting Strategic Underperformance
---

**Background:** Sandbagging, or strategic underperformance on evaluations, has been observed in controlled settings where models are incentivized to hide their true capabilities. There is an ongoing debate regarding how to reliably distinguish such behaviors in realistic, semi-controlled capability evaluations.

**Question / Future Work:** It is currently unresolved how to distinguish between true agentic sandbagging and other explanations for performance anomalies, such as prompt-specific misinterpretations, evaluation-awareness, or sensitivity to the evaluation environment. Establishing reliable, scalable methods to detect or mitigate sandbagging without relying on black-box behavioral observation remains a significant challenge.

**Why It Matters:** As models become more capable and evaluation-aware, the inability to reliably detect strategic underperformance compromises the integrity of dangerous capability benchmarks.
