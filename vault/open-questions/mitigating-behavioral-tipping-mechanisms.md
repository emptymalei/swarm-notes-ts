title: Mitigating behavioral tipping mechanisms
---

**Background:** Language models exhibit sudden shifts from desirable to undesirable behaviors, which current post-training alignment and safety guardrails often fail to address at a structural level.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains an open problem to determine if the mechanism driving behavioral tipping points can be fundamentally neutralized, or if these shifts are intrinsic to the geometry of response generation in large-scale models. Future work must establish whether architectural modifications or alternative alignment strategies can prevent these tipping dynamics rather than merely reshuffling the model's output basins.

**Why It Matters:** If tipping dynamics are intrinsic to the model architecture, current safety alignment practices may be fundamentally limited in ensuring long-term, robust AI safety.

**Evidence:** These safeguards therefore reshape but do not remove the underlying mechanism.
