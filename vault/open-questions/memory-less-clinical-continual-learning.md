title: Memory-less Clinical Continual Learning
---

**Background:** Clinical settings often impose strict privacy constraints that limit the sharing of patient data between institutions, complicating traditional continual learning approaches that rely on accessing representative past data.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work should focus on developing effective, memory-less continual learning strategies that can maintain model performance across different clinical domains while adhering to strict privacy restrictions on inter-hospital patient data transfer.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses a fundamental architectural conflict between standard continual learning benchmarks (which often rely on replay) and the reality of health data privacy.

**Evidence:** in the clinical setting, privacy constraints restrict the transfer of patient information from one clinic to another.
