title: Statistical Fit vs. Physical Reasoning
---

**Background:** Frontier agents often achieve high statistical fit quality in radial-velocity (RV) analysis, but they frequently fail to recover the correct physical planetary configuration.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a significant capability gap between numerical optimization (curve fitting) and physical reasoning (correctly identifying the underlying planetary architecture). It remains unresolved whether LLM-based agents can reliably perform model selection—such as determining the correct number of planets and distinguishing true physical signals from aliases—rather than simply minimizing residuals within a misspecified model.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding this limitation is essential for transitioning AI agents from passive data processors to reliable scientific research partners in physics and astronomy.

**Evidence:** Frontier models often achieve strong statistical fits but fail to recover correct orbital parameters.
