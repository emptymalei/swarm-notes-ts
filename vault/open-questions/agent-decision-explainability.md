title: Agent Decision Explainability
---

**Background:** While agent-based systems improve forecast accuracy through structured reasoning, the synthesis of multiple tool outputs by large language models lacks full transparency. This opacity complicates the interpretation of specific adjustments in professional agricultural environments.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to enhance the explainability of LLM-based agent decisions, moving beyond simple tool-call auditing to provide a transparent synthesis of why specific forecasting corrections were applied.

**Why It Matters:** Explainability is critical for trust and accountability in commercial decision-support systems where users must understand the rationale behind AI-driven adjustments.

**Evidence:** A fixed heuristic correction method is auditable... whereas the agent may reach the same conclusion via different reasoning paths on different runs. In a commercial setting... this is a meaningful limitation.
