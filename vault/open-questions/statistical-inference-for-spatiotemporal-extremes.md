title: Inference for spatio-temporal extremes
---

**Background:** Spatio-temporal extreme value modeling often utilizes point process convergence to quantify the risk of rare, localized events. While asymptotic results are established for independent or univariate time series, the formal extension to spatially indexed time series with site-dependent risk functionals is a complex frontier.

**Question / Future Work:** The framework for the asymptotic behavior of point processes of exceedances in a spatio-temporal setting requires future research into robust statistical estimation procedures for the parameters of these limit point processes, particularly in complex spatial domains. Furthermore, the practical sensitivity of these models to the specification of site-dependent risk functionals and threshold selection remains an open challenge.

**Why It Matters:** This work provides the theoretical foundation for spatio-temporal extremes, but the gap between theoretical limit theorems and practical statistical inference remains substantial for applied researchers.

**Evidence:** However, limit results for such spatio-temporal point processes are still missing in the literature. To fill this gap, in this paper, we will assume that {𝑿_t, t∈ℤ} is a stationary spatially indexed time series, see Section 2 for more details, and consider point processes of exceedances that are based on site-dependent risk functionals and consisting of the rescaled values of the time series, the rescaled occurrence times and sites.
