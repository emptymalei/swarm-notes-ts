title: High-Redshift SIDM Coupling Calibration
---

**Background:** Hydrodynamical simulations of self-interacting dark matter (SIDM) currently lack coverage of high-redshift environments (z > 6), where unique conditions like lower halo concentrations and shallower baryonic potentials may alter the coupling between dark matter potential and the interstellar medium.

**Question / Future Work:** Existing studies rely on an assumed range for the coupling efficiency parameter (η) between the dark matter potential and the interstellar medium, which is currently calibrated only at z=0. Dedicated high-redshift SIDM simulations incorporating radiative transfer are required to accurately determine how this coupling evolves at early cosmic times.

**Why It Matters:** η is identified as the dominant theoretical uncertainty in constraining SIDM at high redshift, directly influencing the interpretation of UV luminosity function data.

**Evidence:** No simulations exist at z > 6, where lower concentrations and shallower baryonic potentials could amplify the coupling. We adopt η ∈ [0, 0.25] as a physically motivated range and note that dedicated high-redshift SIDM simulations with radiative transfer are needed to calibrate η in the relevant regime.
