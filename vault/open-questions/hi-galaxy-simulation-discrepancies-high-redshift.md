title: HI galaxy simulation discrepancies
---

**Background:** Predictions of HI galaxy number counts and clustering from different cosmological simulations exhibit significant discrepancies that grow with increasing redshift. Current models lack consensus on the expected HI galaxy distribution for deep surveys planned for next-generation radio observatories.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a lack of agreement between semi-analytic models and hydrodynamical simulations regarding HI galaxy observables like number density and bias at high redshift (z > 0.5) and faint flux densities. Reconciling these theoretical discrepancies is essential for accurately forecasting the utility of future radio galaxy surveys.

**Why It Matters:** This uncertainty hampers the ability to reliably forecast the sensitivity and cosmological utility of future large-scale HI surveys, potentially biasing cosmological parameter inference.

**Evidence:** At low redshift, the number densities disagree with one another by a factor of a few, but this grows to more than an order of magnitude above z ≳ 0.5... The bias is also quite uncertain.
