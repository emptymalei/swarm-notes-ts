title: Scored Oversight Dimensional Boundaries
---

**Background:** Scored oversight mechanisms involve a principal evaluating an agent's report against a realized outcome using a scoring rule, where the agent has a private type parameter representing information quality. While True-KL0 properties are established for specific scoring rule families in low dimensions, the existence of a corresponding critical threshold for the rule parameter remains uncharacterized for higher dimensions.

**Question / Future Work:** Determine the critical rule parameter threshold p_crit(d) for dimensional boundaries d >= 6 in the scored oversight mechanism. While a threshold exists for d=5, it remains unestablished for d >= 6, limiting the applicability of these truth-eliciting mechanisms in higher-dimensional tasks.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the limits of dominant-strategy incentive compatibility (DSIC) as the dimension of the oversight interface increases is crucial for scaling AI oversight mechanisms.
