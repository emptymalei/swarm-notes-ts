title: Long-horizon drift scaling limits
---

**Background:** Precipitation nowcasting methods are typically formulated as autoregressive processes that predict future states step-by-step from previous outputs, which inherently leads to error accumulation over long horizons. This compounding error, known as drift, causes forecasts to deviate from physically plausible evolution trajectories.

**Question / Future Work:** The scalability and robustness of memory-guided correction mechanisms for maintainining temporal consistency under significantly longer forecasting horizons remains an open challenge. Determining the limits of these methods is essential for extending nowcasting systems to longer temporal scales.

**Why It Matters:** Evaluating the limit of memory-based drift correction is critical for extending nowcasting systems to longer temporal scales, which are essential for proactive disaster management.
