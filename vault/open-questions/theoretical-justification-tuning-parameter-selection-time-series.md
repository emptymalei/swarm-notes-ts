title: Theoretical justification for parameter selection
---

**Background:** Adaptive thresholding methods for sparse covariance estimation typically rely on data-driven tuning parameter selection, often implemented via cross-validation.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of theoretically justified, efficient procedures for selecting regularization parameters in adaptive thresholding for high-dimensional dependent time series remains an open problem, as existing block cross-validation methods lack rigorous theoretical support in this context.

**Why It Matters:** Reliable tuning parameter selection is critical for the practical utility and statistical consistency of thresholding estimators in high-dimensional settings.

**Evidence:** Although we do not provide a theoretical justification for this data-driven procedure, our numerical studies show that it performs reasonably well. ... Future work will focus on developing theoretically justified procedures for selecting regularization parameters.
