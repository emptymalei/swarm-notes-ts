title: Generalization Bounds for Sensorless Forecasting
---

**Background:** Sensorless force and wrench estimation depends on the mapping of proprioceptive state history to interaction dynamics, which frequently encounters distributional drift across varying robotic platforms and task contexts.

**Question / Future Work:** A systematic characterization is required of how sensorless wrench estimation models generalize across different robot hardware, contact materials, and operating conditions, beyond single-environment empirical validation. Establishing benchmarks for cross-domain stability is critical for the deployment of these models in industrial robotics.

**Why It Matters:** Determining the boundaries of generalization for proprioceptive-to-wrench mapping is necessary to move beyond isolated case studies toward universal, robust robotic interaction models.
