title: Multitask urban spatiotemporal forecasting
---

**Background:** Spatiotemporal traffic forecasting models often struggle to achieve a balance between temporal expressiveness, long-range spatial dependency modeling, and computational scalability.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing models that generalize beyond univariate sensor data to complex, multi-modal, and multitask urban prediction scenarios remains a significant challenge for deploying intelligent transportation systems.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the transition from constrained benchmark performance to real-world, complex urban environment modeling.

**Evidence:** As future work, it would be valuable to extend FAST to more challenging settings, such as multivariate traffic forecasting, adaptive spatial structure modeling, and multitask urban prediction with richer contextual signals.
