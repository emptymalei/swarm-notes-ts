title: Ethical Design for Hazard Communication
---

**Background:** Weather forecasting in extreme, high-stakes environments often requires communicating complex risk information to diverse audiences with varying degrees of weather and graphical literacy. Achieving a balance between descriptive, nuanced data and actionable, prescriptive warnings while preserving user trust remains a significant challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research must address the tension between simplifying complex forecast data for rapid consumption and maintaining the depth of information required by domain experts, while establishing ethical standards for information design that prevent both over-simplification and the alienation of diverse user demographics in high-risk domains.

**Why It Matters:** This question exposes a fundamental trade-off in human-AI/information-system interaction design where model output needs to be actionable yet nuanced for heterogeneous user groups.

**Evidence:** we highlight remaining questions around visualization design and design ethics that warrant further study in the domain of how best to communicate cold weather hazards in ways that are mindful of the diversity of literacies and experiences of visitors.
