title: AGI Safety and Governance Gaps
---

**Background:** Technological, institutional, and economic shifts driven by AGI could lead to global instability, power concentration, and the emergence of new, potentially destabilizing, military paradigms. Current international governance frameworks and safety research are in their infancy, with technical methods to ensure alignment and oversight being insufficient for advanced, agentic systems.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved need to develop and integrate robust governance frameworks that ensure AGI remains aligned with human values and constrained by democratic checks and balances, while preventing power concentration in the hands of corporate or national elites. This requires bridging the gap between theoretical AI safety research and enforceable international policy, particularly as AGI-enabled tools lower the barriers to creating mass-casualty weapons or conducting large-scale social manipulation.

**Why It Matters:** These risks represent systemic, global challenges that exceed the control of any single nation-state, and lack current, mature policy solutions, making this an essential focus for future international cooperation.
