title: Uncertainty in causal forecasts
---

**Background:** Predicting potential outcomes in dynamic treatment regimes often necessitates quantifying the uncertainty associated with those causal forecasts to inform clinical decision-making.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of formal methods for uncertainty quantification within the proposed causal adjustment framework is a key direction for future methodological research.

**Why It Matters:** Providing confidence intervals or uncertainty bounds is essential for the clinical application of these models, as clinicians need to understand the reliability of predicted treatment outcomes to make safe medical decisions.

**Evidence:** The adjustment formula also provides a natural basis for uncertainty quantification of causal forecasts, an important direction for future methodological work.
