title: Real-time Heterogeneous Data Integration
---

**Background:** Modern air quality forecasting systems increasingly rely on integrating real-time, multi-modal data streams, including satellite observations, high-resolution sensor networks, and dynamic emission inventories. Adapting models to effectively assimilate these heterogeneous and potentially sparse data sources in real-time remains a significant challenge for robust environmental decision-making.

**Question / Future Work:** Future development should focus on enhancing the framework's ability to ingest and adapt to real-time, heterogeneous data streams (e.g., satellite imagery, disparate sensor networks, and emission data) to maintain predictive accuracy under rapidly changing and unpredictable urban environmental conditions.

**Why It Matters:** Robust, real-time integration of heterogeneous data is essential for the practical deployment of air quality forecasting systems in dynamic, decision-critical urban environments.

**Evidence:** Moreover, integrating real-time satellite, sensor, and emission data streams may enable adaptive forecasting under rapidly changing conditions.
