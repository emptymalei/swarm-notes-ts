title: Unified SCED Reserve Optimization
---

**Background:** Security-constrained economic dispatch (SCED) relies on exogenous uncertainty-set designs for reserve requirements, which often decouples the design phase from the economic realities of the dispatch task.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an explicit need for an application-driven formulation where the uncertainty-set geometry, the SCED solve, and the coverage calibration are optimized as a single, unified pipeline. This includes extending current frameworks to multi-period unit commitment problems, integrating contextual learning, and developing robust extensions that account for structural shifts and extreme operational events.

**Why It Matters:** This is a significant methodological challenge in power systems optimization; solving this provides a direct path to reducing operating costs while maintaining grid reliability in systems with high renewable penetration.

**Evidence:** What remains missing for reserve procurement is an application-driven formulation in which the uncertainty-set geometry, the SCED solve, and the coverage calibration are optimized as one pipeline.
