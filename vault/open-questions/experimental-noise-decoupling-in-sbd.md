title: Experimental Noise Decoupling in SBDs
---

**Background:** Spectral bifurcation diagrams (SBDs) map frequency content against parameter variation to identify dynamical transitions in nonlinear systems. Experimental implementations often suffer from low-power background noise that obscures weak signal components essential for characterizing transitions.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing robust methods to decouple intrinsic system noise from nonlinear responses in experimental SBDs without the loss of critical spectral data or the degradation of nonlinear signatures is a persistent bottleneck.

**Why It Matters:** Robust noise separation is critical for transitioning SBD diagnostics from controlled numerical simulations to noisy real-world physical and industrial applications.
