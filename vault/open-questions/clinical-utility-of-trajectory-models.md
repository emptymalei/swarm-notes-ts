title: Clinical utility of trajectory models
---

**Background:** Foundation models for clinical forecasting are often evaluated on their ability to predict future events based on longitudinal structured diagnostic histories. The utility of these models in actual clinical environments remains uncertain, as prospective performance and integration into decision-support systems have not been fully established.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining whether transformer-based disease trajectory models can effectively inform clinical decision-making or system-level forecasting requires transitioning from retrospective validation to prospective, real-world clinical evaluation paradigms.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the gap between high-performing predictive models in research settings and actionable clinical tools is essential for the translation of AI in medicine.

**Evidence:** Whether such models can ultimately inform clinical decision-making or system-level forecasting remains an open question, requiring further work on model development and alternative evaluation paradigms.
