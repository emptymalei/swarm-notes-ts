title: Astrophysical Neutrino Source Uncertainty
---

**Background:** The flavor composition of high-energy astrophysical neutrinos provides a unique probe of neutrino mixing parameters at energies beyond the reach of terrestrial accelerators. A persistent bottleneck in this field is distinguishing potential high-energy renormalization-group effects from the inherent uncertainties in astrophysical source flavor production.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains unresolved how to robustly account for the unknown neutrino flavor composition at astrophysical sources, which limits the ability to constrain or discover new high-energy dynamics in neutrino flavor transitions. Developing methods to decouple these source uncertainties from particle physics parameters is essential for future multi-detector observations to conclusively test the evolution of neutrino mixing.

**Why It Matters:** This bottleneck is fundamental to the viability of using neutrino astronomy as a precision tool for probing new physics at high energy scales.

**Evidence:** We account for astrophysical uncertainties -- an unavoidable requirement to obtain realistic results, even though this weakens the bounds.
