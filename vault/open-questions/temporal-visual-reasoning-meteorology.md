title: Temporal Visual Reasoning in Meteorology
---

**Background:** Meteorological forecasting requires the synthesis of static visuals, such as weather maps and thermodynamic diagrams, with domain-specific knowledge to interpret complex atmospheric phenomena. Current multimodal large language models demonstrate significant performance bottlenecks when interpreting these specialized charts compared to text-only tasks.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to extend existing evaluation frameworks to assess the temporal reasoning required to interpret atmospheric evolution, such as sequential satellite imagery and radar loops, as current evaluation focuses primarily on static visual snapshots. Developing metrics and datasets that capture dynamic, time-series visual reasoning in meteorological domains remains an unresolved challenge.

**Why It Matters:** Real-world weather forecasting is inherently dynamic; static analysis is insufficient for predictive modeling. Understanding how models handle temporal atmospheric data is crucial for their reliable deployment as forecasting assistants.

**Evidence:** the current dataset does not evaluate the temporal reasoning required to interpret atmospheric evolution, such as sequential radar imagery or satellite loops.
