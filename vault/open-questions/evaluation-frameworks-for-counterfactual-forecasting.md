title: Evaluation Frameworks for Counterfactual Forecasting
---

**Background:** Time series forecasting is often evaluated using metrics like Mean Squared Error or Mean Absolute Error, which require ground-truth future sequences to calculate. Counterfactual forecasting scenarios involve hypothetical conditions that may not have been realized in the historical data, leaving the ground truth undefined.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of standardized and robust evaluation frameworks for counterfactual time series forecasting remains an open challenge due to the inherent absence of ground-truth data in such settings. While semantic consistency metrics have been introduced, establishing comprehensive, universally applicable benchmarks that can assess the validity and rationality of counterfactual predictions across diverse domains requires further research.

**Why It Matters:** Without ground truth, evaluating whether a model accurately predicts the consequences of a counterfactual intervention is difficult. Tracking progress in this area is vital for moving beyond simple accuracy metrics toward validating the causal and logical consistency of time series models.

**Evidence:** The lack of ground-truth for counterfactual settings presents a fundamental obstacle to evaluate forecasting quality.
