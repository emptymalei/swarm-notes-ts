title: Generalization of shot-organization strategies
---

**Background:** Quantum reservoir computing relies on finite shot records from circuit executions to generate features, creating a trade-off between the precision of the feature estimate and the quantity of training examples.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigating whether shot-organization strategies, such as split-ensemble training, provide consistent performance gains across non-linear readout architectures, quantum kernels, and higher-dimensional feature spaces is necessary to determine their broader utility in near-term quantum machine learning.

**Why It Matters:** Determining the universality of these benefits is key to scaling and optimizing near-term quantum machine learning models.

**Evidence:** Natural extensions include adaptive group sizes, nonlinear readouts, richer observables, larger reservoirs, and related repeated-measurement settings such as quantum kernels, Born machines, or hybrid generative models.
