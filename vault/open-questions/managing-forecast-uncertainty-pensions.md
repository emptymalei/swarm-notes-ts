title: Managing forecast uncertainty in pensions
---

**Background:** The design of optimally balanced pay-as-you-go social security systems depends on long-term demographic and productivity forecasts, which are inherently uncertain. These systems typically face fiscal pressure when realized trends deviate from initial projections.

**Question / Future Work:** There is no established framework for incorporating a 'buffer fund' or 'compensation mechanism' within optimally balanced pay-as-you-go systems to share the burden of forecast uncertainty between generations. Future research is needed to design such mechanisms that maintain the optimality and fiscal balance of the system when projections are not met.

**Why It Matters:** Predictability and intergenerational fairness are critical for social security; without a mechanism to manage forecast errors, such systems are prone to sudden, welfare-reducing policy reversals.
