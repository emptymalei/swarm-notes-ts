title: TSFM vs Specialized PEPF Utility
---

**Background:** Probabilistic electricity price forecasting (PEPF) requires robust uncertainty quantification to manage the volatility introduced by renewable energy integration. While Time Series Foundation Models (TSFMs) and task-specific deep learning models are both used for this task, the optimal balance between computational efficiency and predictive performance remains a subject of investigation in variable market environments.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed to clarify the circumstances under which the computational cost of TSFMs is justified by statistically significant performance gains over optimized task-specific architectures like NHITS+QRA in energy market forecasting.

**Why It Matters:** As energy markets move toward higher integration of volatile renewables, practitioners need definitive guidance on the cost-benefit trade-off of foundation-scale models vs. specialized architectures.

**Evidence:** However, it remains an open question which models to employ to produce accurate forecasts. Should these be task-specific machine learning (ML) models or Time Series Foundation Models (TSFMs)?
