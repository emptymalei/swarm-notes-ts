title: Multi-Robot Coordination in Forestry
---

**Background:** Robotic systems for sustainable forestry must navigate complex, unpredictable, and cluttered environments, often requiring cooperation between heterogeneous platforms like legged robots and drones. While individual navigation and mapping tasks are maturing, managing the high-level collaborative coordination of these multi-robot systems remains a significant operational bottleneck.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is needed to develop and validate collaborative planning and map-sharing strategies for marsupial systems, where an aerial robot is deployed from a ground-based quadruped. This includes refining methods for task-driven, compressed map exchange between agents to optimize exploration efficiency and mission success in challenging forest terrain.

**Why It Matters:** Effective marsupial coordination is critical for balancing the high-mobility, short-endurance capabilities of aerial drones with the high-endurance, ground-restricted movement of quadrupedal robots in large-scale forestry operations.

**Evidence:** Testing in forested environments of co-planning and map sharing method... is subject to future work. The method is able to assess whether a deployment of the aerial robot is necessary... and, when the aerial robot is deployed, the two systems exchange a compressed shared map over wifi and individually perform mapping and exploration of the designed area.
