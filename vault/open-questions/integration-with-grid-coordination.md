title: Grid-Data Center Flexibility Coordination
---

**Background:** AI data centers exhibit bursty, non-stationary power demand patterns driven by complex, hidden workload dynamics at the individual node level. Current forecasting models often face an accuracy-deployment tradeoff, where high-capacity sequence models are too computationally expensive for real-time inference, while lightweight point-wise models lack the capacity to capture temporal dependencies.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to investigate the broader integration of deployment-efficient forecasting models into large-scale grid-data center coordination systems. Specifically, research is needed to determine how these lightweight models can effectively support real-time flexibility management, demand response, and job scheduling under bursty AI workload conditions.

**Why It Matters:** This is a key domain-specific application area. Connecting low-level load forecasting to high-level grid flexibility and demand response is critical for the practical utility of these models in sustainable energy management.
