title: Endogenous Project Realization Uncertainty
---

**Background:** Energy infrastructure planning frequently assumes that infrastructure assets can be constructed and commissioned according to predefined schedules. However, real-world projects are regularly delayed or canceled due to factors like permitting challenges and interconnection queues, which are rarely modeled endogenously.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is required to develop frameworks that endogenously model the uncertainty and probability of project realization within capacity expansion models. Future work should investigate how these endogenous realization risks affect optimal investment strategies, system reliability, and long-term decarbonization pathways, moving beyond current deterministic assumptions.

**Why It Matters:** Omitting construction and interconnection delays leads to overly optimistic expansion plans that fail to reflect the reality of infrastructure deployment, potentially resulting in systemic reliability issues or missed decarbonization targets.
