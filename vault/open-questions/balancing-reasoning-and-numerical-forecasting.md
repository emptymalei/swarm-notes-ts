title: Unifying reasoning and numerical performance
---

**Background:** LLM-based time series forecasting methods often struggle to balance general-purpose semantic reasoning with precise numerical sequence prediction.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop unified architectures that can leverage the reasoning strengths of LLMs while achieving the numerical accuracy levels of specialized forecasting models, particularly under dynamic environments and temporal distribution shifts. Addressing this requires overcoming the limitations of static generative paradigms, where a single model is forced to balance conflicting objectives of semantic reasoning and continuous-valued sequence prediction.

**Why It Matters:** This is a foundational bottleneck in LLM-based time series forecasting, impacting their application in high-stakes, real-world scenarios.
