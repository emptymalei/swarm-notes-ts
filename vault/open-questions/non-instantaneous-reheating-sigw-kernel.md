title: SIGW kernels for non-instantaneous reheating
---

**Background:** The calculation of scalar-induced gravitational waves (SIGWs) often relies on the instantaneous reheating approximation, which simplifies the transition from the inflationary epoch to the radiation-dominated era. This approximation neglects the detailed dynamics and potentially non-trivial evolution of the Universe during this transition phase.

**Question / Future Work:** The precise SIGW feature near the reheating scale depends on the transition to radiation domination, for which an exact analytical kernel is currently unavailable. Extending the analysis to include the full, non-instantaneous dynamics of the reheating transition remains a significant open challenge in accurately predicting SIGW spectra.

**Why It Matters:** This is a critical technical bottleneck because the current reliance on the instantaneous reheating approximation may lead to inaccurate predictions for signals whose characteristic frequencies are close to the reheating temperature scale.
