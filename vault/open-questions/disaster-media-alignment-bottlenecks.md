title: Media-Disaster Database Alignment Challenges
---

**Background:** Disaster event reporting in international media often exhibits significant temporal misalignment with official disaster databases, which are typically designed for local or regional administrative purposes.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop more sophisticated, robust methods to align media-based event detection with official disaster databases. Current approaches using simple temporal windowing frequently fail due to the ambiguous or aggregated nature of media coverage compared to the specific, location-bounded entries in national disaster registries. Further research is required to resolve these misalignments and to integrate multi-source data effectively.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing this misalignment is critical for validating the use of global news as a proxy for disaster impact and for understanding the selective nature of media attention, which in turn influences humanitarian aid and policy.
