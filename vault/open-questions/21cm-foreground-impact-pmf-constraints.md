title: 21-cm foreground impact on cosmological constraints
---

**Background:** The 21-cm power spectrum from the post-reionization epoch is a highly sensitive probe of small-scale matter clustering, but it is heavily obscured by intense astrophysical and terrestrial foregrounds. Foreground removal remains a central challenge for intensity mapping experiments attempting to extract cosmological signals.

**Question / Future Work:** Current forecasts for constraining primordial magnetic fields (PMF) using the 21-cm auto-power spectrum often assume idealized foreground-free conditions. A rigorous assessment of how foreground removal techniques and residual foreground power impact the ability to constrain PMF parameters is required to ensure these observational goals are scientifically attainable.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental bottleneck for 21-cm intensity mapping science; without realistic foreground modeling, forecast constraints remain overly optimistic.

**Evidence:** the presence of strong foregrounds in the 21 cm sector has been neglected in this study, which must be taken into account for a more realistic analysis of the 21 cm auto-spectrum and its capacity of providing constraints on the PMF sector.
