title: Multi-source Data Integration
---

**Background:** Building load forecasting is frequently constrained by limited input features, often relying solely on historical load and basic weather indices. Integrating diverse exogenous data streams into forecasting architectures remains an active challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to explore the robust integration of heterogeneous multi-source data, such as real-time occupancy schedules, human behavioral patterns, and building topology, into deep time-series models to better capture complex energy load dynamics.

**Why It Matters:** Expanding input features is a critical step to moving beyond simple time-series forecasting and capturing the underlying physical and social drivers of energy consumption.

**Evidence:** Future research will focus on integrating broader multi-source data, such as real-time occupancy schedules, user behaviors, and spatial building topologies, to better model complex load dynamics.
