title: Interpretability in Federated Weather Models
---

**Background:** Deep learning models are increasingly utilized in weather modeling, yet their internal decision-making processes often remain opaque to researchers and forecasters. Providing transparency and clear explanations is essential for building trust and ensuring the reliability of predictive systems in safety-critical meteorological applications.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an urgent need to develop interpretable federated weather modeling systems. Currently, complex deep learning architectures hinder users from understanding which specific meteorological factors influence forecasting decisions, how disparate variables interact, or why the model prioritizes certain data sources over others. Improving interpretability is crucial to prevent prediction errors and provide actionable insights for environmental and social decision-making.

**Why It Matters:** Interpretability is essential for the adoption of deep learning in high-stakes weather forecasting where opaque models pose significant risks to societal and environmental safety.

**Evidence:** A reliable federated weather modeling system must provide clear explanations of its decision-making, ensuring transparency and building trust for real-world weather applications.
