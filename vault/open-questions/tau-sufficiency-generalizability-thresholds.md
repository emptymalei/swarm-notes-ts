title: Universality of tau-sufficiency thresholds
---

**Background:** Forecast evaluation in energy markets often relies on cardinal error metrics (MAE/RMSE) despite many dispatch decisions being ordinal in nature. The concept of tau-sufficiency proposes that a specific rank correlation threshold is more predictive of decision performance.

**Question / Future Work:** It is currently unknown if the identified tau-sufficiency threshold (τ ≈ 0.85–0.95) is a universal constant for electricity dispatch or if it varies significantly based on market liquidity, regional volatility, and the specific hierarchical structure of the power grid. Expanding this analysis to diverse international markets is required to establish its robustness as a general performance indicator.

**Why It Matters:** Standardizing forecast metrics around decision-utility rather than statistical error is a high-leverage move for energy market operations.

**Evidence:** Validation on other European markets would refine the quantitative estimate of τ* and strengthen the generalizability of the finding.
