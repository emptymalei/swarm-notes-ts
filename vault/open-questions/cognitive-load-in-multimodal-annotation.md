title: Multimodal Annotation Cognitive Load
---

**Background:** The integration of multi-modal robotic sensory data (e.g., force/torque, proprioception) in annotation tools improves temporal precision but complicates user interaction.

**Question / Future Work:** It is currently unclear how to optimize the presentation or interface design of multi-modal robotic data to balance annotation efficiency with temporal precision. Systematic research into cognitive load during the annotation of complex robotic tasks is needed to develop more efficient human-in-the-loop interfaces.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing this tradeoff is critical for making large-scale manual annotation of robotic data scalable.

**Evidence:** We hypothesize that the additional sensor information increases the cognitive load during annotation, as users must analyze multiple data modalities simultaneously. This may slow down the annotation process; however, further experiments are required to validate this hypothesis.
