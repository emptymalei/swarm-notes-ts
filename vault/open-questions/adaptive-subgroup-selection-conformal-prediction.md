title: Adaptive subgroup selection methods
---

**Background:** Mondrian conformal prediction enables group-specific uncertainty quantification by computing thresholds for individual predefined subgroups. In thin-cell settings where subgroup observation counts are small, this approach is often unstable and can exacerbate fairness-efficiency trade-offs.

**Question / Future Work:** Research is needed into adaptive or data-driven methods for subgroup selection that move beyond fixed, pre-defined demographic boundaries to improve conditional coverage. This includes techniques that cluster calibration cells to satisfy multi-group coverage requirements while minimizing prediction set inflation.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing fixed subgroup limitations is essential because predefined demographic categories may not align with the actual dimensions where model uncertainty or disparate impact are concentrated.
