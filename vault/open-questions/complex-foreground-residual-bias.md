title: Complex Foreground Residual Bias
---

**Background:** Needlet internal linear combination (NILC) is a method for component separation in CMB studies to remove foreground contamination. Current implementations often rely on specific, simplified foreground models and assumptions regarding residual bias.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to assess the impact of more complex, realistic Galactic foreground models on the residual bias in CMB polarization power spectra. Currently, simplified models may underestimate foreground residuals, potentially impacting the interpretation of large-scale anomalies.

**Why It Matters:** As instrumental sensitivity increases, foreground contamination and the accuracy of foreground cleaning methods become dominant sources of systematic error in testing CMB anomalies.

**Evidence:** We defer to future work the consideration of residual foreground spectra arising from more complex foreground models.
