title: Integrating Exogenous Covariates
---

**Background:** Urban air quality forecasting often relies on historical pollutant data without explicit integration of external factors such as meteorology, traffic patterns, and industrial emissions.

**Question / Future Work:** Future efforts should investigate the integration of exogenous domain-specific covariates into graph-based forecasting architectures to enhance predictive capacity and physical interpretability.

**Why It Matters:** Accounting for exogenous physical drivers is a known bottleneck for improving accuracy and causal interpretability in spatiotemporal air quality models.

**Evidence:** The current framework relies mainly on historical pollutant concentrations and a distance-based graph structure, without explicitly incorporating meteorological, traffic, land-use, or emission-source variables.
