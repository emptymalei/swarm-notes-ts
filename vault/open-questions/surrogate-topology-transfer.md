title: Topology Transfer in Surrogates
---

**Background:** Surrogate models for complex physical systems are often trained on high-fidelity synthetic data before being adapted to experimental or operational data. A key question is whether these models can effectively generalize across different system configurations without extensive re-training.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is needed to evaluate the feasibility of \"topology transfer,\" wherein a surrogate model trained on one facility is transferred to other facilities with related but distinct thermal-hydraulic layouts, assessing if pre-trained physical priors accelerate convergence on new system configurations.

**Why It Matters:** Validating topology transfer would significantly reduce the computational cost and data requirements for deploying digital twins across diverse reactor designs and experimental facilities.
