title: Hybrid Models for Upside Risks
---

**Background:** Quantile-based forecasting models and stochastic volatility models often exhibit complementary strengths, with the former performing well for downside risks and the latter for upside spikes.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop hybrid forecasting frameworks that synthesize the benefits of quantile-specific coefficient modeling with flexible variance dynamics to address the persistent difficulty of capturing upside tail risks, particularly during supply-side market shocks.

**Why It Matters:** Improving upside tail risk prediction is essential for energy market risk management, where models currently struggle compared to downside risk assessment.

**Evidence:** The persistent difficulty in forecasting right-tail events suggests that upside risks may require hybrid approaches combining quantile-specific coefficients with stochastic volatility.
