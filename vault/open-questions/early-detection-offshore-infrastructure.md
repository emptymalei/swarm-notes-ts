title: Early-stage offshore infrastructure detection
---

**Background:** Offshore wind infrastructure monitoring relies on identifying the location of structures before analyzing their temporal deployment dynamics. Current methods often face a tradeoff between precision and recall, especially in complex near-coastal or harbor environments.

**Question / Future Work:** Reliable early-onset detection of turbine foundations without prior location knowledge remains a significant bottleneck. Research is needed to develop detection strategies capable of capturing the very first construction signals—without being overwhelmed by false positives—and potentially moving toward single-acquisition, near-real-time monitoring.

**Why It Matters:** Spatial detection serves as the fundamental prerequisite for time series analysis; without efficient early-stage detection, the ability to monitor the full lifecycle of offshore wind energy infrastructure in near-real-time is limited.
