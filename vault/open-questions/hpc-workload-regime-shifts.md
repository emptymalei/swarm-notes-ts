title: Predicting HPC Workload Shifts
---

**Background:** Supercomputing facilities exhibit distinct shifts in operational patterns and resource utilization metrics over long-term deployment periods. The presence of abrupt, non-stationary changes in workload behavior, such as those observed in mid-2017 for the Titan supercomputer, complicates the development of robust predictive models based on longitudinal data.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop methodologies that can account for and adapt to regime shifts in HPC cluster workload behavior. Current predictive models struggle when system usage patterns change significantly, suggesting a requirement for models that are either resilient to these shifts or capable of dynamic recalibration as systemic usage evolves over time.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical for improving the accuracy of long-term resource forecasting in evolving HPC environments, where static training data may become obsolete due to shifting user demographics, software changes, or policy adjustments.
