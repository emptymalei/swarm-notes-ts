title: Logistics model generalization limits
---

**Background:** Logistics predictive frameworks are often evaluated on static topologies, leaving the transferability of these models to networks with different structural properties and demand distributions largely unexplored.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work should investigate the cross-network generalizability of supply chain graph learning models to ensure consistent performance across diverse, heterogeneous operational environments.

**Why It Matters:** Adaptability to varying network architectures is a fundamental requirement for the broad industrial adoption of supply chain forecasting models.
