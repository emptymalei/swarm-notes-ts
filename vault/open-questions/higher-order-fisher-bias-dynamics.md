title: Higher-order Fisher information bias
---

**Background:** The Fisher information acts as a Riemannian metric on a statistical model, representing the norm of an infinitesimal displacement along the model curve on a probability simplex. In practical applications, this curve is reconstructed from noisy, sampled time-series data, resulting in a discrete statistical model with an associated bias in the estimated Fisher information.

**Question / Future Work:** While the bias of the Fisher information for a sampled statistical model is characterized to leading order in terms of the sample size, higher-order corrections depending on the underlying dynamics are not fully resolved. Determining the exact influence of curvature and higher-order moments on the estimation metric is crucial for refining dynamical reconstruction accuracy.

**Why It Matters:** Enables more precise bias correction beyond current universal leading-order approximations, which is critical for systems with sparse or noisy data.
