title: Adaptive Spatial Structure Modeling
---

**Background:** Spatiotemporal forecasting often relies on predefined graph structures to model spatial dependencies between sensors, limiting the model's ability to adapt to non-static or unobserved inter-sensor relationships.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing spatial modeling techniques that can dynamically learn or adapt graph topologies without relying on static adjacency matrices remains a critical challenge for improving the generalization of traffic and network forecasting models.

**Why It Matters:** Moving beyond fixed graphs is essential for creating robust, generalizable spatiotemporal models in real-world environments where connectivity patterns evolve over time.
