title: Optimal SDN Controller Placement
---

**Background:** The placement of a central controller in software-defined satellite networks requires a balance between global topology visibility, computational capacity, and propagation latency constraints.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the optimal deployment strategy—whether ground-based, space-based, or hybrid—remains an unresolved challenge due to the trade-offs between the computational power of terrestrial clouds and the persistent line-of-sight visibility of orbital platforms. Further research is needed to develop efficient protocols for distributed control, state synchronization across variable-latency links, and mechanisms for robust split-brain resolution in split-control architectures.

**Why It Matters:** The choice of controller placement fundamentally dictates the feasibility of real-time orchestration for mega-constellations; an suboptimal architecture would exacerbate latency bottlenecks or lead to network instability.
