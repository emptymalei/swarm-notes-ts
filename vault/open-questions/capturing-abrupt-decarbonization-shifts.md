title: Capturing abrupt decarbonization shifts
---

**Background:** Integrated Assessment Model (IAM) scenarios project high-dimensional climate transition time series, which often include abrupt policy shifts that are poorly captured by aggregate long-term trend metrics.

**Question / Future Work:** Current decarbonization speed metrics often rely on smooth parametric functions that fail to resolve or quantify acute phase-shifts or abrupt policy-driven transitions in climate pathways. Developing metrics capable of capturing these discontinuities is essential for distinguishing the systemic risks associated with sudden vs. gradual mitigation strategies.

**Why It Matters:** The ability to distinguish between gradual and abrupt policy shifts is critical for accurate risk assessment in climate modeling, where the timing and rate of transition significantly alter economic and physical outcomes.
