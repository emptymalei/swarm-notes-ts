title: Non-stationary stochastic forcing dynamics
---

**Background:** Stochastic models of complex systems, such as precipitation, rely on parameterized forcing to simulate real-world outcomes under fluctuating environmental conditions.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate how parameterized stochastic forcing in jump-diffusion models captures the emergence of extreme events and multifractal scaling behavior under non-stationary conditions, such as long-term climate-driven environmental shifts.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding how non-stationary environmental shifts dynamically alter the intensity, frequency, and multifractal complexity of precipitation is a central challenge in climate-aware forecasting.

**Evidence:** investigate the emergence of extreme events and multifractal scaling under slowly varying environmental conditions
