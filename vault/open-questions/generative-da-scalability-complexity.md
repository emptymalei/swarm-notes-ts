title: Generative DA Scalability Limits
---

**Background:** Diffusion models used for data assimilation in geophysical fluid flows are often evaluated on idealized testbeds, leaving the scalability of these methods to high-complexity or operational environments largely unexplored.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to determine the scalability and performance of generative DA frameworks in more realistic, high-complexity scenarios where dynamical systems are non-idealized and observation processes are highly diverse. Investigating this robustness is a critical bottleneck for transitioning from idealized fluid flow research to operational weather and climate forecasting.

**Why It Matters:** Determines the viability of generative data assimilation for real-world application, representing a major research frontier.
