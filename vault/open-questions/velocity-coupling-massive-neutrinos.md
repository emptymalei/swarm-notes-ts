title: Velocity coupling in massive neutrinos
---

**Background:** Current cosmological models and Large-Scale Structure (LSS) analyses often assume that galaxies trace the cold dark matter plus baryon (cdb) field and that their velocity fields coincide. However, the theoretical validity of this assumption in the presence of massive neutrinos is not fully established.

**Question / Future Work:** A self-consistent theoretical derivation is required to justify the common ansatz that galaxies, as tracers of large-scale structure, fall at the same rate as the CDM plus baryon field (v_g = v_cb) in cosmologies with massive neutrinos. Investigating potential deviations from this approximation is necessary to ensure the accuracy of future high-precision cosmological constraints.

**Why It Matters:** The assumption of galaxy-matter velocity coupling directly affects the interpretation of Redshift Space Distortions (RSD) and the resulting cosmological parameter constraints. As surveys move toward sub-percent precision, the potential bias introduced by this assumption needs to be rigorously quantified.

**Evidence:** While a self consistent theoretical derivation of this result is still the subject of ongoing study, we adopt this ansatz in all that follows.
