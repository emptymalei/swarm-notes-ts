title: Cross-Population Clinical Generalizability
---

**Background:** Clinical diagnostic and treatment practices vary significantly across different hospital settings, patient demographics, and geographical locations. Evaluating predictive models across diverse populations is crucial for ensuring clinical generalizability and fairness.

**Question / Future Work:** Further evaluation is needed to understand the generalizability of spectral augmentation and predictive performance gains across a broader range of international patient populations, regional health practices, and demographic groups.

**Why It Matters:** Demonstrating generalizability across diverse ICU cohorts is a prerequisite for the reliable clinical application of sepsis prediction models.
