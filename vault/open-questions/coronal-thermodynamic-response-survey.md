title: Parametric Survey of Atmospheric Coupling
---

**Background:** The solar atmosphere exhibits a non-monotonic thermodynamic response to magnetic flux emergence, where increased chromospheric heating can paradoxically lead to lower coronal temperatures due to increased mass loading and subsequent radiative losses. The precise mechanisms behind this coupling and the threshold at which these effects occur are not fully established.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is needed to perform a more comprehensive parametric survey across a broader range of magnetic configurations and environments to confirm the robustness of the observed non-monotonic thermodynamic response. This includes quantifying the relative contributions of mass loading and radiative cooling as primary regulators of coronal-base temperatures in response to varied magnetic field inputs.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding this density-driven cooling is essential for improving the accuracy of space-weather forecasts and global solar-wind models, which currently rely on simplified prescriptions of the low-solar atmosphere.

**Evidence:** Assessing the robustness and physical origin of this trend over a broader parameter space is a key objective of future work.
