title: Scaling and Refining Degradation-Aware EMS
---

**Background:** Fuel cell degradation is a major driver of operational costs in shipboard power systems, necessitating the development of effective degradation-aware energy management strategies. While current research has focused on fuel consumption minimization, there is a need for robust, multi-objective control approaches that effectively handle dynamic operational conditions and aging.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to extend the current energy management framework to accommodate multiple parallel generation and storage devices, coordinate different component ratings, and address the on-off switching of fuel cell modules. Further research is needed to replace fixed end-of-life performance definitions with optimization variables and to explicitly model the economic impact of efficiency losses attributable to degradation.

**Why It Matters:** These questions address fundamental limitations in scaling the proposed energy management strategy to realistic, multi-unit maritime power systems and refining the economic models used for degradation.
