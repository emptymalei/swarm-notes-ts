title: Realistic Detector Response Modeling
---

**Background:** Gravitational wave detector sensitivity is often modeled using simplified analytic forms that assume idealized noise responses and overlap reduction functions. Moving beyond these idealized models is essential for accurate future forecasting of stochastic backgrounds.

**Question / Future Work:** Current forecasts rely on effective strain noise models. A more precise treatment incorporating multi-channel Time Delay Interferometry (TDI) response and accurate sky-averaged stochastic gravitational wave background response functions is required to capture the impact of realistic detector geometry and channel noise on parameter estimation.

**Why It Matters:** Idealized sensitivity models provide potentially optimistic bounds; realistic instrument modeling is necessary for reliable, mission-specific performance predictions that account for complex hardware responses.

**Evidence:** A full multi-channel TDI treatment using R(f) and relevant noise channel TDI spectra {SI(f), SII(f)} is left for future refinement.
