title: Capturing Abrupt Policy Changes
---

**Background:** Climate scenarios often include abrupt policy changes, such as delayed transitions, which are essential for modeling realistic socio-economic responses. Current standardized, smooth analytical metrics often struggle to incorporate these non-linear or discontinuous behaviors.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work needs to develop analytical metrics capable of capturing acute shifts and abrupt policy adjustments within climate scenario trajectories. Existing smooth, parametric definitions for decarbonization speed are effective for long-term trends but fail to represent the specific temporal dynamics of sudden mitigation or policy shocks, which are vital features of many modern climate scenario frameworks.

**Why It Matters:** Capturing the timing and severity of policy interventions is essential for accurate risk analysis, particularly in economic modeling where 'shock' events have non-linear consequences on transition pathways.
