title: Coordination Reforms for Scalability
---

**Background:** Resource adequacy (RA) frameworks traditionally use aggregate planning models that operate independently of distribution-level constraints and specific DER participation rules. This lack of integration often leads to either overestimating resource capability or failing to account for local network limitations during system-wide scarcity events.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to determine which specific combinations of cross-gate coordination reforms—such as linking distribution-level hosting capacity studies with wholesale RA accreditation, or synchronizing planning cycles with enforcement—are most effective at scaling DER participation across different market architectures. Identifying these optimal institutional configurations is critical for moving beyond piecemeal, gate-level reforms that currently fail to resolve broader structural barriers.

**Why It Matters:** The authors highlight that existing gate-level reforms have failed to scale DER participation precisely because they do not address cross-stage coupling, making it essential to identify which coordination mechanisms yield the greatest improvements in DER adequacy contributions.
