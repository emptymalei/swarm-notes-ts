title: Validating Generative Social Simulations
---

**Background:** Validation techniques commonly used in classical machine learning are often ineffective for generative social simulations due to the unavailability of ground-truth data for counterfactual policy scenarios and the systemic nature of existing datasets.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a critical, unresolved need to develop rigorous validation frameworks for generative social simulations, particularly those using large language models. Existing methods fail to adequately address the challenge of counterfactuals that never manifest in reality and the inherent biases in historical data used for calibration, necessitating new approaches such as network science or novel methods for interrogating simulation scenarios.

**Why It Matters:** Without robust validation, simulation-informed policy decisions remain susceptible to significant, unpredictable errors, potentially undermining the reliability and credibility of computational models in governance.
