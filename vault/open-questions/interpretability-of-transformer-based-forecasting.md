title: Explainability of Transformer Models
---

**Background:** Deep learning models for time-series forecasting are often criticized for their 'black-box' nature, complicating the understanding of how meteorological variables influence specific predictions.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to implement advanced explainability methods, such as SHAP or integrated gradients, to better interpret how meteorological inputs drive power output predictions in transformer-based architectures. This would provide greater insight into the model's decision-making process beyond mere attention weight visualization.

**Why It Matters:** Model interpretability is essential for building trust and ensuring the reliability of automated grid management systems.

**Evidence:** a deeper understanding of how specific meteorological inputs drive (power output) predictions requires advanced explainability methods (e.g., SHAP, integrated gradients).
