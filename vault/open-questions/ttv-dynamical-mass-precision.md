title: Precise TTV-based Mass Determination
---

**Background:** Transit Timing Variations (TTVs) in multi-planet systems are sensitive to gravitational perturbations from non-transiting companions, but accurately determining their dynamical masses and orbital architectures requires extensive, long-baseline photometric and radial velocity observations.

**Question / Future Work:** The observed TTV super-period in multi-planet systems is often insufficiently sampled, which limits the precision of dynamical mass measurements for non-transiting companions and complicates the confirmation of specific resonant dynamical links. Further long-baseline observations are required to improve characterization of these gravitational interactions.

**Why It Matters:** It is crucial for refining dynamical models of compact multi-planet architectures and for improving planetary mass precision in systems where radial velocity data alone provide weak constraints.
