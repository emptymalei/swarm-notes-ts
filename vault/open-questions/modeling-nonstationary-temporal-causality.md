title: Modeling Nonstationary Temporal Causality
---

**Background:** Temporal causal discovery in complex systems is frequently complicated by nonstationarity, where causal relationships evolve over time due to external forcing or systemic regime shifts.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing causal discovery methodologies that explicitly account for time-varying causal parameters and nonstationary dependency structures remains a significant challenge for long-term predictive reliability in dynamic environments.

**Why It Matters:** Stationary assumptions in causal inference lead to structural bias in systems subject to long-term change, making this a fundamental bottleneck for climate and socioeconomic modeling.
