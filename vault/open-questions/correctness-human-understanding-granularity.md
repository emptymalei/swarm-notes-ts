title: Fine-grained correctness-understanding link
---

**Background:** Computational evaluation metrics like correctness aim to quantify how accurately an explanation reflects the underlying model's reasoning, operating under the assumption that higher scores lead to better human understanding.

**Question / Future Work:** The exact functional form of the relationship between explanation correctness and human understanding remains unresolved, particularly regarding the transition points where performance degrades. Specifically, the comparison between 85% and 100% correctness remained inconclusive, highlighting the need for finer granularity in the 70–100% range, where practical differences between Explainable AI (XAI) methods often occur.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the precise threshold where small functional correctness gains stop yielding human performance improvements is crucial for efficiently prioritizing XAI metric development.

**Evidence:** The unresolved comparison between 85% and 100% correctness highlights the need for finer granularity in the 70–100% range, which is where practical differences between XAI methods often fall.
