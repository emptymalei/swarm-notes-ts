title: Breaking Source Flavor Degeneracy
---

**Background:** High-energy astrophysical neutrinos serve as a unique laboratory to test the Renormalization Group (RG) evolution of neutrino mixing parameters as they evolve with transferred momentum (Q). However, current experimental constraints are broad, and the flavor composition at the source remains a significant unknown nuisance parameter that limits sensitivity.

**Question / Future Work:** It is currently unresolved how to break the statistical degeneracy between high-energy RG-induced mixing modifications and the unknown flavor composition of astrophysical neutrinos at the source. Future work is required to determine if coincident multi-messenger observations can constrain the initial flavor composition at the source, thereby significantly enhancing the sensitivity to high-Q neutrino mixing evolution.

**Why It Matters:** The unknown source composition represents the primary theoretical bottleneck in translating high-energy neutrino flavor measurements into constraints on fundamental physics. Breaking this degeneracy is essential to reaching the required precision to detect potential RG running.

**Evidence:** This profiling is inevitable, since the flavor composition at the sources is inferred from the same observations that are used to constrain the high-Q mixing parameters.
