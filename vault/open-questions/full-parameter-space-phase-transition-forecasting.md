title: Full Parameter Space Forecasting
---

**Background:** The characterization of stochastic gravitational wave backgrounds from cosmological first-order phase transitions using the Fisher information matrix is currently restricted to subsets of the full parameter space, such as fixing the transition temperature and bubble wall velocity. Extending this analysis to include these parameters requires accounting for their role in spectral shape and peak placement.

**Question / Future Work:** Current forecasting models often simplify the parameter space by fixing variables like transition temperature and bubble wall velocity. Extending these forecasts to include these variables is necessary to quantify how they broaden constraint contours and impact parameter degeneracy through their influence on the gravitational wave spectral peak and shape.

**Why It Matters:** Full parameter estimation is essential to determine whether current constraints, derived from fixed-parameter assumptions, are overly optimistic and to understand how these physical variables interact with the primary transition parameters.

**Evidence:** Since both T∗ and vw affect the spectral shape and peak placement it is possible that allowing them to vary could introduce additional degeneracy directions and broaden constraints.
