title: Intermediate network dynamics modeling
---

**Background:** Network changepoint localization methods often rely on whether signal information is contained in marginal or joint distributions of latent vertex positions, which are often not fully captured by existing idealized models.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a critical need to develop intermediate latent position process models and corresponding dissimilarity measures that bridge the gap between models where information is strictly marginal or strictly joint, enabling more robust changepoint detection in complex, non-idealized real-world networks.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing this gap is essential for building network time series models that are both analytically tractable and representative of the complex dynamics observed in empirical data.

**Evidence:** This raises the question of whether we can construct intermediate cases, where the signal is contained in both marginal and joint information, and finding appropriate distances for measuring the dynamics in such cases.
