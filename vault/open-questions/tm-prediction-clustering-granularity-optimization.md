title: Optimizing TM Clustering Granularity
---

**Background:** Traffic matrix (TM) prediction involves forecasting future traffic demands between node pairs, often utilizing clustering to partition heterogeneous flows into smaller, manageable subproblems for independent prediction.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains unclear how to systematically determine the optimal number of clusters and choose the most effective flow representations for varying network scales and non-stationary traffic dynamics. Further research is required to characterize the flow-level predictability that necessitates model specialization and to optimize the balance between computational overhead and predictive accuracy in large-scale network settings.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the fundamental trade-off between model granularity, computational cost, and performance in network traffic engineering, providing a roadmap for developing adaptive prediction frameworks.
