title: Automated Redshift Reliability Metrics
---

**Background:** Automated metrics for assessing spectroscopic redshift quality in the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI) pipeline are optimized for bright, color-selected survey targets rather than for the faint galaxy populations required for future imaging experiments. The discrepancy between these metrics and manual visual inspection (VI) quality flags suggests a need for new, validated automated reliability measures.

**Question / Future Work:** The current reliance on visual inspection (VI) for assessing the reliability of redshift measurements in deep spectroscopic samples is unsustainable for large-scale surveys. Future work is required to develop robust, automated methods for identifying reliable redshifts that are specifically calibrated for faint galaxy populations and compatible with the DESI spectroscopic pipeline, moving beyond simple Δχ² thresholds.

**Why It Matters:** Automated reliability metrics are essential for scaling deep spectroscopic surveys to the sample sizes required for next-generation photometric redshift calibration (e.g., for LSST), as manual inspection is too labor-intensive for the tens of thousands of spectra needed.

**Evidence:** While the DESI main survey uses Δχ2 for determining redshift measurement reliability, we observe a significant scatter between VI quality classifications and Δχ2 values, without any clear threshold that separates reliable redshift measurements.
