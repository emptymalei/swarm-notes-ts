title: Detecting subtle structural damage
---

**Background:** Structural health monitoring often requires distinguishing between subtle damage signatures and significant operational or environmental variability.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of methods to enhance the sensitivity of self-supervised representation learning frameworks to early-stage, low-amplitude structural damage that is currently often masked by signal noise.

**Why It Matters:** Early detection of subtle structural changes is a critical bottleneck for predictive maintenance and long-term structural reliability.
