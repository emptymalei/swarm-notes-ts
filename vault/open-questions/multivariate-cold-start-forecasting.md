title: Multivariate cold-start trajectory forecasting
---

**Background:** Cold-start forecasting involves predicting the full life-cycle of a product when little or no product-specific history exists. While existing models leverage cross-product transfer or static attributes, a unified framework that adapts to evolving information—from pre-launch to early post-launch phases—remains an active area of research.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to extend cold-start forecasting frameworks to multivariate trajectory generation, allowing multiple operationally relevant outcomes to be predicted jointly rather than focusing solely on univariate adoption signals.

**Why It Matters:** Most product launch decisions involve multiple interdependent variables (e.g., sales, inventory, capacity, and marketing impact); developing multivariate models is critical for holistic operational planning.

**Evidence:** A second is to extend the framework to multivariate trajectory generation so that multiple operationally relevant outcomes can be forecast jointly.
