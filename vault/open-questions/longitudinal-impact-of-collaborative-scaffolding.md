title: Longitudinal impact of scaffolding
---

**Background:** Proactive pedagogical scaffolding is designed to improve short-term collaborative task performance, yet the sustained influence of these interventions on long-term cognitive development remains under-researched. Understanding these effects is essential to distinguishing between temporary performance enhancement and durable skill acquisition.

**Question / Future Work:** Longitudinal studies are required to assess whether repeated exposure to AI-driven collaborative scaffolding fosters durable self-regulation and the transfer of metacognitive skills. This research is necessary to verify if interventions improve genuine mastery or merely provide temporary performance crutches.

**Why It Matters:** Assessing long-term impact is critical for determining the educational value of adaptive tutoring interventions versus their potential to mask underlying learner deficiencies.

**Evidence:** Longitudinal studies could clarify whether repeated exposure to proactive collaborative scaffolding supports durable self-regulation and metacognitive awareness.
