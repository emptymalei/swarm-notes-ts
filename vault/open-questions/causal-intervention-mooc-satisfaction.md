title: Causal Intervention in Forecasting
---

**Background:** Automated early-warning systems in education prioritize predictive accuracy, yet mere identification of at-risk students does not quantify the impact of specific interventions on their eventual satisfaction.

**Question / Future Work:** Bridging the gap between predictive modeling and causal inference remains a significant research frontier for ensuring that automated warnings lead to meaningful and efficient educational support.

**Why It Matters:** Moving from predictive to causal models is critical for translating model outputs into actionable and ethically sound interventions in sensitive domains.
