title: Minimax Regret in Unknown-State Forecasting
---

**Background:** Robust forecast aggregation typically relies on known state spaces (e.g., binary {0, 1}), but many real-world environments involve arbitrary or unknown state values.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining tight minimax regret bounds for prior-agnostic aggregation under complex, non-independent information structures and varying expert counts remains an open challenge. The fundamental limits of achieving robustness when the aggregator is ignorant of both the joint information structure and the full prior require further theoretical exploration.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding these limits is critical for building decision-making systems that are truly robust to model misspecification in unpredictable environments.
