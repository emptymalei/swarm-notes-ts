title: Generalizing Meteorological Benchmarks Across Regions
---

**Background:** Meteorological expertise involves applying universal physical laws to specific regional topographies and institutional conventions. Localized models often demonstrate superior performance in their native geographic contexts compared to larger, general-purpose global models.

**Question / Future Work:** Current domain-specific benchmarks are often rooted in a single regional context, such as Korea, which limits their generalizability to other climatic regions. Establishing frameworks for adapting these expert-level diagnostic benchmarks to different geographic and institutional contexts without requiring exhaustive manual re-construction is an unresolved research direction.

**Why It Matters:** Meteorological requirements vary drastically across regions due to unique topographical and climatic factors. A truly robust AI agent must demonstrate performance consistency across diverse global contexts, which requires scalable adaptation strategies.

**Evidence:** the dataset is geo-specifically rooted in the Korean context. Although this design effectively evaluates geo-cultural alignment... it inherently limits direct generalizability to other climatic regions without adaptation.
