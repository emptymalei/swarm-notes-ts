title: Early-Onset Construction Detection
---

**Background:** Offshore wind infrastructure monitoring relies on spatial detection to identify turbine locations before temporal analysis of operational and deployment status can be performed. Currently, there is no reliable method for identifying infrastructure during the earliest phases of construction without prior knowledge of the site's location.

**Question / Future Work:** A critical gap exists in early-onset detection of offshore wind turbine construction. Existing spatial detection methods often fail to identify foundations during the initial installation phase, and there is a trade-off where increasing sensitivity for early detection leads to an unmanageable number of false positives. Future work is needed to develop detection strategies that can reliably identify the very first construction signals without overwhelming downstream analysis.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental bottleneck for near-real-time monitoring, as current workflows depend on pre-existing knowledge of wind farm locations or rely on quarterly aggregations that suppress early transient signals.
