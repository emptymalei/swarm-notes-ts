title: Pathological ground truth validation
---

**Background:** Vineyard disease risk forecasting often relies on management-oriented proxies, which can diverge from actual field-level disease severity and pathogen-specific development. Validating predictive models against objective pathological measurements is essential for closing the gap between management-driven forecasts and true biological risk.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is required to replace management-oriented proxy labels with direct pathological measurements of biological disease development and severity to improve the accuracy and clinical relevance of early warning systems.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the gap between proxy labels and true biological ground truth is critical for moving from experimental models to robust, high-stakes decision support in precision agriculture.

**Evidence:** the target annotations should be interpreted as management-oriented disease-risk proxies derived from environmental monitoring and treatment-related records, rather than direct pathological measurements of biological onset or disease severity.
