title: OECT Drift Mitigation Limits
---

**Background:** Organic electrochemical transistors (OECTs) are increasingly used for chemical signal detection in biological contexts, such as brain organoid interfaces, where baseline drift and common-mode noise are primary performance bottlenecks.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the limits of matched control-referencing under conditions of sensor aging, biochemical degradation, and control-pixel mismatch remains a critical barrier to deploying long-term autonomous molecular communication interfaces.

**Why It Matters:** As bio-integrated devices move from theoretical models to long-term deployment, the inability to account for physical degradation and signal drift limits the robustness of signal decoding.

**Evidence:** Future work can extend the same framework to unequal channel spacing, control mismatch, richer cross-reactivity models, and collaborative experimental validation of tri-channel OECT front-ends.
