title: Autonomous Ensemble Parameter Adaptation
---

**Background:** Ensemble learning methods often struggle with class imbalance, frequently prioritizing majority classes at the expense of underrepresented minority classes. Current approaches often lack systematic, domain-neutral mechanisms for preserving minority signal detection and confidence calibration.

**Question / Future Work:** There remains an unresolved need for fully automated, passive mechanisms for dynamically shifting and adapting ensemble hyperparameters based on the input data distribution, rather than relying on manual, dataset-specific threshold tuning. Future work is required to develop self-optimizing ensemble frameworks capable of maintaining robustness across shifting distributions without human intervention.

**Why It Matters:** Automated hyperparameter adaptation is a critical bottleneck for deploying ensemble methods in dynamic, real-world classification systems where human-in-the-loop tuning is infeasible.
