title: Higher-Dimensional Dark Dimension PBH Viability
---

**Background:** The Dark Dimension scenario, motivated by the Swampland Program, typically predicts extra dimensions correlated with the smallness of the cosmological constant, with research often focusing on the simplest 5D (one extra dimension) case. Recent theoretical extensions consider scenarios with two or more extra dimensions.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains to be investigated how higher-dimensional (e.g., 6D) Dark Dimension scenarios—where cosmological constraints on extra-dimensional models may be significantly stronger—affect the viable mass windows and secondary gravitational wave signatures of primordial black holes. Determining if these scenarios produce distinct phenomenological consequences is essential for discriminating between specific models of quantum gravity and string compactification in future gravitational wave observations.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the phenomenological dependence on the number of extra dimensions is necessary for interpreting potential gravitational wave signals and mapping them back to fundamental theory.
