title: MCMC Integration in Generative Training
---

**Background:** Generative models often rely on iterative processes where distribution shifts and errors from earlier steps accumulate throughout the sequence. Post-hoc correction mechanisms are often required to maintain long-term sequence fidelity.

**Question / Future Work:** Integrating MCMC-based correction mechanisms directly into the training loop of generative models (e.g., as part of the objective function) rather than treating them as post-hoc modules is a significant open problem. Furthermore, determining the scalability and stability of these corrections in high-dimensional diffusion models remains unresolved.

**Why It Matters:** This explores the transition from external, post-hoc correction to end-to-end differentiable regularization, which is critical for long-horizon time-series generation.

**Evidence:** Future work may explore integrating the MCMC mechanism into diffusion models and addressing distribution shift in data generated by diffusion models. Furthermore, we plan to investigate integrating the MCMC framework directly into the training process of generative models.
