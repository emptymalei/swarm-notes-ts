title: Computational Efficiency of Quadratic Rough Heston
---

**Background:** Rough volatility models are non-Markovian frameworks that have demonstrated superior ability to capture refined volatility dynamics compared to traditional stochastic volatility models. While their predictive power for realized volatility is documented, their application remains hampered by high computational complexity due to the lack of closed-form solutions for many model variants.

**Question / Future Work:** The quadratic rough Heston model is capable of resolving significant challenges in joint calibration of S&P 500 and VIX option smiles, but it remains difficult to implement on large-scale datasets due to its computational intensity. Future research is required to optimize computational efficiency, specifically by developing rough volatility-tailored accelerated numerical schemes to enable the use of this more sophisticated model in practical forecasting applications.

**Why It Matters:** The quadratic rough Heston model is a significant advancement in quantitative finance that enables consistent joint calibration of equity and volatility markets. Bridging the gap between its theoretical superiority and its practical computational bottleneck is essential for the next generation of volatility forecasting models.
