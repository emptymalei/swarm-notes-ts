title: Reconstructing Asymmetric Leader-Follower Networks
---

**Background:** Network reconstruction from partial observations in consensus systems often relies on specific symmetry assumptions to resolve mathematical degeneracies in the inferred dynamical matrices. When leaders are not symmetrically coupled to followers, these inference techniques frequently exhibit reduced accuracy.

**Question / Future Work:** The development of methods to reconstruct hidden leader-follower network structures that do not rely on symmetric coupling assumptions remains a substantial challenge. Resolving this would extend the applicability of partial network reconstruction to arbitrary coupling architectures in complex systems.

**Why It Matters:** The requirement for symmetric coupling is a common bottleneck that restricts the application of consensus-based network inference to a wider range of real-world networked systems.
