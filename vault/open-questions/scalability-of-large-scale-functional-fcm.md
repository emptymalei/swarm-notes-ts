title: Scalability of large-scale functional FCMs
---

**Background:** Fuzzy Cognitive Maps (FCMs) are neuro-symbolic models that represent dynamic systems as directed graphs with nodes and edges, typically limited to monotonic causal relationships by static synaptic weights.

**Question / Future Work:** Large-scale systems often feature complex, dense topologies that challenge the interpretability of functional causal maps. Future research is required to evaluate the scalability and clarity of these models when applied to high-dimensional networks where the number of learned functional edges becomes substantial.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding how to maintain both high predictive accuracy and human-understandable interpretations in dense, large-scale networks is a fundamental challenge for the adoption of neuro-symbolic models in real-world scenarios.

**Evidence:** While we acknowledge that analyzing a dense functional adjacency matrix in large-scale systems remains challenging, the KA-FCM provides explicit functional forms for each specific edge.
