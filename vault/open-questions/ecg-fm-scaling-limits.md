title: Scaling Limits of Physiological FMs
---

**Background:** Foundation models for physiological time-series data like electrocardiography (ECG) are typically evaluated using diverse, unstandardized methodologies, complicating the understanding of how architecture and pretraining strategy affect performance at scale.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains unclear whether the observed performance gains in ECG foundation models from current pretraining scales and architecture choices generalize to larger corpora or tasks beyond existing benchmark suites. Future work is required to determine the saturation points for scaling data and the transferability of these representations across broader clinical domains.

**Why It Matters:** Establishing whether performance gains continue beyond current scales is fundamental to defining universal scaling laws for physiological time series foundation models.
