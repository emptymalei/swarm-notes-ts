title: Adaptive Multimodal Time-Series Representation
---

**Background:** Time-series classification methods often convert raw sequences into visual image representations, such as charts, to leverage vision-based models, yet there is a lack of systematic understanding regarding which encoding choices are most effective.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop adaptive techniques that dynamically select optimal visual representations based on input signals and task characteristics, while also determining automated modality selection mechanisms to minimize redundant information in multimodal time-series classification.

**Why It Matters:** Enabling models to automatically optimize for the most discriminative representations is crucial for efficiency and robustness in multimodal TSC applications.

**Evidence:** These insights establish practical design principles and open avenues for future research, including adaptive chart generation, integration with domain-specific visual encodings, and automated modality selection.
