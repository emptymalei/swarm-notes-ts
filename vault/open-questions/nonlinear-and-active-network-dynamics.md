title: Nonlinear and Active Network Dynamics
---

**Background:** Stochastic dynamics of complex networks operating far from equilibrium often result in steady states characterized by broken time-reversal symmetry and persistent probability currents. The current understanding of how these non-equilibrium steady states (NESS) emerge from network connectivity and noise structures, as well as how they map to physical thermodynamic systems, remains incomplete.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to extend the current linearized framework to higher-order nonlinear effects, particularly for systems with multiple coexisting stable fixed points. Additionally, characterizing the thermodynamics of these networks in terms of measurable time-correlation functions and exploring the dynamics of networks under temporally correlated (colored) noise are critical for generalizing the theory beyond the linearized white-noise regime.

**Why It Matters:** These unresolved areas are central to unifying stochastic network theory with non-equilibrium statistical mechanics, which is necessary for applications in biological and synthetic systems where linearity and white noise are poor assumptions.

**Evidence:** Presumably, nonlinear effects in noisy network dynamics can be examined systematically via perturbations... It would be interesting to investigate systems with multiple and distinct steady states... Third, for the interesting case of network dynamics under temporally correlated (colored) noises, a deeper understanding on possible fluctuation-dissipation relations can provide deeper insight...
