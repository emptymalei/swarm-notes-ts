title: Uncertainty-Aware Climate Agentic Reasoning
---

**Background:** Climate agentic frameworks rely on modular tool suites to perform multi-step reasoning and domain-specific analysis, yet they currently face challenges in balancing computational complexity, tool-invocation accuracy, and the propagation of errors from underlying data sources.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop robust, uncertainty-aware, and scenario-driven analytical capabilities for climate decision support, specifically addressing error propagation across multi-step tool chains and the requirement for explicit uncertainty quantification in high-stakes environments.

**Why It Matters:** As climate decision support systems move toward real-world application, the ability to manage cascading tool errors and quantify uncertainty is critical for stakeholder trust and safety.

**Evidence:** Tool failures can also propagate through multi-step traces, even when the language model’s intent is correct. ... our regression suite ... does not exhaustively cover all climate tasks (e.g., full uncertainty quantification, attribution, or long-horizon scenario planning).
