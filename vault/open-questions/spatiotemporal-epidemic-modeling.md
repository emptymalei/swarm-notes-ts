title: Spatiotemporal epidemic forecasting frameworks
---

**Background:** Most current data-driven forecasting benchmarks for infectious diseases primarily focus on modeling temporal dependencies within single regions or across multiple series without explicit consideration of the underlying spatial propagation of pathogens.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to transition from purely temporal multivariate forecasting frameworks to integrated spatiotemporal modeling that can account for disease transmission dynamics across geographical boundaries and the influence of neighboring regions.

**Why It Matters:** Spatiotemporal modeling is essential for accurately capturing the real-world propagation of epidemics between interconnected geographical units.
