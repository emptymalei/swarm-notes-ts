title: Correctness metric equivalence test
---

**Background:** Functional evaluation metrics are often developed differently than human-centered evaluation methods, leading to a gap in understanding if they are measuring the same underlying construct.

**Question / Future Work:** The equivalence between the localized notion of correctness studied (temporal displacement of a contiguous feature region) and what perturbation-based correctness metrics measure remains an open question, as these approaches quantify explanation accuracy differently and their interchangeability is assumed rather than tested.

**Why It Matters:** Resolving this impacts whether findings from human studies based on localization metrics can inform the validity of established, widely used perturbation-based functional metrics.

**Evidence:** Whether our operationalization captures the same property that perturbation-based correctness metrics measure is also an open question. Both aim to quantify explanation accuracy but do so differently, and this equivalence is assumed rather than tested.
