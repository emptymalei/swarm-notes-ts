title: Covariate-dependent Background Intensity
---

**Background:** The proposed model currently treats the background intensity of the Hawkes process as constant, ignoring potential external drivers of extreme event rates. Incorporating temporal covariates into the background intensity could allow for the separation of long-run trends in extreme event rates from short-run, self-exciting clustering dynamics.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work should explore extensions of the model where the background intensity is time-varying or dependent on exogenous covariates. This would provide a more nuanced understanding of the long-term nonstationarity of extreme event occurrence compared to models assuming a constant background intensity.

**Why It Matters:** Distinguishing between long-term drivers and short-term cascades is vital for robust risk assessment in both natural and financial contexts.

**Evidence:** The background intensity μ(t) could be made time-varying or allowed to depend on covariates, allowing long-run changes in the rate of exceedances to be separated from short-run self-excitation.
