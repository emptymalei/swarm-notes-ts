title: Wide binary evolution modeling
---

**Background:** The dynamical evolution of wide binaries in ultra-faint dwarf galaxies is governed by a complex interplay between their initial formation processes and subsequent disruption by the surrounding gravitational potential, including dark matter halos, subhalos, and baryonic structures. Present-day binary populations reflect this combined historical evolution rather than a single physical mechanism.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved need for a self-consistent model that simultaneously accounts for the diverse formation channels of wide binaries and their long-term dynamical disruption by both global dark matter potential and small-scale substructures. Current approaches rely on fixed survival prescriptions which assume simplified formation models, limiting the ability to draw definitive conclusions about the underlying dark matter distribution from observed binary fractions.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical because interpreting wide-binary statistics as a probe of dark matter core/cusp profiles is currently limited by the degeneracy between initial conditions and environmental disruption, preventing a definitive test of dark matter properties.
