title: Causal Identification in Forecasting
---

**Background:** Forecasting benchmarks primarily prioritize outcome-based accuracy, which often obscures the underlying causal mechanisms driving agent performance.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing rigorous methods to causally isolate the relative importance of specific strategic behaviors (e.g., search queries, reasoning techniques) on final forecasting accuracy remains an open challenge. Current metrics often rely on correlations that do not sufficiently distinguish between effective reasoning and lucky outcomes.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the causal drivers of forecasting performance is essential for building robust, reliable reasoning agents rather than optimizing for leaderboard metrics.

**Evidence:** While BTF-2 does enable controlled experiments in reasoning strategies for agents, causally isolating the role of strategic factors is challenging.
