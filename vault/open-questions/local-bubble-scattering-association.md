title: Linking Scintillation to Local Bubble
---

**Background:** Determining whether observed pulsar scintillation screens are physically associated with the shell of the Local Bubble remains challenging due to current uncertainties in both screen distances and the structural model of the bubble.

**Question / Future Work:** Clarifying the connection between scattering screens identified through interstellar scintillation and the boundary of the Local Bubble requires more precise distance measurements and improved mapping of the bubble's surface. Identifying whether observed scintillation is indeed caused by the Local Bubble's shell is essential for understanding the distribution and origin of small-scale electron density turbulence in the local interstellar medium.

**Why It Matters:** This is a fundamental question in interstellar medium research, as it relates to the physical nature of the scattering screens and the properties of the local bubble structure.

**Evidence:** However, recent work by Ocker et al. (2024) mentioned that accurately associating the scattering screens with the Bubble’s boundary remains difficult, due to uncertainties in both the screen distances and the Bubble’s modeled surface. More observations are needed to further provide additional evidence linking scattering screens of pulsars to the Bubble’s boundary.
