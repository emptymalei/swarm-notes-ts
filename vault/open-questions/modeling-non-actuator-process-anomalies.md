title: Modeling Non-Actuator Process Anomalies
---

**Background:** Chemical process monitoring relies on distinguishing between normal operational variations and genuine anomalies, yet current simulation models are largely confined to actuator-based perturbations.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing methods to simulate complex, non-actuator process anomalies—such as foaming agents, sensor noise, or unmodeled physical drifts—remains an unresolved challenge for generating comprehensive training datasets in industrial anomaly detection.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging this modeling gap is critical for creating high-fidelity, comprehensive datasets that enable the training of robust machine learning models capable of detecting diverse, non-linear, and non-actuator-related fault scenarios.

**Evidence:** Anomalies stemming from other perturbations, such as the addition of foaming agents or the corruption of sensor data (e.g., due to noise or drift), could not be simulated, as these effects are not covered by the simulation model.
