title: Robustness to operational forecast uncertainty
---

**Background:** The performance of adaptive sensor placement frameworks trained on synthetic forecast ensembles is dependent on the quality and representativeness of those ensembles relative to actual operational forecasting systems.

**Question / Future Work:** Evaluate the framework using more diverse and higher-fidelity forecast ensembles to ensure that the learned sensor placements remain robust to the specific bias or error patterns encountered in operational settings.

**Why It Matters:** This is critical for bridging the gap between Observing-System Simulation Experiments (OSSEs) and practical deployment in operational oceanography, where the accuracy of the sensor placement strategy is sensitive to the fidelity of forecast uncertainty models.

**Evidence:** The performance of our framework depends on the realism and representativeness of the synthetic ensemble used for training.
