title: Theory-Driven Pattern Analysis
---

**Background:** Current approaches for predicting conflict at subnational levels often rely on fixed spatial and temporal lags, which fail to capture the complex, varying diffusion patterns of violence.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need for robust, theory-driven methods to evaluate and identify specific conflict types or covariates associated with distinct spatio-temporal patterns identified by shape-based models. Investigating which latent conflict mechanisms (e.g., specific conflict types, infrastructure, or socio-economic drivers) correspond to detected 3D diffusion patterns remains an open challenge.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging descriptive pattern recognition with underlying causal drivers is essential for the transition from statistical forecasting to explanatory early-warning intelligence.

**Evidence:** Future work could adopt a more theory-driven approach, using the model to examine patterns associated with specific variables such as conflict type or commonly used covariates in conflict modeling.
