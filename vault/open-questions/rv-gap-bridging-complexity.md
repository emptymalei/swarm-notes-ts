title: Complexity in RV Gap Bridging
---

**Background:** Radial velocity (RV) monitoring is a primary technique for detecting long-period giant exoplanets, yet instrument upgrades or retirements frequently introduce data gaps and zeropoint offsets that complicate long-term signal recovery. The effectiveness of connecting disparate RV datasets across such gaps remains a critical uncertainty in ensuring continued planetary discovery.

**Question / Future Work:** Determine how the inclusion of planets with non-zero eccentricity, the presence of multiple-planet systems, and the implementation of model-selection algorithms affect the enhancement factor observed from bridging observational gaps. Additionally, optimize the placement or number of critical observations required to maximize long-term detection performance across heterogeneous instrument baselines.

**Why It Matters:** This question is technically important because it addresses the scalability of observational gap mitigation from simplified models to complex, realistic orbital architectures in long-term time-series analysis.

**Evidence:** Experiments that relax our single, circular-planet model might yield lower values of Q: Injecting planets with non-zero eccentricity, Injecting multiple planets, Running fits for numbers of planets other than the number of planets injected.
