title: Long-memory effects in 2D turbulence
---

**Background:** In two-dimensional turbulence, the inverse energy cascade can lead to the formation of large-scale coherent structures depending on the damping parameters.

**Question / Future Work:** It is currently unclear if or under what conditions long-memory effects, such as those modeled by fractional Gaussian noise, emerge in two-dimensional turbulent flows, particularly when very large structures dominate the dynamics due to the inverse cascade. Future research is needed to determine the existence and impact of such long-memory processes in these regimes, as they were not significantly observed in the present investigation.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding long-memory effects is crucial for developing accurate stochastic models of turbulence, as current models based on short-memory processes (like filtered white noise) may fail if long-range correlations are present.
