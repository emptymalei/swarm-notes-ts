title: FDR for Correlated Noise
---

**Background:** Network dynamics are often subject to external influences or internal mechanisms that introduce non-Markovian effects through temporally correlated noise. Theoretical frameworks for understanding fluctuation-dissipation relations (FDR) are traditionally rooted in white noise assumptions, which may not capture the full complexity of these active systems.

**Question / Future Work:** A deeper understanding of possible fluctuation-dissipation relations (FDR) is needed for network dynamics under temporally correlated (colored) noise. Establishing these relations could provide essential insights into the behavior of overdamped Brownian systems operating within active, non-Markovian environments.

**Why It Matters:** This extends the scope of stochastic thermodynamics from ideal white-noise models to realistic, active reservoirs where memory effects significantly alter dissipation and flux properties.
