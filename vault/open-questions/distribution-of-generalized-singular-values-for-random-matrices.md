title: Distribution of Generalized Singular Values
---

**Background:** Generalized singular values (GSV) characterize the relationship between two matrices in a way analogous to how singular values characterize a single matrix. Distributions of these values for random matrices remain largely unknown in the literature.

**Question / Future Work:** Establishing the theoretical distribution of generalized singular values (GSVs) for random matrices is a necessary step for developing a closed-form analytical framework for spatiotemporal dependence identification. Current methods frequently rely on computationally expensive permutation tests or empirical null distributions due to the lack of formal theoretical results.

**Why It Matters:** Defining the theoretical limits of GSV for random matrices is a foundational problem that would enable more robust statistical inference in high-dimensional correlation analysis.

**Evidence:** Since no relevant random matrix results are known for the distribution of GSV in the null case, the observed GSV distribution will be compared with the critical values of an empirically obtained (null) distribution of GSVs.
