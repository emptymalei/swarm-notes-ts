title: Breaking degeneracies in wide-binary DM probes
---

**Background:** The interpretation of wide-binary stellar survival as a probe of dark matter density profiles in ultra-faint dwarf galaxies is hindered by the overlap of global potential effects with local stochastic influences from subhalos and stellar cluster histories. These disparate physical processes currently produce degenerate signals in projected spatial distributions.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research must move toward sophisticated forward-modeling frameworks that simultaneously integrate a realistic dark matter subhalo population, the dynamical history of baryonic star clusters, and the complex formation pathways of wide-binary stars. Establishing these multi-physics models is essential to differentiate between competing core-cusp dark matter theories using two-point correlation measurements.

**Why It Matters:** Addressing these degeneracies is fundamental to transforming the wide-binary method from a model-dependent assessment into a robust probe of small-scale dark matter physics.

**Evidence:** Disentangling their respective influences from the imprint of the global DM density profile is a non‑trivial task. It requires forward modeling that simultaneously includes: (i) a realistic distribution of DM subhalos, (ii) a model for the formation, evolution, and dissolution of star clusters in UFDs, and (iii) a treatment of wide binary formation and subsequent dynamical evolution.
