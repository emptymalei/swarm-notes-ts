title: Diagnostic methods for HMM modes
---

**Background:** Hidden Markov models (HMMs) often exhibit genuine multimodality in their posterior distribution, which can complicate the identification of high-density regions and the assignment of uncertainty. The determination of whether these modes reflect substantive information or are computational artifacts is essential for valid inference.

**Question / Future Work:** Robust diagnostic and validation methods are needed to distinguish between substantively meaningful HMM posterior modes and those that arise as computational artifacts.

**Why It Matters:** Distinguishing between genuine and spurious modes is foundational to ensuring that parameter estimates and uncertainty quantifications are valid rather than misleading.

**Evidence:** Model validation is essential to determine whether such multimodality arises as a computational artifact or reflects substantively relevant uncertainty.
