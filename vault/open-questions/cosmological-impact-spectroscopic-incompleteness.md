title: Cosmological Impact of Incompleteness
---

**Background:** Photometric redshift training sets are frequently limited by spectroscopic measurement failures, which are not distributed randomly but rather correlate with galaxy properties such as color and redshift, potentially biasing cosmological parameter estimates if not accounted for. Forecasts of the cosmological impact of these failures often rely on extreme assumptions about the distribution of failures, such as assuming they are entirely random or entirely concentrated at high redshifts.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is required to fully characterize the complex and non-linear effects of spectroscopic redshift measurement failures on cosmological parameter constraints. This involves understanding the bias-inducing nature of non-random redshift failures and developing more nuanced frameworks to account for these failures in cosmological forecasting for future imaging-based dark energy experiments.

**Why It Matters:** A better understanding of how spectroscopic incompleteness maps into cosmological biases is essential for designing efficient training sets that maximize the constraining power of upcoming surveys like LSST.
