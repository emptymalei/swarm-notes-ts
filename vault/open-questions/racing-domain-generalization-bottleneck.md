title: Autonomous Racing Domain Generalization
---

**Background:** High-speed autonomous racing imposes perception and prediction constraints characterized by extreme relative velocities and sparse, non-urban environmental contexts. Perception models trained on conventional autonomous driving data often fail to translate to these high-dynamic regimes.

**Question / Future Work:** The challenge lies in quantifying and mitigating domain shift between simulation, urban-driving, and racing environments. Future research must determine whether multi-task joint modeling of detection and prediction can overcome data scarcity and leverage motion-distribution coverage to ensure robustness under high-speed dynamics.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the sim-to-real gap is the primary bottleneck for deploying autonomous agents in high-velocity, high-uncertainty scenarios where safety and reaction time are critical.
