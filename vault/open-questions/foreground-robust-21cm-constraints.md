title: Foreground-robust 21-cm constraints
---

**Background:** The impact of foreground contamination is a primary challenge for 21-cm intensity mapping, significantly reducing the signal-to-noise ratio of cosmological measurements.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to incorporate realistic models of astrophysical and terrestrial foregrounds into the Fisher forecast analysis for 21-cm auto-power and cross-power spectra. Existing assessments often rely on optimistic, foreground-free assumptions; a comprehensive study is necessary to quantify how foreground removal techniques affect the constraining power of upcoming radio facilities like SKA1-Mid and PUMA.

**Why It Matters:** Foreground contamination is the dominant limiting factor for 21-cm surveys; robustly incorporating it is critical for validating the feasibility of using these observables to constrain PMFs.

**Evidence:** As already highlighted, the presence of strong foregrounds in the 21 cm sector has been neglected in this study, which must be taken into account for a more realistic analysis of the 21 cm auto-spectrum and its capacity of providing constraints on the PMF sector.
