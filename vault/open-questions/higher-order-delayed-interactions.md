title: Higher-order delayed interactions
---

**Background:** Many real-world systems, such as biological networks and climate models, exhibit interactions characterized by memory effects, non-local dependencies, or non-instantaneous connections.

**Question / Future Work:** Future developments are needed to adapt symbolic discovery frameworks to handle higher-order, delayed, or non-local interactions, potentially through the use of hypergraph representations or delay-embedding techniques.

**Why It Matters:** Standard network frameworks often struggle with systems where dynamics depend on past states or non-adjacent nodes; addressing this is essential for predictive accuracy in complex, real-world phenomena.

**Evidence:** Second, higher-order, delayed, or nonlocal interactions common in biology and climate may require hypergraph or delay-embedded extensions.
