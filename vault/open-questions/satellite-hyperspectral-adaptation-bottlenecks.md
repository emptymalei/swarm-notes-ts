title: Satellite-based Hyperspectral Retrieval Adaptation
---

**Background:** The transition from in situ hyperspectral reflectance measurements to satellite-based remote sensing is necessary for wide-area aquatic monitoring, but it introduces complex sources of error such as atmospheric interference and sensor-specific spectral responses.

**Question / Future Work:** Integrating current physics-aware BGC retrieval frameworks with satellite-based remote sensing requires addressing significant hurdles, including atmospheric correction inaccuracies, adjacency effects in complex water bodies, and the distinct spectral response functions of sensors. Dedicated benchmarking and adaptation are required to maintain retrieval performance under these observational conditions.

**Why It Matters:** Developing accurate satellite-based BGC retrieval is the fundamental bottleneck to moving from local, point-based observations to regional and global-scale, continuous water quality monitoring.
