title: Volatility Forecast Regime Dependence
---

**Background:** The predictive power of monetary policy signals from prediction markets for Bitcoin volatility has demonstrated significant regime dependence tied to specific economic cycles, such as the 2024–2025 rate-cutting phase. Establishing whether these signals provide persistent value across diverse macroeconomic regimes remains a necessary step for evaluating their long-term utility in financial modeling.

**Question / Future Work:** The effectiveness of using prediction market-derived monetary policy signals to forecast cryptocurrency volatility appears contingent on the specific monetary policy environment. It is currently unknown if the observed predictive relationship is a stable, structural feature of cryptocurrency markets or if it is transient, manifesting only during periods of active or specific types of policy shifts. Longer-term data spanning multiple tightening and easing cycles are required to characterize this dependence.

**Why It Matters:** If a predictive signal is only valid in specific regimes, its utility for general-purpose risk management or options pricing is limited. Determining the scope and stability of these regimes is essential for practitioners.
