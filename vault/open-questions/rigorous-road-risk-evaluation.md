title: Rigorous Road-Risk Model Evaluation
---

**Background:** Road-segment incident forecasting often evaluates models using holdout sets from the same spatial and temporal distribution, which fails to account for the persistence of historical patterns.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing and implementing rigorous evaluation protocols for road-segment risk models—including geographic holdouts, cold-segment holdouts, and prospective roll-forward tests against unseen live data—is essential to quantify true deployment validity.

**Why It Matters:** Standard metrics in this domain often conflate predictive power with historical pattern persistence, leading to over-optimistic performance estimates.

**Evidence:** The correct next step ... [is] geographic holdouts, cold-segment holdouts, source holdouts, and prospective roll-forward tests against future live data.
