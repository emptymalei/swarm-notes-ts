title: Multi-product capacity-constrained bullwhip analysis
---

**Background:** The bullwhip effect is characterized by amplified demand variability as it propagates upstream in a supply chain, traditionally analyzed under simplified assumptions such as stationarity, single-echelon isolation, and known distributions. Real-world supply chains exhibit complex dynamics such as structural breaks, regime-switching demand, and heterogeneous lead times that remain inadequately captured by current analytical models.

**Question / Future Work:** The extension of bullwhip simulation models to account for multi-product demand with cross-product substitution and endogenous capacity constraints (e.g., the rationing game) remains a significant bottleneck. Future work is needed to generalize current demand generators and simulation engines to handle multivariate time series and resource-constrained supply chain modeling.

**Why It Matters:** These factors are critical for accurately modeling high-capacity, multi-product industrial supply chains where resource competition and substitution dynamics significantly influence bullwhip amplification.
