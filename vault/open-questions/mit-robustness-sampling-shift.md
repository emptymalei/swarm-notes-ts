title: Robustness to sampling distribution shift
---

**Background:** Multimodal irregular time series (MITS) data, such as longitudinal EHR, is heavily dependent on observational policies that dictate sampling patterns. Models trained on this data often face performance degradation when the underlying data collection protocols change.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is needed to evaluate the robustness of LLM-based MITS models when the sampling distribution shifts, such as when deploying models trained on one clinical environment to another with different diagnostic or documentation policies.

**Why It Matters:** Ensuring robustness against sampling shifts is essential for the reliability and generalizability of predictive models in high-stakes clinical settings.

**Evidence:** Future work includes... studying robustness under sampling distribution shift, such as deployment across hospitals with different sampling policies.
