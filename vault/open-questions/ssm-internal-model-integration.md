title: Integrating Internal Model Principles
---

**Background:** Internal model principles improve control robustness against persistent disturbances by incorporating an internal representation of the disturbance within the controller. While currently applied in various control frameworks, integrating these principles into structured state-space model (SSM) based control remains an unaddressed technical challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Future efforts should focus on integrating the internal model principle into the established contraction-based framework for structured state-space models (SSMs) to improve robustness and performance against external disturbances. Current controller synthesis for SSMs provides exponential stability, but incorporating internal models is necessary to achieve better tracking or rejection of persistent disturbance signals.

**Why It Matters:** The internal model principle is critical for achieving robust tracking and disturbance rejection in practical control applications, representing a natural progression for this data-driven control framework.
