title: Causal identification of reflexivity
---

**Background:** The causal impact of public prediction market signals on real-world outcomes is difficult to isolate from other observational data. Current empirical approaches lack the granular, structural identification strategies necessary to quantify this reflexive feedback mechanism.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a critical need for structural identification strategies or natural experiments that can disentangle the marginal contribution of prediction market prices from broader informational environments. Specifically, determining whether market-driven outcomes are self-fulfilling or self-defeating requires counterfactual frameworks that are currently unsupported by existing observational data.

**Why It Matters:** This is fundamental to validating the reflexivity thesis; without isolating the causal direction, it is impossible to distinguish between market accuracy, self-fulfilling prophecies, and self-defeating dynamics.

**Evidence:** Disentangling the marginal contribution of prediction market prices from this broader informational environment requires either natural experiments ... or structural identification strategies that are not yet available in the existing literature.
