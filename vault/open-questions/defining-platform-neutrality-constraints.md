title: Defining platform neutrality constraints
---

**Background:** Platforms often face neutrality and non-discrimination mandates that limit their ability to explicitly favor certain sellers when routing demand. Demand allocation algorithms that adhere to these constraints while influencing inventory remain a significant design challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Further research is needed to define neutrality in richer ways. Current definitions focus on equal long-run mean demand shares and equal one-step-ahead forecast uncertainty (root MSFE), but alternative constraints—such as those involving multi-step forecast errors, preservation of spectral densities, or exposure floors—warrant exploration to determine their impact on the implementable set of allocation policies and platform optimal design.

**Why It Matters:** Defining neutrality is a foundational component of marketplace governance, and varying the definition changes the feasible space for platform optimization. Expanding these definitions is critical for applying the model to practical, regulated marketplace environments.
