title: Integrating heterogeneous cyber threat streams
---

**Background:** Vulnerability sightings exhibit sparse and bursty patterns that challenge traditional time-series forecasting, often rendering conventional statistical approaches like SARIMAX inadequate due to limited observational history and high event volatility.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to develop methods for integrating heterogeneous cyber threat intelligence streams (such as varying sighting categories like 'confirmed' vs. 'exploited') with automatically generated severity scores. Research is required to determine how to effectively weight these distinct information signals to differentiate between mere public attention and imminent exploit weaponization.

**Why It Matters:** Improving the granularity of vulnerability forecasting is essential for operational security, as different types of sighting activity imply vastly different risk profiles for defenders.
