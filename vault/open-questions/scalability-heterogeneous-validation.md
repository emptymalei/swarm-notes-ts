title: Digital Twin Scalability Validation
---

**Background:** Digital twin architectures are increasingly utilized for optimizing data center energy efficiency, yet their deployment in complex, large-scale, or heterogeneous environments remains underexplored compared to constrained testbed scenarios.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is needed to validate the effectiveness and scalability of digital twin frameworks beyond controlled, small-scale, or partially simulated environments. This includes testing in large-scale, heterogeneous data center infrastructures to ensure that the performance gains in energy efficiency, predictive accuracy, and PUE observed in isolated settings are reproducible and effective in real-world, complex operational conditions.

**Why It Matters:** Validation in large-scale, heterogeneous settings is critical to determine the practical viability and generalization of digital twin-based energy optimization beyond academic or small-scale testbeds.

**Evidence:** The experiments were conducted in a controlled environment with partial reliance on simulated data, which may affect the generalization of the findings to large-scale or highly heterogeneous data center environments.
