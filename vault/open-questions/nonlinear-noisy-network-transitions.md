title: Nonlinear Noisy Network Transitions
---

**Background:** Many complex dynamical networks operate far from equilibrium, where broken time-reversal symmetry and persistent probability currents characterize the non-equilibrium steady state (NESS). While the linearized fluctuations around stable fixed points are well-understood, the behavior of these systems remains complex due to the interplay of network topology, non-linear dynamics, and heterogeneous noise sources.

**Question / Future Work:** It remains to be systematically investigated how higher-order nonlinear effects, which go beyond the current linearized framework, influence the steady-state distribution and stability of noisy network dynamics. Specifically, the emergence of multiple coexisting noise-free stable fixed points and the resulting non-Gaussian behavior in the steady-state distribution, as well as the potential for non-equilibrium phase transitions between these states, require further exploration.

**Why It Matters:** This addresses the limitations of the current linearization and bridges the gap to understanding more complex, real-world network behaviors where nonlinearities are dominant and transitions between states are critical.
