title: Refining poromechanical constitutive relationships
---

**Background:** Poromechanical models represent glioma growth as a multiphase continuum, capturing complex interactions between tumor cell proliferation, tissue deformation, and interstitial fluid dynamics. However, the phenomenological relationships used to define the coupling, such as the pressure-saturation law, often lead to unrealistically high pressures at high saturation levels.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to refine the constitutive relationships, specifically the pressure-saturation law, to avoid non-physical, excessive solid pressure predictions in the tumor core. Future research should prioritize conducting experimental mechanical characterization of specific glioma cell lines to derive more accurate, physiologically consistent relationships.

**Why It Matters:** Solid pressure is a key factor in inhibiting tumor growth and predicting treatment response; ensuring this variable is accurately estimated is essential for model reliability.

**Evidence:** First, the peak pressures obtained in our study point to a potential limitation in the formulation of the pressure saturation law. Since the solid pressure is linked to saturation through a phenomenological relationship using a tangent function (see Eq. 15), high saturation values may produce unrealistically large peak pressures.
