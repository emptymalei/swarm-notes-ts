title: Calibrated Longitudinal Clinical Risk Prediction
---

**Background:** Medical diagnosis remains challenging due to the need to balance longitudinal clinical evidence with probabilistic reasoning under data uncertainty.

**Question / Future Work:** Developing more robust methodologies for calibrated clinical risk prediction in the absence of reliable population priors remains a significant bottleneck. This includes integrating prevalence-aware modeling, evidence governance at the knowledge base scale, and rigorous study of human-AI interaction latency and failure modes.

**Why It Matters:** This is crucial for transitioning research-grade clinical decision support agents into verified, trustworthy bedside tools that can safely handle the complexities of patient-specific longitudinal data.
