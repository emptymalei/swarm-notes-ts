title: SDN Controller Placement Trade-offs
---

**Background:** The integration of mega-constellations into global networks necessitates centralized control, but the physical placement of controllers introduces trade-offs between global network visibility and computational constraints.

**Question / Future Work:** Determining the optimal deployment strategy for SDN controllers in large-scale space networks—whether ground-based, space-borne, or hybrid—remains a fundamental challenge in balancing orchestration efficacy with Size, Weight, and Power (SWaP) constraints.

**Why It Matters:** Effective controller placement is critical for managing the high-latency and dynamic nature of large-scale LEO networks while ensuring consistent and stable network operations.

**Evidence:** The physical placement of the central SDSN controller presents a fundamental trade-off between global visibility and computational capability.
