title: Calibrating SIDM-baryon coupling efficiency
---

**Background:** Self-interacting dark matter (SIDM) models are constrained by their impacts on high-redshift galaxy formation, yet these impacts are often degenerate with uncertainties in astrophysical feedback parameters. Simulations currently lack consistent calibration for the coupling efficiency between SIDM potential modifications and the interstellar medium at high redshifts.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an explicit need for dedicated, high-redshift hydrodynamical simulations that incorporate radiative transfer to calibrate the coupling efficiency parameter η, which governs the interaction between the dark matter potential and the interstellar medium. Calibrating this parameter is essential for making precise predictions about the impact of SIDM on galaxy formation at early cosmic epochs.

**Why It Matters:** The parameter η is the dominant theoretical uncertainty in determining whether SIDM can be constrained by existing or future observations.
