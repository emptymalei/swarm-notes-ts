title: Isolating error sources in wearable ECGs
---

**Background:** Data quality assessment for wearable electrocardiogram (ECG) devices is essential for their adoption in clinical decision-making. However, distinguishing between various factors influencing measurement accuracy—such as intrinsic device errors, calibration issues, signal processing artifacts, and sensor placement—remains a significant analytical challenge.

**Question / Future Work:** Future research is required to isolate and quantify the individual contributions of different error sources (e.g., intrinsic device limitations, calibration inaccuracies, peak detection errors, and improper sensor placement) to the overall quality of ECG data collected in non-clinical, unmonitored environments. Identifying these specific error components is critical for improving the reliability of wearable sensors for medical applications.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the specific origins of data noise and inaccuracy in wearable ECGs is necessary to develop targeted mitigation strategies, move beyond empirical validation, and establish standardized quality-control protocols required for clinical integration.

**Evidence:** Furthermore, we aim to differentiate the various potential sources of error (e.g., intrinsic device inaccuracies, calibration error, peak detection errors, placement errors).
