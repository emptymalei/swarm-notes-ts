title: Architecting Domain-Specialized Financial Agents
---

**Background:** Professional equity research relies on multi-stage workflows involving complex data retrieval, qualitative synthesis, and rigorous quantitative modeling. Current AI systems are typically optimized for single-pass tasks rather than these nuanced, iterative professional financial workflows.

**Question / Future Work:** Future development of deep research agents for finance needs to address the architectural gap between generic search-augmented LLMs and the multi-stage, domain-specific requirements of equity research. Specifically, agents require improved planning, sub-task decomposition, and reliable synthesis capabilities to move beyond template-filling and provide differentiated, professional-grade insights.

**Why It Matters:** The paper demonstrates that while current AI agents can structure reports, they fail to provide the analytical depth, causal reasoning, and robust scenario analysis characteristic of professional analyst work. Establishing a roadmap for domain-specialized agent architecture is critical for moving towards decision-relevant AI in finance.
