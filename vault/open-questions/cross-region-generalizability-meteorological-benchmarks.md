title: Cross-Region Generalizability of Benchmarks
---

**Background:** Localized AI applications require models to be grounded in specific geographic, climatological, and regulatory contexts. Current benchmarks frequently rely on universal or global data, which may fail to measure a model's ability to adapt to region-specific meteorological realities.

**Question / Future Work:** It is unclear how evaluation benchmarks grounded in specific geo-cultural contexts can be adapted or scaled to diverse climatic regions. Research is needed to develop methodologies that extend these evaluation frameworks without requiring extensive manual reconstruction of domain-specific data.

**Why It Matters:** Meteorological AI requires global utility, but geo-cultural sensitivities create significant barriers to zero-shot deployment; tracking benchmark generalizability is key to scalable, domain-aware AI.

**Evidence:** the dataset is geo-specifically rooted in the Korean context... it inherently limits direct generalizability to other climatic regions without adaptation.
