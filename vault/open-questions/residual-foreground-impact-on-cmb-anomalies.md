title: Foreground Residuals in Large-Scale Anomalies
---

**Background:** Component separation methods are essential for removing Galactic foregrounds, yet residual contamination can introduce biases in large-scale cosmic microwave background statistical estimators.

**Question / Future Work:** Systematic assessment is required to determine how different or more complex foreground models influence the sensitivity and potential biases of anomaly estimators on the largest angular scales, particularly given the reliance on component separation methods like NILC.

**Why It Matters:** Large-scale anomalies reside at the lowest multipoles where both cosmic variance and residual foregrounds are most significant; failing to model these residuals can lead to false claims of anomaly detection.

**Evidence:** We defer to future work the consideration of residual foreground spectra arising from more complex foreground models.
