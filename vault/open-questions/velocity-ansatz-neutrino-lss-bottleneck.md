title: Velocity Ansatz Neutrino LSS Bottleneck
---

**Background:** The Effective Field Theory of Large-Scale Structure (EFTofLSS) provides a framework for modeling galaxy clustering in redshift space by introducing counterterms that capture the impact of non-linear small-scale physics on large-scale observables. While the current formalism assumes a specific ansatz for the galaxy velocity field relative to the cold dark matter and baryon fluid, the consistency and accuracy of this assumption in the presence of massive neutrinos remain to be rigorously validated.

**Question / Future Work:** A self-consistent theoretical derivation of the ansatz equating the galaxy velocity field to the CDM+baryon field, specifically in the presence of massive neutrinos, is currently lacking. Future work is required to investigate potential deviations from this approximation to ensure the robustness of the EFTofLSS predictions for forthcoming high-precision galaxy surveys.

**Why It Matters:** This is a critical theoretical bottleneck for future stage IV galaxy surveys, as errors in modeling the velocity field directly propagate into systematic biases in cosmological parameter inference, including neutrino mass measurements. Validating this assumption is essential for maintaining the sub-percent precision expected from the Roman Space Telescope and similar experiments.

**Evidence:** While a self consistent theoretical derivation of this result is still the subject of ongoing study, we adopt this ansatz in all that follows. We plan to investigate possible deviations from this approximation in a forthcoming work.
