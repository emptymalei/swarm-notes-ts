title: Drivers of glioma growth directionality
---

**Background:** Mathematical models of glioma growth often incorporate tissue vascularization, estimated from contrast-enhanced MRI, to inform mass transfer or growth dynamics. In many cases, it is hypothesized that tumors preferentially expand into more highly vascularized regions.

**Question / Future Work:** The assumption that tumor growth direction is primarily governed by vascular supply, as inferred from contrast-enhanced MRI, requires further validation. It is currently unresolved whether other factors such as tissue permeability or tumor cell viscosity dominate growth trajectories in poorly vascularized regions, suggesting a need for more robust, data-informed growth laws.

**Why It Matters:** Correctly identifying the primary drivers of tumor spatial progression is necessary for reliable spatiotemporal forecasting and treatment planning.

**Evidence:** Future studies should investigate whether and how other parameters might be responsible for the tumor growth directions, such as permeability and tumor dynamic viscosity.
