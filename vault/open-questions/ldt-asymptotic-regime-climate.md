title: LDT Asymptotic Regime Validation
---

**Background:** Large deviation theory (LDT) characterizes the probabilities of rare events in the tails of distributions, but its standard application assumes specific mixing properties and asymptotic regimes. Climate datasets often exhibit long-range correlations and memory effects that challenge these foundational assumptions.

**Question / Future Work:** Investigate the validity of LDT-based return time estimations in the presence of long-range dependencies and non-stationary signals common in climate time series. This involves defining formal criteria for confirming that the asymptotic regime has been reached in empirical data.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the breakdown of LDT assumptions in real-world climate data is crucial for preventing overconfident predictions of extreme events.

**Evidence:** In systems with strong memory or long-range correlations, anomalous large-deviation scalings with powers different from T may occur. ... In applications, however, whether the asymptotic regime has been reached must still be checked empirically.
