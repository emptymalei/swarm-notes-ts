title: Failure-tail forecastability scaling
---

**Background:** Safety assessment frequently employs extreme value theory to extrapolate failure rates from small evaluation sets to larger deployment populations. The validity of these safety forecasts relies on the failure score tail distribution being predictable and well-aligned with observed samples.

**Question / Future Work:** It is currently unresolved how the tail shape of model failure rates scales with model capacity, architecture, and training data diversity. Understanding these relationships is critical for ensuring that safety forecasting remains reliable as models increase in complexity.

**Why It Matters:** This question is central to the future scalability and validity of current model safety assessment methodologies.

**Evidence:** ...how failure-tail shape depends on model size, training data, and post-training procedure is open.
