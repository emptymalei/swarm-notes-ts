title: Benchmarking Clinical EEG Utility
---

**Background:** EEG foundation models are frequently evaluated using standard machine learning metrics that fail to capture the clinical nuances of neurological conditions.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an open need to standardize benchmarks that utilize clinically grounded, task-specific evaluation measures—such as event-level decision-making quality, hypnogram-derived sleep features, and brain-age gap biomarkers—to accurately assess model utility for real-world neurological applications.

**Why It Matters:** Distinguishing between technical performance and clinical utility is a major bottleneck in adopting foundation models for neurology.

**Evidence:** Standard machine learning metrics are insufficient to assess clinical utility: thus, we thoroughly evaluate more appropriate measures such as the quality of event-level decision-making, hypnogram-derived features, and the brain-age gap.
