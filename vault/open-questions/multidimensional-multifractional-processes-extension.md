title: Multidimensional Multifractional Processes Extension
---

**Background:** Multifractional stochastic processes, which generalize classical Brownian motion by allowing the Hurst parameter to vary over time, are increasingly applied in modeling non-stationary real-world data such as financial volatility and network traffic. Extending these models to multi-dimensional settings could significantly enhance their utility in fields like geostatistics and spatial data analysis.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to generalize the current univariate Gaussian Haar-based multifractional process framework to multidimensional cases to facilitate the simulation and statistical analysis of random fields.

**Why It Matters:** Expanding from 1D time series to 2D/3D random fields is a critical bottleneck for applying multifractional models to spatial, image, or terrain data.

**Evidence:** Generalizing the developed approach to multidimensional cases could enable similar simulations and analysis for random fields and spatial data, see [15].
