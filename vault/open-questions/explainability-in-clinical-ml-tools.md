title: Explainability in Clinical ML
---

**Background:** Machine learning models for medical applications often operate as "black boxes," which significantly limits their clinical interpretability and hampers physician trust. Providing justifications for model outputs is a recognized necessity for integrating AI into routine medical practice.

**Question / Future Work:** Future development of machine learning toolkits for healthcare requires the integration of Explainable AI (XAI) modules. These modules should translate complex model decision processes into transparent, clinical justifications that clinicians can interpret and validate, thereby bridging the current gap in trust and accountability.

**Why It Matters:** Explainability is a prerequisite for the ethical and practical deployment of AI in high-stakes clinical decision support. Bridging this gap is essential for moving research models into real-world bedside adoption.

**Evidence:** In future work, we plan to introduce explainable AI modules to provide clinical justifications for model decisions, thereby increasing physician trust in these systems.
