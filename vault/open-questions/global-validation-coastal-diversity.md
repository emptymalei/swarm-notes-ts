title: Global Validation of Coastal Diversity
---

**Background:** The hyperspectral retrieval of biogeochemical parameters in aquatic environments is hampered by limited data transferability across regions due to diverse and variable bio-optical properties. Existing methods often fail to generalize when applied to optically distinct waters, necessitating region-specific calibration that is resource-intensive and often infeasible due to data scarcity.

**Question / Future Work:** There is an unresolved need to validate the transferability and robustness of physics-aware meta-learning frameworks across a more diverse spectrum of global aquatic optical regimes beyond limited local datasets to establish universal applicability.

**Why It Matters:** Ensuring global generalizability is the central requirement for these frameworks to move from site-specific tools to robust environmental monitoring systems.

**Evidence:** The current evaluation is limited to five sites in Australian coastal waters. While these sites span distinct coastal and estuarine regimes, they do not fully represent the broader range of optical water types observed across global coastal environments.
