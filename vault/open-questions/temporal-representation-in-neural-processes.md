title: Temporal representation in Neural Processes
---

**Background:** Neural processes combine the flexibility of neural networks with the uncertainty estimation of Gaussian processes to map context sets to target sets. Despite their potential in multimodal action prediction, their ability to capture and generalize complex temporal dependencies remains a critical research barrier.

**Question / Future Work:** The fundamental challenge lies in designing temporal inductive biases for Neural Processes that allow them to generalize to unseen action sequences, move beyond simple autoregressive limitations, and handle high-dimensional, multimodal robotic sensory data. Current architectures struggle with representing temporal dynamics effectively, necessitating new approaches to temporal feature integration.

**Why It Matters:** Without robust temporal representation, Neural Processes cannot reliably predict future states or generalize to new sequences in dynamic, real-world robotic environments.
