title: ERB Modeling and Fluctuations
---

**Background:** Extragalactic Radio Background (ERB) models are currently constructed using empirical extrapolations of galaxy star formation rates and stellar-to-halo mass relations, which introduce significant uncertainties. The potential impact of intrinsic spatial fluctuations in the ERB on cosmological signal analysis remains a critical area for improved theoretical modeling.

**Question / Future Work:** The current framework for modeling the extragalactic radio background (ERB) relies on bottom-up integrations of galaxy properties that lack high-precision observational constraints, particularly at higher redshifts. Future work needs to quantify how anisotropies and fluctuations in the ERB could impact the precision of cross-correlation signals in upcoming radio surveys.

**Why It Matters:** Uncertainties in the ERB modeling directly affect the sensitivity projections for axion-like particle (ALP) dark matter detection, as these backgrounds act as both signal enhancers (via stimulated decay) and potential sources of systematic noise.

**Evidence:** The fidelity of our ERB modeling can be further improved by incorporating more sophisticated galaxy evolution prescriptions and high-redshift radio luminosity functions.
