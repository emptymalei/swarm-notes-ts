title: Empirical calibration of participation-response probabilities
---

**Background:** The effectiveness of appointment-based resource systems relies on accurately modeling the probability that a solicited participant will follow through with attendance. Currently, many logistical planning models rely on proxy estimates rather than empirical response rates derived from actual outreach data.

**Question / Future Work:** Determine methodologies to empirically calibrate participation-response probabilities across diverse demographic segments and solicitation channels, specifically addressing how these probabilities shift over time and in response to cumulative solicitation fatigue.

**Why It Matters:** This is a core bottleneck for matching logistical supply to capacity; inaccurate probabilities lead to either systematic resource wastage or significant gaps in demand fulfillment.
