title: Negotiation-Aware Threshold Secret Sharing
---

**Background:** Threshold Secret Sharing (TSS) is used to distribute execution authorization for privacy-preserving data exchanges among multiple independent entities.

**Question / Future Work:** Current implementations of threshold-based authorization rely on generic schemes that are independent of the underlying negotiation logic. A negotiation-aware TSS mechanism would integrate policy constraints directly into the secret reconstruction process to enhance the security and integrity of data release authorizations.

**Why It Matters:** Directly addresses the integration of privacy policy negotiation with the cryptographic primitives that enforce secure data release in decentralized systems.

**Evidence:** Designing a next-generation, negotiation-aware TSS that embeds policy constraints directly into the reconstruction process is left for future work.
