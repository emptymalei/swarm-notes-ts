title: Reducing CME Forecast Uncertainty
---

**Background:** Coronal Mass Ejections (CMEs) propagate from the solar corona through the heliosphere to 1 AU, where their geoeffective impact is governed by complex, nonlinear solar-wind interactions.

**Question / Future Work:** There is a need to refine the observational constraints for flux-rope model input parameters—such as magnetic configuration and poloidal-flux injection—and improve the physical representation of internal magnetic and plasma evolution to achieve more precise arrival and impact forecasting.

**Why It Matters:** Bridging the gap between semi-analytic model inputs and high-fidelity observational constraints is a prerequisite for improving the reliability of operational space weather forecasting.
