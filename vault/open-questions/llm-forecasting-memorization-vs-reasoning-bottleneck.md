title: LLM forecasting memorization bottleneck
---

**Background:** Large language models frequently exhibit inflated performance on forecasting tasks due to exposure to resolved event information during pretraining.

**Question / Future Work:** Distinguishing between a model's true prospective reasoning capability and its memorized factual knowledge remains a fundamental challenge in evaluating agentic forecasting performance, requiring new paradigms beyond simple knowledge cutoffs.

**Why It Matters:** Understanding the limits of an LLM's true forecasting ability is essential for deploying them in high-stakes decision-support environments where factual memorization can lead to deceptive accuracy.
