title: Disentangling SIDM from bursty star-formation
---

**Background:** Bursty star formation histories are common in high-redshift galaxies, leading to significant variations in star formation rates over short timescales. Current models face challenges in distinguishing these astrophysical stochastic effects from signatures of non-standard dark matter models.

**Question / Future Work:** Future work is required to develop techniques, such as utilizing the 21 cm bispectrum or more detailed galaxy clustering statistics, to separate the signature of SIDM-induced suppression from the effects of stochastic, bursty star formation histories in high-redshift galaxies.

**Why It Matters:** As observational data reveals increasingly complex star formation histories, the ability to isolate dark matter effects from baryonic physics is a critical bottleneck in precision cosmology.
