title: Modeling Discrepancies in HI Surveys
---

**Background:** The use of neutral hydrogen (HI) emission lines for large-scale cosmological surveys, particularly with upcoming arrays like the SKA-MID, requires accurate predictions of galaxy number counts and clustering properties, which currently exhibit significant discrepancies across different simulation models.

**Question / Future Work:** The substantial disagreement in predicted number counts and bias between current HI galaxy simulation methods (such as semi-analytic models vs. hydrodynamical simulations) limits the robustness of cosmological forecasts, especially at higher redshifts (z > 0.5) and lower flux density thresholds where simulation-specific predictions can vary by several orders of magnitude. Further investigation is needed to reconcile these models and quantify the systematic uncertainties inherent in simulating the HI content within cosmic structures.

**Why It Matters:** Accurate predictions for HI galaxy distributions are critical for interpreting data from next-generation radio surveys; without resolving these modeling discrepancies, the reliability of inferred cosmological constraints, such as the expansion rate and growth factor, remains compromised by unquantified theoretical systematic errors.
