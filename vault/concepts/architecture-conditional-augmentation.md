# Architecture-Conditional Augmentation

> *Auto-generated stub. Edit this file to add more details.*

The performance impact of time series data augmentation is heavily dependent on the model's channel-modeling paradigm.


## Why It Matters

Highlights a fundamental interaction between model inductive bias (channel-mixing vs. channel-independent) and data distribution, a critical consideration for future time series model design.

## Evidence

> The effect is sharply architecture-conditional: channel-mixing models (TimesNet, iTransformer) benefit in the majority of trials, while channel-independent models (DLinear, PatchTST) are consistently degraded.
