### Evaluating the "Process" Behind the Data

*   **Evaluation Instability:** The study on *Temporal Taskification in Continual Learning* (2604.21930) is a crucial reality check. It proves that benchmark results can be artifacts of how we "slice" the time dimension. Their proposed *Boundary-Profile Sensitivity (BPS)* metric forces researchers to acknowledge that the temporal structure of the evaluation protocol is a latent variable that dictates performance.
*   **Informative Missingness:** The clinical time-series work (2604.21235) treats the act of recording data as a feature itself. By encoding "informative missingness," the model avoids the pitfall of assuming data gaps are random, successfully linking the clinical decision process to the patient's underlying health state.

*   **Diffusion as Data Assimilation:** *DiffSRDA* (2604.21180) cleverly repurposes denoising diffusion models to replace traditional high-cost ensemble methods in fluid dynamics.
