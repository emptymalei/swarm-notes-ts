### The Shift Toward Differentiable Physics
A standout theme is the move toward **differentiable scientific software stacks**. Papers like *sapphire* (2604.06318) for galaxy formation and the GNN-ODE approach for reactor thermal-hydraulics (2604.07292) exemplify this. By implementing physical models in frameworks like JAX, researchers are enabling automatic differentiation of the entire simulation pipeline. This allows for gradient-based parameter inference that was previously computationally prohibitive, effectively closing the loop between observational data and theoretical model refinement. 

Similarly, the physics-informed reconstruction of graphene (2604.07271) demonstrates how regularization—using molecular dynamics as a prior—can overcome signal-to-noise limitations in low-dose microscopy. The common thread here is the transition from "fitting trajectories" to "recovering physical correlations" (e.g., Reynolds numbers or electronic modulations), marking a significant shift in how we leverage data to probe hidden physical regimes.

### Advanced Dynamics & Conformal Uncertainty
On the methodological front, there is a strong focus on **asymptotic guarantees and robustness in time-series forecasting**.
*   **Conformal Prediction:** The introduction of Sequential Conformalized Density Regions (SCDR) (2604.07325) is particularly insightful. By allowing for disconnected prediction sets, it addresses the "bifurcation" problem in dynamical systems, providing a more nuanced approach than traditional interval-based methods.
*   **Amortized Filtering:** AFSF (2604.07169) builds on this by coupling forward and backward flows to enable smoothing and extrapolation. This reflects a broader effort to make Bayesian filtering more efficient by moving the computational burden from online inference to an amortized, trained encoder.
*   **Decision-Focused Learning:** Both the CVR control (2604.07106) and the "learning debt" framework (2604.06438) shift the objective from minimizing simple forecast error (MSE) to minimizing **decision regret**. This is a vital evolution for real-world deployment, where the cost of a "bad forecast" is rarely symmetric.
