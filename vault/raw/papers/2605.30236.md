Abstract

Conclusions
The search for primordial oscillatory features in the cosmic microwave background provides a unique, yet challenging, window into the dynamics of the early Universe. In this work, we have systematically compared high-frequency oscillatory templates (LIN, LOG, BUMP, and PSC) with the most stringent unbinned
Planck

Exploiting this physical distinction, our forecasts for a combined Simons Observatory and LiteBIRD configuration reveal the transformative potential of next-generation polarisation data.
