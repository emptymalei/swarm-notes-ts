# Markov reads Pushkin, again: A statistical journey into the poetic world of Evgenij Onegin

**Authors**: Angelo Maria Sabatini
**Date**: 2026-04-22
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.20221](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20221)

## Summary

This study revisits the use of Markov modeling for literary analysis by encoding the phonological structure of 'Evgenij Onegin' and its Italian translation into binary vowel/consonant sequences. The author demonstrates that a simple four-state Markov chain effectively captures the sequence's memory depth and autocorrelation, highlighting fundamental structural asymmetries between the two languages. Furthermore, the paper introduces 'phonological probes' to connect surface graphemic patterns to narrative development, providing a framework for comparative poetics through computational statistics.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a minimalist four-state Markov chain approach for capturing the phonological structure of literary texts via vowel/consonant encoding.
- Demonstrates that low-complexity Markov models can successfully reproduce autocorrelation and memory depth characteristics in poetic sequences.
- Develops phonological probes to quantify structural divergence between original Russian poetic sequences and translated texts, revealing distinct memory depth profiles.
