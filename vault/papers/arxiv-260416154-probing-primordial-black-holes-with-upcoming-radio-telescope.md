# Probing Primordial Black Holes with upcoming Radio Telescopes: a case study for LOFAR2.0, FAST Core Array and BINGO

**Authors**: Joao R. L. Santos, Guillem Domènech, Amilcar R. Queiroz, 
**Date**: 2026-04-17
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.16154](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16154)

## Summary

This paper evaluates the potential of the upcoming LOFAR2.0, FAST Core Array, and BINGO radio telescopes to constrain the Primordial Black Hole (PBH) dark matter fraction via the detection of lensed Fast Radio Bursts. By analyzing prospective survey performance, the study establishes competitive upper bounds on the PBH mass fraction across various mass regimes. The findings highlight the utility of FRB lensing as an independent cosmological probe, providing complementary evidence to existing dark matter detection strategies.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates the capability of using Fast Radio Burst (FRB) lensing to provide independent and complementary constraints on the Primordial Black Hole (PBH) dark matter fraction.
- Forecasts PBH mass-fraction constraints for three upcoming radio telescope arrays: LOFAR2.0 (f_PBH < 0.16 for M_PBH > 1 M_sun), FAST Core Array (f_PBH < 0.39 for M_PBH > 10 M_sun), and BINGO (f_PBH < 0.39 for M_PBH > 10^-2 M_sun).
- Provides a systematic evaluation of upcoming radio instrumentation for probing dark matter models through transient astronomical event lensing.

### Approved Open Questions
