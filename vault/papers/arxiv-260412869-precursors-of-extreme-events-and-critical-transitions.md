# Precursors of extreme events and critical transitions

**Authors**: Riccardo Consonni, Luca Magri
**Date**: 2026-04-14
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.12869](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12869)

## Summary

This paper presents a theoretical framework for predicting extreme events and critical transitions in fast-slow nonlinear dynamical systems. By analyzing the behavior of fast and slow Covariant Lyapunov Vectors (CLVs) across three distinct regimes—slow, transition, and critical—the authors derive two novel precursors based on spectral gap and transversality metrics. The approach is validated on low- and high-dimensional systems, demonstrating perfect predictive accuracy (100% precision and recall) for extreme event detection.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes a theoretical framework identifying a three-stage cascade of Fast-Slow CLV alignments and stability shifts preceding extreme events.
- Proposes two theoretically grounded precursors derived from Covariant Lyapunov Vector (CLV) dynamics that achieve 100% precision and recall in tested nonlinear systems.
- Demonstrates that the loss of transversality between fast and slow subspaces serves as a universal indicator for critical transitions.
