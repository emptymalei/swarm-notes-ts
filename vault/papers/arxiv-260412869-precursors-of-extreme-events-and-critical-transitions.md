# Precursors of extreme events and critical transitions

**Authors**: Riccardo Consonni, Luca Magri
**Date**: 2026-04-14
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.12869](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12869)

## Summary

This paper presents a dynamical systems theory for predicting extreme events and critical transitions in fast-slow nonlinear systems. By analyzing the behavior of Covariant Lyapunov Vectors (CLVs) across a three-stage cascade, the authors characterize the geometric and spectral conditions that signal an impending extreme event. The proposed precursors, derived from these conditions, achieve perfect predictive performance in numerical simulations across different dimensionalities. This framework provides a rigorous, physically-informed approach to forecasting catastrophic transitions that overcomes the limitations of purely statistical methods.

## Key Contributions

- Identifies a three-stage cascade of dynamical systems states (slow, transition, critical) leading to extreme events.
- Proposes two theoretically grounded precursors based on fast-slow Covariant Lyapunov Vector (CLV) alignment and subspace transversality.
- Demonstrates 100% precision and recall for predicting extreme events across varied low- and high-dimensional nonlinear systems.
