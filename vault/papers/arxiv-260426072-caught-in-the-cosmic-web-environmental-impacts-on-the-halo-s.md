# Caught in the Cosmic Web: Environmental Impacts on the Halo Substructure Boosts to Dark Matter Annihilation Signals

**Authors**: Feven Markos Hunde, Wojciech A. Hellwing, Maciej Bilicki
**Date**: 2026-04-28
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.26072](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26072)

## Summary

This paper introduces an environment-aware prescription to improve the accuracy of dark matter annihilation signal predictions, which traditionally rely on universal boost factors dependent solely on host-halo mass. By incorporating the cosmic web's influence on subhalo populations—including environment-dependent host concentrations and subhalo mass functions—the authors reveal significant variations in boost factors for halos residing in filaments, walls, and voids. Their findings show that ignoring the environmental context leads to substantial systematic biases, particularly for void-dwelling halos. The proposed framework provides a robust, modular approach for refining dark matter indirect detection forecasts and cosmological halo modeling.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies the environmental modulation of the dark matter subhalo boost factor across different cosmic web structures, showing void-dwelling halos exhibit ~30-33% suppression compared to the cosmic mean.
- Develops a deterministic model propagating environment-dependent ratios (halo concentration, subhalo mass function, and internal structure proxies) through semi-analytic boost frameworks.
- Demonstrates that filament-resident halos exhibit a mass-dependent transition in boost behavior, shifting from suppression to enhancement for massive host halos.
