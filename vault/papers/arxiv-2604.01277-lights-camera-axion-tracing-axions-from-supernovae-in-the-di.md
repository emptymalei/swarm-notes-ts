# Lights, Camera, Axion: Tracing Axions from Supernovae in the Diffuse $\gamma$-ray Sky

**Authors**: Brijesh Kanodia, Debajit Bose, Subhadip Bouri, Ranjan Laha
**Date**: 2026-04-01
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.01277](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01277)

## Summary

This paper presents a novel framework for calculating the diffuse gamma-ray flux arising from the conversion of axions produced in core-collapse supernovae. By consistently modeling axion-photon conversion across diverse astrophysical magnetic environments and integrating updated star formation rates, the authors set new constraints on axion-photon coupling. The study provides critical insights into the observability of these signals, offering projections for upcoming MeV-scale gamma-ray observatories.

## Key Contributions

- Developed a comprehensive framework for modeling axion-photon conversion across multiple astrophysical magnetic field environments including progenitor, host galaxy, IGM, and the Milky Way.
- Derived competitive constraints on axion-photon coupling by comparing diffuse gamma-ray flux models against observational data from COMPTEL, EGRET, and Fermi-LAT.
- Conducted Fisher forecast analysis to quantify the discovery potential of next-generation MeV gamma-ray telescopes for detecting axion-induced diffuse signals.
