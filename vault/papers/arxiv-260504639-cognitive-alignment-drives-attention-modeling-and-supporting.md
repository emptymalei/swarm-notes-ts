# Cognitive Alignment Drives Attention: Modeling and Supporting Socially Shared Regulation in Pair Programming

**Authors**: Anahita Golrang, Kshitij Sharma
**Date**: 2026-05-06
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.04639](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04639)

## Summary

This paper investigates the dynamics of socially shared regulation of learning (SSRL) in pair programming using dual eye-tracking and pupillometry to measure joint mental effort (JME) and joint visual attention (JVA). Through three studies involving 182 dyads, the authors establish a causal link where cognitive alignment, manifested as JME, drives attentional coordination. The findings demonstrate that AI-driven, proactive feedback targeting both JME and JVA effectively sustains shared regulation and prevents collaborative breakdowns, positioning AI as an intelligent co-regulator in human-AI-human collaborative systems.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that high-performing dyads in collaborative debugging exhibit stronger joint mental effort (JME) and joint visual attention (JVA) with JME acting as a causal predictor for JVA.
- Evaluates AI-driven adaptive feedback mechanisms, showing that combined feedback targeting both JME and JVA outperforms single-channel interventions in regulatory coherence and task performance.
- Introduces a proactive, forecast-based feedback strategy that anticipates collaborative breakdowns, demonstrating superior performance outcomes compared to reactive support models.

## Limitations

The study focuses on collaborative debugging tasks; the generalizability of these regulatory patterns to other collaborative domains remains to be established. The efficacy of proactive support is constrained by the accuracy of the underlying collaborative state prediction models.
