# State-resolved multimodal contributions to stratospheric polar vortex predictability

**Authors**: Shuo Yang, Dan Zhao, Tingting Xue, Chunhua Zeng, Yongwen Zhang, Xiaosong Chen
**Date**: 2026-05-13
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.13417](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13417)

## Summary

This paper presents a novel framework for analyzing the dynamical predictability of the stratospheric polar vortex (SPV) by decomposing geopotential height fields into coherent circulation states using eigen microstate theory. By applying a mesoscopic Granger-causality approach, the study attributes predictability to specific stratospheric and tropospheric structures at varying lead times. The results reveal that while short-term predictability is primarily driven by persistence, extended-range predictability depends on higher-order structures, particularly during sudden stratospheric warming events. This work provides a physically interpretable mechanism for enhancing subseasonal-to-seasonal forecast models.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a state-resolved framework for stratospheric polar vortex predictability using eigen microstate theory.
- Quantifies the lead-time dependent contributions of circulation states to SPV predictability via mesoscopic Granger-causality.
- Demonstrates that extended SPV predictability relies on higher-order stratospheric structures and tropospheric variability compared to persistence-dominated short-term forecasts.
