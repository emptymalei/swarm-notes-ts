## Summary

This study investigates long-term interstellar scintillation variations in the radio pulsar PSR J0814+7429 using ten years of LOFAR High Band Antennae data. By analyzing the 2D auto-covariance function of dynamic pulse intensity spectra, the authors extract scintillation bandwidth and timescale parameters. The results demonstrate clear annual oscillations in the scintillation timescale, which are modeled to reveal an anisotropic scattering screen located approximately 0.23 kpc from Earth, likely associated with the Local Bubble.

## Key Contributions

- Conducted a decade-long (2013-2023) observational study of PSR J0814+7429 scintillation using LOFAR HBA.
- Derived scintillation bandwidth and timescale parameters from 2D auto-covariance functions of dynamic spectra.
- Identified significant annual variations in scintillation timescales linked to an anisotropic scattering screen at 0.23 kpc, potentially the Local Bubble boundary.
