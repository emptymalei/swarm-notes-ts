# Secondary Gravitational Wave Signatures from 5D Rotating Primordial Black Holes in the Dark Dimension

**Authors**: Waqas Ahmed, George K. Leontaris
**Date**: 2026-05-13
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.12948](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12948)

## Summary

This paper investigates the viability of 5D rotating primordial black holes (PBHs) as dark matter within the Dark Dimension framework. The authors demonstrate that a micron-scale extra dimension combined with the quantum-gravitational memory burden effect significantly suppresses Hawking evaporation, extending PBH lifetimes. They further show that the primordial curvature perturbations responsible for these PBHs induce a detectable stochastic gravitational wave background in the nHz–Hz range, which provides a testable signature for future detectors like LISA and DECIGO/BBO.

## Key Contributions

- Derived evaporation dynamics and enhanced lifetimes for 5D rotating primordial black holes (PBHs) under the memory burden effect in the Dark Dimension scenario.
- Identified a viable dark matter mass window of 10^10–10^21 g for 5D rotating PBHs suppressed by micron-scale extra dimensions.
- Calculated the stochastic gravitational wave background energy density generated by the curvature perturbations required for PBH formation, demonstrating peak sensitivities within the LISA and DECIGO/BBO frequency bands.
