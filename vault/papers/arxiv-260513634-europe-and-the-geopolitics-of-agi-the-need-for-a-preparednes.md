# Europe and the Geopolitics of AGI: The Need for a Preparedness Plan

**Authors**: Maximilian Negele, Daan Juijn, Afek Shamir, David Janků, Bengüsu Özcan, Lisa Soder, Lucia Velasco, Max Reddel, Michiel Bakker, Lorenzo Pacchiardi, Maksym Andriushchenko
**Date**: 2026-05-13
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.13634](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13634)

## Summary

This paper evaluates the geopolitical implications of Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) and assesses Europe's readiness for its potential emergence between 2030 and 2040. The authors analyze how AGI could reshape the global distribution of power and identify significant structural vulnerabilities within Europe, including gaps in compute infrastructure and policy fragmentation. Finally, the study outlines a strategic preparedness agenda intended to enhance European institutional capacity and foster international stability in the face of rapid AI advancement.

## Key Contributions

- Provides a strategic assessment of AGI emergence, projecting a plausible timeline between 2030 and 2040.
- Identifies four critical structural gaps in Europe's AI readiness: strategic awareness, compute infrastructure, talent retention, and fragmented governance.
- Proposes a coordinated European preparedness agenda focusing on institutional capacity building, supply chain positioning, and international stability frameworks.
