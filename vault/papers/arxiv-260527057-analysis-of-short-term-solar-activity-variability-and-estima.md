# Analysis of Short-term Solar Activity Variability and Estimating the Timings of the Next Enhanced Bursts

**Authors**: Juie Shetye, Mausumi Dikpati
**Date**: 2026-05-26
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.27057](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.27057)

## Summary

This paper proposes a hybrid forecasting strategy to predict enhanced bursts of solar activity on intermediate timescales of 6 to 18 months. By modeling each solar hemisphere independently, the authors integrate SARIMA baselines with asymmetric Gaussian overrides and random forest regression to capture the non-linear rise and decay of burst events. The approach is validated on historical sunspot data from 1878 to 2025, providing actionable forecasts for upcoming solar activity bursts in late 2025.

## Key Contributions

- Develops a hybrid forecasting framework combining SARIMA, asymmetric Gaussian overrides, and random forest regression for predicting solar activity bursts.
- Identifies and characterizes space weather seasons on intermediate (6-18 month) timescales using historical monthly smoothed sunspot data.
- Demonstrates improved predictive performance by modeling solar hemispheres independently to account for asymmetric activity patterns and varying burst timing.
