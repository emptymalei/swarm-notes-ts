# The Design of Optimally Balanced Pay-as-you-go Social Security Systems

**Authors**: Leandro Lyra Braga Dognini
**Date**: 2026-04-13
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.12125](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12125)

## Summary

This paper presents a theoretical framework for designing optimally balanced pay-as-you-go social security systems by integrating social security design with general equilibrium theory. Utilizing a backward calculation algorithm for non-stationary overlapping generations economies, the model accounts for household heterogeneity and demographic shifts. The study shows that these systems inherently mirror notional accounts through the application of Walras' law, providing a robust tool for pension reform during demographic transitions as demonstrated through multi-national case studies.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a design methodology for pay-as-you-go social security systems derived from general equilibrium theory in non-stationary overlapping generations (OLG) economies.
- Demonstrates that Walras' law in equilibrium prices leads these social security systems to naturally adopt a structure analogous to notional accounts.
- Illustrates the model's application for demographic transition policy using longitudinal data from Brazil, China, India, Italy, and the United States (1950–2070).

### Approved Open Questions
- Endogenizing demographic and productivity dynamics: Treating demographic and economic variables as exogenous limits the applicability of the model to real-world policy reform, as the introduction of significant pension changes may alter household behavior, fertility choices, and human capital investment.
- Managing forecast uncertainty in pensions: Predictability and intergenerational fairness are critical for social security; without a mechanism to manage forecast errors, such systems are prone to sudden, welfare-reducing policy reversals.
