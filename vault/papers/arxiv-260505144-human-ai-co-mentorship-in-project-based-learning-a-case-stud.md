# Human-AI Co-Mentorship in Project-Based Learning: A Case Study in Financial Forecasting

**Authors**: Freyaa Chawla, Ahan Chawla, Rishi Singh, Joe Germino, Grigorii Khvatskii
**Date**: 2026-05-06
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.05144](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05144)

## Summary

This paper explores a pedagogical framework for project-based learning where high-school and early-undergraduate students use AI tools to accelerate research in financial forecasting. Instead of traditional sequential teaching, the approach emphasizes workflow design and iterative interaction with AI to bridge knowledge gaps, allowing students to focus on problem formulation. The study indicates that this method successfully enables novice participants to build complex forecasting models while deepening their interest in computer science and finance.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a pedagogy for AI-assisted project-based learning that prioritizes workflow design over traditional sequential skill-acquisition.
- Demonstrates that AI-driven tools enable high-school and undergraduate students to perform technical financial forecasting tasks despite minimal prior domain knowledge.
- Proposes a co-mentorship model utilizing AI as an iterative code generation partner alongside graduate-student led conceptual stand-ups.
