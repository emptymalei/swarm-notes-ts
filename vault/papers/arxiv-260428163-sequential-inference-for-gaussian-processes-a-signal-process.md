# Sequential Inference for Gaussian Processes: A Signal Processing Perspective

**Authors**: Daniel Waxman, Fernando Llorente, Petar M. Djurić
**Date**: 2026-04-30
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.28163](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28163)

## Summary

This paper presents a tutorial-style overview of Gaussian Processes (GPs) focused on sequential, incremental, and streaming inference. It bridges traditional signal processing perspectives with modern machine learning, addressing the limitations of IID assumptions in conventional ML. By synthesizing recent methodological advances, the authors provide a practical roadmap for deploying GP-based sequential models in real-world systems such as forecasting and active sensing.

## Key Contributions

- Provides a comprehensive tutorial-style survey of Gaussian Process (GP) methodologies specifically for sequential, incremental, and streaming inference tasks.
- Synthesizes advances in sequential GP inference by bridging traditional signal processing paradigms with modern machine learning frameworks.
- Categorizes methodologies and identifies direct applications for sequential GP models across state-space modeling, forecasting, anomaly detection, and active sensing.
