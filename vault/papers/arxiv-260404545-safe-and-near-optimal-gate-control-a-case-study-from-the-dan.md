# Safe and Near-Optimal Gate Control: A Case Study from the Danish West Coast

**Authors**: Martin Kristjansen, Kim Guldstrand Larsen, Marius Mikučionis, Christian Schilling
**Date**: 2026-04-06
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.04545](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04545)

## Summary

This paper presents a control solution for managing water gates at Ringkoebing Fjord to optimize water levels while respecting safety and ecological constraints. The authors utilize a digital twin created in Uppaal Stratego to simulate fjord dynamics, which is integrated with sea level and wind forecasts to train an online controller. Experimental results show the proposed approach effectively maintains safety requirements across various tidal and weather scenarios, outperforming manual or baseline control strategies.

## Key Contributions

- Developed a digital twin of the Ringkoebing Fjord gate system using Uppaal Stratego to model complex hydraulic and operational dynamics.
- Formulated an online learning-based gate control strategy that balances conflicting safety, maritime, and ecological constraints.
- Demonstrated that learned controllers consistently meet safety requirements under diverse sea-level scenarios (normal, high, and low waters) where baseline controllers fail.
