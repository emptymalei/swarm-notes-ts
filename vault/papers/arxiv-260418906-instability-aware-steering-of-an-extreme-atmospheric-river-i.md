# Instability-Aware Steering of an Extreme Atmospheric River in an AI Weather Foundation Model

**Authors**: Moyan Liu, Qin Huang, Upmanu Lall
**Date**: 2026-04-20
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.18906](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18906)

## Summary

This paper explores the feasibility of controlling extreme atmospheric rivers by applying small, instability-aware perturbations within the Aurora AI weather foundation model. By identifying sensitive upstream regions using finite-time Lyapunov exponents, the authors apply an idealized cloud-seeding operator to modulate latent heat release. Results from a severe California AR case study demonstrate that these perturbations can induce nonlinear, coherent downstream shifts in moisture transport. The study highlights the potential for leveraging atmospheric chaotic sensitivity for purposeful extreme event risk mitigation.

## Key Contributions

- Develops a framework for redirecting extreme atmospheric rivers using small perturbations guided by finite-time Lyapunov exponents and jet-eddy interactions.
- Demonstrates that an idealized cloud-seeding operator within the Aurora AI weather foundation model can induce nonlinear downstream shifts in moisture transport.
- Validates the potential for using intrinsic atmospheric chaos as a tool for dynamical control and extreme weather risk mitigation in a California AR case study.
