# Optimization Under Uncertainty for Energy Infrastructure Planning: A Synthesis of Methods, Tools, and Open Challenges

**Authors**: Rahman Khorramfar, Aron Brenner, Lara Booth, Ana Rivera, Ruaridh Macdonald, Priya Donti, Saurabh Amin
**Date**: 2026-04-12
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.10795](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.10795)

## Summary

This survey examines the intersection of energy infrastructure planning and optimization under uncertainty, addressing complexities introduced by decarbonization and extreme weather. It categorizes existing methodological approaches, including stochastic and robust optimization, and evaluates how these frameworks account for system interdependencies and modeling fidelity. The paper further explores the role of emerging machine learning integration—such as learned uncertainty sets and probabilistic forecasting—in enhancing the scalability and accuracy of long-term investment decision tools.

## Key Contributions

- Provides a comprehensive taxonomy of energy infrastructure planning models categorized by modeling fidelity, uncertainty characterization, and solution methods.
- Synthesizes traditional optimization frameworks—stochastic programming, robust optimization, and distributionally robust optimization—for long-term investment planning.
- Evaluates the integration of machine learning techniques, such as surrogate modeling and probabilistic forecasting, within established optimization workflows for energy systems.

## Archivist Review

This paper is a synthesis survey rather than a primary technical contribution, so no new specific algorithmic concepts were identified as warranting permanent vault entries. The open questions were approved because they define substantial, structural, and recurring challenges in the design of energy planning models (fidelity scaling and endogenous project risk) that are highly relevant to future research in infrastructure and time-series forecasting.

### Approved Open Questions
- Fidelity-Decision Value Thresholds: Defining these thresholds is critical for moving beyond 'more fidelity is always better' towards computationally efficient models tailored to specific decision needs, thereby bridging the gap between academic model complexity and practical utility for policymakers and system operators.
- Endogenous Project Realization Uncertainty: Omitting construction and interconnection delays leads to overly optimistic expansion plans that fail to reflect the reality of infrastructure deployment, potentially resulting in systemic reliability issues or missed decarbonization targets.
