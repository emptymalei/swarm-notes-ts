# Decomposing Ensemble Spread in Lorenz '96 With Learned Stochastic Parameterizations

**Authors**: Birgit Kühbacher, Daan Crommelin, Niki Kilbertus
**Date**: 2026-05-21
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.22242](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22242)

## Summary

This paper presents a systematic framework for decomposing forecast ensemble spread into its constituent sources of uncertainty within the chaotic two-scale Lorenz '96 testbed. The authors investigate how various deterministic, autoregressive, Bayesian, and flow-based stochastic parameterizations contribute to underdispersive forecasts. Their findings reveal that stochastic parameterizations with temporal structure are essential for achieving consistent spread-error relationships, offering critical insights for improving operational weather and climate ensemble forecasting.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a systematic decomposition framework to disentangle intrinsic variability, initial-condition errors, and model uncertainty in chaotic systems.
- Demonstrates that stochastic parameterizations with temporally persistent structures significantly improve early spread growth and spread-error consistency in Lorenz '96 ensembles.
- Provides quantitative evidence that ensemble perturbations regulate trajectory decorrelation and invariant measure exploration rather than increasing long-term system variance.
