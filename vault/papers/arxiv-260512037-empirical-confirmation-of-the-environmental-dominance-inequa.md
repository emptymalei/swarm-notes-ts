# Empirical Confirmation of the Environmental-Dominance Inequality A direct decomposition of Var(ln \r{ho}eff ) across four levels of aggregation

**Authors**: Kristian Sestak
**Date**: 2026-05-12
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.12037](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12037)

## Summary

This paper provides an empirical validation of the environmental-dominance inequality, which posits that the variance in effective environment (rho_eff) far exceeds individual trait variance (k). The analysis utilizes three major public datasets to calculate the dominance ratio at four levels of aggregation, revealing high stability at global and within-country levels. Findings further show a clear structural shift in global dispersion patterns over the 1990-2022 period, signaling a move from between-country to within-country inequality.

## Key Contributions

- Validated the environmental-dominance inequality Var(ln rho_eff) >> Var(ln k) empirically across four levels of aggregation and multiple socio-economic outcomes.
- Demonstrated that the dominance ratio is robust to selection bias, remaining in the range [14, 80] even after adjusting for 40-50% selection share.
- Observed a significant shift from between-country to within-country dispersion between 1990 and 2022, consistent with international convergence patterns.
