# Closing the Loop: A Software Framework for AI to Support Business Decision Making

**Authors**: Jeffrey Wong, Antoine Creux
**Date**: 2026-04-27
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.24116](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24116)

## Summary

This paper introduces a software framework designed to enable AI agents to autonomously manage the business experimentation cycle—from ideation to learning. By integrating mathematical reductions and structured orchestration, the framework supports sophisticated causal analyses, including heterogeneous effects and mediation analysis, within a single interface. Experimental results show that this approach significantly enhances the reliability and efficiency of AI agents in performing complex business decision-making tasks compared to baseline methods.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a software framework that orchestrates heterogeneous causal analysis (heterogeneous effects, policy algorithms, mediation, forecasts) in a unified interface accessible to AI agents.
- Integrates variance reduction and anytime valid inference techniques to accelerate the AI-driven experimentation cycle.
- Demonstrates that the proposed framework improves code correctness, reduces code volume, and enhances performance compared to baseline vanilla agents in business experimentation tasks.
