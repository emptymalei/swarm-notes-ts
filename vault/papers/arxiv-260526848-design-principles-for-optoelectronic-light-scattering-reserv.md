# Design principles for optoelectronic light-scattering reservoir computing at the edge of chaos

**Authors**: Geon Kim, YongKeun Park
**Date**: 2026-05-26
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.26848](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26848)

## Summary

This paper establishes systematic design principles for reconfigurable optoelectronic light-scattering reservoir computing by mapping the relationship between hardware control variables and computational performance. By identifying optimal configurations across reservoir dynamics, input coupling, and interconnectivity, the authors achieve computational peaks at the edge of chaos, consistent with other physical substrates. The proposed framework demonstrates high effectiveness in both chaotic time-series prediction and speech digit classification, providing a transferable design methodology for photonic neuromorphic hardware.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes quantitative design principles for optoelectronic light-scattering reservoirs across three axes: dynamics, input coupling, and interconnectivity.
- Identifies an optimal computational performance regime coinciding with the edge-of-chaos, marked by near-zero maximal Lyapunov exponents.
- Demonstrates 84.5% classification accuracy on the Speech Commands benchmark and effective Mackey-Glass time-series prediction using the optimized photonic substrate.
