# Allowing for imprecision in the game-theoretic characterisation of the Poisson process

**Authors**: Alexander Erreygers
**Date**: 2026-04-01
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.00598](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00598)

## Summary

This paper introduces the 'imprecise Poisson process', a game-theoretic generalization of the classical Poisson process that replaces the strict requirement of a known, exact rate with rate bounds. By allowing for imprecision in rate specification, the model provides a more realistic framework for scenarios where information about the underlying process is incomplete. The author demonstrates that this process retains key characteristics of the standard Poisson process, offering a theoretically sound approach for game-theoretic forecasting under uncertainty.

## Key Contributions

- Generalizes Vovk's game-theoretic characterization of the Poisson process by relaxing the assumption of an exact rate to a rate interval.
- Demonstrates that the imprecise Poisson process maintains structural properties analogous to the precise Poisson process while incorporating rate imprecision.
- Provides a theoretical framework that enhances the robustness of game-theoretic forecasting models when faced with incomplete information.
