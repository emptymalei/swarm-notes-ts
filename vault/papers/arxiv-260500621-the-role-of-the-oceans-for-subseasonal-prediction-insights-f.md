# The role of the oceans for subseasonal prediction: insights from eddy-permitting and eddy-rich coupled forecast systems

**Authors**: Christopher David Robert, Sarah Keeley, Kristian Mogensen, Charles Pelletier, Hao Zuo
**Date**: 2026-05-01
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.00621](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00621)

## Summary

This study evaluates the impact of ocean-atmosphere coupling on 46-day subseasonal reforecasts using the ECMWF Integrated Forecasting System (IFS). The results indicate that coupling substantially improves MJO forecast skill in the tropics, particularly at longer lead times, while offering limited benefits for extratropical predictability. Furthermore, experiments with an eddy-rich 8 km ocean resolution configuration suggest that accurate initialization of the existing eddy-permitting model is more critical for subseasonal atmospheric skill than increasing mesoscale resolution.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that ocean-atmosphere coupling in the IFS significantly enhances MJO forecast skill, extending predictability by approximately 5 days.
- Identifies that ocean-atmosphere coupling provides limited benefit for extratropical atmospheric circulation indices at subseasonal timescales.
- Shows that increasing ocean horizontal resolution to an eddy-rich configuration (~8 km) has minimal impact on subseasonal atmospheric forecasts when initialized accurately.
