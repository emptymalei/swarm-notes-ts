# Neutrino self-interactions in post-reionization era: Lyman-$α$, 21-cm and cross-spectra

**Authors**: Sourav Pal, Supratik Pal
**Date**: 2026-04-16
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.15287](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15287)

## Summary

This paper investigates the signatures of neutrino self-interactions on the matter power spectrum during the post-reionization era using Lyman-$α$ forest and 21-cm intensity mapping. By parameterizing self-interactions through an effective four-fermion coupling $G_{\rm eff}$, the authors employ Fisher matrix analysis to forecast constraints from upcoming surveys like SKA1-Mid and PUMA. The results indicate that combining these tracers with CMB observations significantly enhances sensitivity to interaction regimes and resolves degeneracies inherent in CMB-only studies.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that the Ly$α$--21-cm cross-correlation effectively breaks the degeneracy between the primordial scalar amplitude ($A_s$) and neutrino self-interaction strength ($G_{\rm eff}$).
- Forecasts that the combination of CMB and PUMA survey data improves constraints on neutrino self-interaction strength by up to two orders of magnitude compared to CMB-only baselines.
- Establishes that the Ly$α$--21-cm cross-correlation serves as a systematics-resilient probe for detecting neutrino self-interaction signatures in the post-reionization era.
