# Neutrino self-interactions in post-reionization era: Lyman-α, 21-cm and cross-spectra

**Authors**: Sourav Pal, Supratik Pal
**Date**: 2026-04-16
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.15287](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15287)

## Summary

This paper explores the cosmological signatures of neutrino self-interactions on the post-reionization Lyα forest and 21-cm power spectra. By modeling these interactions through an effective four-fermion coupling (G_eff), the authors employ Fisher matrix analysis to show that cross-correlating these two probes effectively breaks the degeneracy between the primordial scalar amplitude and neutrino interaction strength. The study forecasts that next-generation configurations (CMB+PUMA/SKA1-Mid) significantly enhance sensitivity, offering up to a two-order-of-magnitude improvement in constraints compared to CMB-only baselines.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes the use of Lyα–21-cm cross-correlation as a systematics-resilient probe to break degeneracies between the scalar power spectrum amplitude (As) and neutrino self-interaction strength (G_eff).
- Demonstrates that combining next-generation CMB observations with PUMA/SKA1-Mid surveys yields 1-2 orders of magnitude improvement in G_eff constraints compared to CMB-only analysis.
- Provides formal Fisher matrix forecasts for neutrino self-interaction parameters across a range of coupling strengths (log10(G_eff) from -6 to -1.77) in the post-reionization epoch.
