# GeomHerd: A Forward-looking Herding Quantification via Ricci Flow Geometry on Agent Interactive Simulations

**Authors**: Lake Yang, Junwei Su, Jingfeng Zeng, Wenhao Lu, Xingzhi Qian, Weitong Zhang, Chuan Wu, Dunhong Jin
**Date**: 2026-05-12
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.11645](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11645)

## Summary

GeomHerd is a forward-looking framework designed to quantify herding behavior in financial markets by analyzing the structural topology of agent-interaction graphs. Unlike traditional methods that rely on lagging price-correlation statistics, GeomHerd utilizes Ollivier–Ricci curvature on graphs generated by LLM-driven multi-agent simulations. By establishing a bridge to macroscopic statistics like CSAD, the framework provides an early-warning signal for systemic market fragility. Empirical results on continuous-spin and Vicsek substrates demonstrate that GeomHerd anticipates cascading behavior significantly earlier than price-based baselines.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes GeomHerd, a geometric framework that quantifies herding behavior through discrete Ollivier–Ricci curvature on agent-interaction graphs.
- Establishes a theoretical mean-field mapping between the graph-theoretic curvature metric and the classical Cross-Sectional Absolute Deviation (CSAD) herding statistic.
- Demonstrates significant lead-time performance, with detectors firing up to 318 steps before the onset of critical contagion trajectories in simulated financial substrates.
- Validates out-of-domain transferability to the Vicsek self-driven-particle model, showing robustness across different agent interaction structures.
