# Regimes of Scale in AI Meteorology

**Authors**: Anya Martin, Cindy Lin
**Date**: 2026-04-07
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.06000](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06000)

## Summary

This paper investigates the integration of AI/ML tools within meteorology, a field already defined by massive physics-based modeling and high-throughput data pipelines. Through qualitative analysis of 12 expert interviews, the authors identify systemic tensions caused by "regimes of scale," which represent incompatible philosophies of data organization and observation between platform-born AI/ML models and traditional meteorology. The findings suggest that AI/ML is not a domain-agnostic tool, but one that carries infrastructure-specific biases that challenge its adoption in domain-specific scientific practices.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the "regimes of scale" conceptual framework to explain why AI/ML models often struggle to integrate with established scientific data infrastructures.
- Identifies that AI/ML methods are inherently tied to specific platform and internet infrastructures, creating misalignment with meteorological physics-based data pipelines.
- Provides qualitative evidence from 12 interviews with expert meteorologists on the systemic tensions in adopting AI-driven weather modeling.
