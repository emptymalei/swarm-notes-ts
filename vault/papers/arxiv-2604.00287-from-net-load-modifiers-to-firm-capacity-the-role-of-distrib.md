# From Net Load Modifiers to Firm Capacity: The Role of Distributed Energy Resources in Resource Adequacy

**Authors**: Yujia Li, Alexandre Moreira, Miguel Heleno
**Date**: 2026-03-31
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.00287](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00287)

## Summary

This paper addresses the institutional challenges of integrating distributed energy resources (DERs) into resource adequacy frameworks by shifting focus from isolated technical barriers to cross-stage systemic friction. The authors introduce a 'four-gate compliance pathway'—comprising entry, metering, accreditation, and enforcement—to track where capacity value is lost at institutional interfaces. Through a comparative study across five U.S. and European jurisdictions, the research demonstrates that these barriers are structural rather than jurisdiction-specific, and provides coordination principles to guide end-to-end compliance redesign.

## Key Contributions

- Identified that compliance architecture, rather than DER technology, is the primary constraint on firm resource adequacy contributions.
- Proposed a unified four-gate compliance pathway framework to identify and trace capacity value loss at institutional interfaces.
- Demonstrated that DER integration barriers are structural and persistent across diverse U.S. and European market designs through a cross-jurisdictional comparative synthesis.
