# Height Variations of Magnetoacoustic Cutoff Frequency in the Solar Atmosphere

**Authors**: Pradeep Kayshap, Gayathri Hegde, Z. E. Musielak, Kris Murawski, Tobıas Felipe
**Date**: 2026-05-13
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.13040](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13040)

## Summary

This study investigates the variation of the magnetoacoustic cutoff frequency in the solar atmosphere using Doppler velocity time series from the IRIS NUV spectral dataset. By applying cross-wavelet analysis to pairs of spectral lines sampling different atmospheric heights, the authors demonstrate that the cutoff frequency increases significantly as waves move from the photosphere to the chromosphere. These observational findings provide a critical benchmark for theoretical models of wave propagation and atmospheric heating in solar physics.

## Key Contributions

- Quantified the increase of the magnetoacoustic cutoff frequency from 3.0 mHz to 8.5 mHz across six atmospheric layers ranging from the photosphere (0.38 Mm) to the chromosphere (1.2 Mm).
- Identified evidence of standing oscillations in higher chromospheric regions using cross-wavelet analysis of IRIS spectral line Doppler velocity time series.
- Established an empirical benchmark for validating and refining theoretical models of wave propagation in the stratified solar atmosphere.
