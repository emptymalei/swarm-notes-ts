# Probing Scalar-Tensor-Induced Gravitational Waves in the nHz Band: NANOGrav and SKA

**Authors**: William Iania, Angelo Ricciardone
**Date**: 2026-04-14
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.13012](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13012)

## Summary

This paper investigates the production of scalar-tensor-induced gravitational waves (STGWs) as a cosmological source of the stochastic gravitational wave background. The authors derive expressions for STGWs under different expansion histories—specifically pure matter-dominated and early matter-dominated eras—working in the Poisson gauge. By comparing these theoretical signals with the NANOGrav 15-year dataset and assessing prospective SKA sensitivity, the study characterizes the viability of STGWs as a contribution to the observed nanohertz gravitational wave signals.

## Key Contributions

- Derives the analytical formulation for STGWs generated during a generic matter-dominated (MD) era and an early matter-dominated (eMD) epoch transitioning to a radiation-dominated (RD) phase.
- Demonstrates that STGW energy density in a purely MD era dilutes rapidly, whereas an eMD phase results in a persistent, non-vanishing SGWB contribution.
- Evaluates the viability of STGWs as an explanation for the NANOGrav 15-year stochastic background signal and forecasts future detection sensitivity using SKA.
