# Probing Scalar-Tensor-Induced Gravitational Waves in the nHz Band: NANOGrav and SKA

**Authors**: William Iania, Angelo Ricciardone
**Date**: 2026-04-14
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.13012](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13012)

## Summary

This paper investigates the cosmological generation of scalar-tensor-induced gravitational waves (STGWs) as a potential source for the stochastic gravitational wave background in the nanohertz band. The authors analyze STGW production during matter-dominated and early matter-dominated eras using the Poisson gauge, identifying distinct behaviors in energy density dilution. Finally, the work assesses the compatibility of these signals with the NANOGrav 15-year dataset and provides future detection projections for the Square Kilometre Array (SKA).

## Key Contributions

- Derived analytical expressions for scalar-tensor-induced gravitational waves (STGWs) generated during matter-dominated and early matter-dominated eras in the Poisson gauge.
- Demonstrated that STGW energy density generated during an early matter-dominated phase persists, unlike in a purely matter-dominated epoch.
- Evaluated the potential contribution of STGW signals to the NANOGrav 15-year dataset and forecasted detectability for the Square Kilometre Array (SKA).
