# JWST Lensed Quasar Dark Matter Survey V: Measuring the minimum halo mass with strong gravitational lensing

**Authors**: A. M. Nierenberg, D. Gilman, T. Treu, X. Du, C. Gannon, H. Paugnat, S. Birrer, A. J. Benson, K. N. Abazajian, T. Anguita, S. G. Djorgovski, S. F. Hoenig, R. E. Keeley, A. Kusenko, H. R. Larsson, L. A. Moustakas, P. Mozumdar, W. Sheu, D. Sluse, D. Stern, D. Williams, K. C. Wong
**Date**: 2026-04-06
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.05237](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05237)

## Summary

This paper utilizes 28 strong gravitational lensing systems observed by JWST to constrain the minimum halo mass in the Cold Dark Matter (CDM) framework. By modeling a sharp cutoff in the halo mass function and accounting for tidal stripping effects, the authors derive upper limits on the mass scale of subhalos. Their results provide competitive constraints compared to traditional methods using Milky Way satellites, with future potential to improve these bounds significantly via upcoming large-scale lens surveys.

## Key Contributions

- Established an upper limit on the low-mass cutoff of the halo mass function at m_low < 10^8.3 M_sun using 28 strong gravitational lensing systems.
- Demonstrated that gravitational lensing constraints on the halo mass function are comparable to or exceed those derived from Milky Way satellite galaxy observations.
- Provided a forecasting analysis indicating that a sample of 200 quadruply imaged quasar lenses can improve current constraints on the low-mass halo cutoff by over an order of magnitude.
