# Assessing the Potential of Masked Autoencoder Foundation Models in Predicting Downhole Metrics from Surface Drilling Data

**Authors**: Aleksander Berezowski, Hassan Hassanzadeh, Gouri Ginde
**Date**: 2026-04-16
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.15169](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15169)

## Summary

This systematic mapping study evaluates the potential of applying Masked Autoencoder Foundation Models (MAEFMs) to the domain of oil and gas drilling analytics. Despite the prevalence of surface sensor data, current predictive models for downhole metrics rely heavily on supervised architectures like ANNs and LSTMs, which often struggle with the scarcity of labeled downhole targets. The authors argue that MAEFMs represent an untapped opportunity, leveraging self-supervised pre-training on unlabeled surface data to improve generalization and multi-task prediction performance. The study provides a roadmap for future empirical research to bridge the gap between foundation model capabilities and practical drilling operations.

## Key Contributions

- Systematic mapping study of thirteen papers (2015-2025) evaluating the feasibility of foundation models for drilling analytics.
- Identified a research gap by highlighting that current drilling prediction relies on ANNs and LSTMs, while Masked Autoencoder Foundation Models (MAEFMs) remain unexplored in this domain.
- Proposed MAEFMs as a superior alternative for drilling metrics prediction due to their capacity for self-supervised pre-training on abundant unlabeled surface sensor data.
