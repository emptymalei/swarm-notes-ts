# Random Polarization Position Angle Behaviors across Bursts of Repeating Fast Radio Bursts

**Authors**: Xiaohui Liu, Jiarui Niu, Tiancong Wang, Jun-Shuo Zhang, Yuanhong Qu, Jinchen Jiang, Yongkun Zhang, Heng Xu, Dejiang Zhou, Wei-Yang Wang, Weiwei Zhu, Weiwei Zhu, Bing Zhang, Xuelei Chen, Xiang-Han Cui, Jinlin Han, Kejia Lee, Di Li, Jiawei Luo, Rui Luo, Chengwei Liang, Chenhui Niu, Wan-Peng Sun, Bojun Wang, Fayin Wang, Pei Wang, Qin Wu, Ziwei Wu, Jiangwei Xu, Yuan-Pei Yang, Shiqian Zhao
**Date**: 2026-05-01
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.00372](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00372)

## Summary

This study investigates the polarization position angle (PA) behaviors of repeating fast radio bursts (FRBs), finding that their intrinsic PAs are approximately Gaussian distributed. Through comprehensive periodicity searches using the Lomb-Scargle periodogram, the authors identify no credible periodic signals in the PA time series. To reconcile these observations, the paper extends the traditional rotating vector model to account for stochastic perturbations in the magnetosphere, successfully explaining the observed PA distributions and lack of periodicity through geometric projection.

## Key Contributions

- Identified that the intrinsic polarization position angles (PA) of repeating fast radio bursts follow an approximately Gaussian distribution.
- Demonstrated the lack of periodic signals in PA time series across the 10 ms to 10^7 ms range using Lomb-Scargle periodograms.
- Proposed an extension to the rotating vector model by incorporating a dynamically evolving magnetosphere to explain stochastic PA wandering as a geometric projection effect.
