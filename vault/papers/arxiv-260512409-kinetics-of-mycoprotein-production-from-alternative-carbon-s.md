# Kinetics of Mycoprotein Production from Alternative Carbon Substrates

**Authors**: Mason Banks, Nipon Sarmah, Yiying He, Thomas Vinestock, Mark Taylor, Miao Guo
**Date**: 2026-05-12
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.12409](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12409)

## Summary

This study investigates the biokinetics of *F. venenatum* A3/5 fermentation using various carbon substrates, including single/mixed sugars and expired food-industry beverage waste. By employing modified single and multiphase Gompertz models, the researchers quantified growth rates and metabolic shifts across different conditions. Results reveal that substrate type significantly dictates carbon allocation, with complex interactions like metabolic inheritance and environmental shifts limiting secondary sugar utilization in dual-substrate environments. The findings demonstrate that complex waste streams like expired functional drinks can outperform synthetic controls in biomass production.

## Key Contributions

- Systematic biokinetic characterization of F. venenatum A3/5 across diverse carbon substrates including food industry waste.
- Demonstration that metabolic inheritance and environmental shifts induce kinetic limitations, specifically stunted diauxic growth in dual-substrate systems.
- Proof-of-concept that expired functional beverage waste serves as an effective feedstock, achieving higher growth rates and biomass titres than synthetic controls.
