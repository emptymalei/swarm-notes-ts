# Chromosphere of the quiet Sun -- II. Atmospheric response to small-scale magnetic flux emergence

**Authors**: Quentin Noraz, Mats Carlsson, Guillaume Aulanier
**Date**: 2026-05-04
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.03019](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03019)

## Summary

This study uses 3D radiative-MHD simulations (Bifrost) to analyze the impact of small-scale magnetic flux emergence on the quiet Sun's atmospheric coupling. The authors find that while chromospheric heating increases monotonically with magnetic field strength, the coronal-base temperature exhibits a non-monotonic response due to feedback from density-driven radiative losses. These findings characterize the chromosphere's role as a thermodynamic gatekeeper, highlighting its regulatory influence on the energy balance of higher atmospheric layers. The results provide critical insights for improving global solar-wind and space-weather forecasting models.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrated that chromospheric mechanical heating rises monotonically with magnetic flux emergence, with reconnecting current sheets maintaining ~50% of the heating contribution.
- Identified a non-monotonic coronal-base temperature response to increasing sub-surface magnetic flux, where strong flux increases coronal density, thereby amplifying radiative losses and cooling the coronal base.
- Established the chromosphere as a critical regulator of coronal thermodynamics through density-driven mass loading and subsequent radiative cooling.
