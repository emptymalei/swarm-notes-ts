## Summary

This paper reports the discovery and analysis of SN 2022riv, a strongly lensed Type Ia supernova at redshift z=1.522 identified in the field of galaxy cluster RX J2129.7+0005. Using JWST NIRSpec spectroscopy and HST imaging, the authors confirmed the supernova type and applied the SALT3-NIR light-curve fitter to derive a precise, cosmology-independent magnification value. The study further investigates the role of microlensing due to the extremely high local stellar mass density near the brightest cluster galaxy, providing critical insights into how local gravitational environments modulate magnification estimates in strongly lensed events.

## Key Contributions

- Discovery and spectroscopic confirmation of SN 2022riv, a strongly lensed Type Ia supernova at redshift z=1.522 detected by HST and JWST.
- Obtained a cosmology-independent magnification measurement of 5.35 ± 1.01 using the SALT3-NIR light-curve fitter.
- Demonstrated the impact of microlensing from high stellar mass density regions on magnification measurements, reconciling model predictions with observational data.
