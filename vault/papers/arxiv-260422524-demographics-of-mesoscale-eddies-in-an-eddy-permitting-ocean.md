# Demographics of Mesoscale Eddies in an Eddy-Permitting Ocean Model and Reanalysis

**Authors**: Benjamin Lombardi, Ian Grooms, William Kleiber
**Date**: 2026-04-24
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.22524](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22524)

## Summary

This paper assesses the realism of mesoscale eddy representations in eddy-permitting ocean models and reanalysis products by comparing them against satellite altimetry data. The analysis reveals that current models significantly underrepresent the frequency of eddies while exhibiting systematic biases in their physical characteristics, including longer lifespans, larger spatial extents, and reduced intensity. These findings highlight critical limitations in the fidelity of current climate modeling frameworks, which could impact downstream applications in biogeochemistry and marine heat wave forecasting.

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies the demographic discrepancies between eddy-permitting ocean models/reanalysis and satellite altimetry observations.
- Demonstrates that eddy-permitting models exhibit systematic biases, specifically underestimating eddy counts by approximately 30% while generating eddies that are larger, longer-lived, and weaker than observational data.
- Provides a comprehensive spatial and global statistical framework for evaluating the fidelity of ocean model eddy representations.
