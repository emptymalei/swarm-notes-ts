# Bandwidth selection with a frequency-domain version of the AIC

**Authors**: Erhard Reschenhofer
**Date**: 2026-06-01
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2606.02295](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02295)

## Summary

This paper addresses the lack of standard objective methods for automatic bandwidth selection in nonparametric spectral density estimation, which often relies on subjective visual tuning. The author proposes a frequency-domain version of the AIC that enables automatic bandwidth determination for nonparametric estimators. Empirical evaluation across synthetic and real-world time series demonstrates that this approach yields spectral density estimates competitive with established parametric autoregressive (AR) models.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a frequency-domain version of the Akaike Information Criterion (AIC) for automatic bandwidth selection in nonparametric spectral density estimation.
- Demonstrates that the frequency-domain AIC achieves performance comparable to standard parametric AR-based spectral density estimation.
- Validates the method using both synthetic time series of varying spectral complexity and real-world time series datasets.
