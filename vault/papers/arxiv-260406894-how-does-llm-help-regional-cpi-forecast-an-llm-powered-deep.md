# How Does LLM Help Regional CPI Forecast: An LLM-powered Deep Panel Modeling Framework

**Authors**: Tianchen Gao, Ao Sun, Yurou Wang, Jingyuan Liu, Cheng Hsiao
**Date**: 2026-04-08
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.06894](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06894)

## Summary

This paper addresses the limitations of traditional macroeconomic panel modeling in capturing high-frequency regional CPI dynamics by integrating social media narratives. The authors propose a residual-joint-modeling framework that leverages LLMs to generate high-frequency surrogates from a newly collected Sina Weibo dataset. The approach combines these surrogates with a deep panel learning procedure featuring region-wise homogeneity pursuit and conformal prediction intervals for uncertainty quantification. Empirical results demonstrate superior performance in reducing short-term forecasting errors and identifying sudden inflationary trends compared to conventional parametric models.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a residual-joint-modeling framework that integrates LLM-derived social media surrogates with traditional panel data models to enhance regional CPI forecasting.
- Introduces a deep panel learning procedure incorporating region-wise homogeneity pursuit to effectively process combined macroeconomic and textual information.
- Demonstrates significant reduction in short-term CPI forecasting errors and improved detection of abrupt inflationary shifts compared to standard econometric benchmarks.
