# AI-Based KPI Prediction Methods in Future 6G Networks: A Survey

**Authors**: Niloofar Mehrnia, Gourav Prateek Sharma, Samie Mostafavi, Andreas Johnsson, Sinem Coleri, Carlo Fischione, James Gross
**Date**: 2026-06-01
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2606.01972](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01972)

## Summary

This paper presents a comprehensive survey of data-driven Key Performance Indicator (KPI) prediction methods for the evolution towards 6G networks. It introduces a multi-dimensional taxonomy to classify existing approaches, covering aspects such as prediction horizons, data sources, and model families. The authors contrast classical statistical models with modern deep learning and reinforcement learning techniques for managing network performance. Furthermore, the survey addresses critical implementation hurdles and sets an agenda for future research in predictive network automation.

## Key Contributions

- Provides a systematic taxonomy classifying KPI prediction methods across dimensions such as KPI type, protocol stack, and prediction horizon.
- Analyzes state-of-the-art predictive approaches ranging from classical statistical methods to deep and reinforcement learning for 6G KPIs.
- Identifies critical deployment challenges including scalability, data privacy, and sustainability within the context of future 6G networks.
- Outlines future research directions focusing on probabilistic prediction and explainability for network automation.
