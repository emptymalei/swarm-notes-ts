# Neutrino self-interactions in post-reionization era: Lyman-$\alpha$, 21-cm and cross-spectra

**Authors**: Sourav Pal, Supratik Pal
**Date**: 2026-04-16
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.15287](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15287)

## Summary

This paper investigates the signatures of neutrino self-interactions on post-reionization structure formation using 21-cm intensity mapping and the Lyman-$\alpha$ forest. By parameterizing self-interactions through an effective four-fermion coupling constant $G_{\rm eff}$, the authors model modifications to auto- and cross-power spectra at redshifts $z \sim 2-3.5$. They demonstrate that combining these probes with next-generation CMB observations effectively breaks existing parameter degeneracies, yielding significant improvements in constraining the interaction strength compared to CMB-only analyses.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that cross-correlating Ly$α$ forest and 21-cm intensity mapping data breaks the $A_s-G_{\rm eff}$ degeneracy limiting CMB-only constraints on neutrino self-interactions.
- Provides Fisher matrix forecasts for next-generation missions showing that CMB+PUMA combinations improve constraints on neutrino self-interaction coupling $G_{\rm eff}$ by 1-2 orders of magnitude compared to CMB alone.
- Establishes the Ly$α$--21-cm cross-correlation as a robust, systematics-resilient observational probe for detecting neutrino self-interaction signatures in the post-reionization era.
