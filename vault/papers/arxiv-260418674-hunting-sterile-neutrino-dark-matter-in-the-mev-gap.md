# Hunting Sterile Neutrino Dark Matter in the MeV Gap

**Authors**: Shivam Gola, Akash Kumar Saha, Manibrata Sen
**Date**: 2026-04-20
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.18674](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18674)

## Summary

This paper investigates the potential of upcoming MeV gamma-ray telescopes to detect sterile neutrino dark matter with masses between 0.2 and 100 MeV. The authors analyze radiative two-body and three-body decay channels to derive projected constraints on the decay rate. Using a Fisher information-based forecasting approach with realistic background and detector modeling, the study shows significant improvements over existing limits. These findings quantify the discovery potential of next-generation instruments in a critical, previously underexplored energy gap.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes new sensitivity projections for sterile neutrino dark matter in the 0.2–100 MeV mass range using upcoming gamma-ray telescope models.
- Performs a Fisher forecasting analysis incorporating realistic astrophysical backgrounds and instrument-specific detector responses.
- Demonstrates that next-generation MeV observatories can improve existing dark matter decay rate constraints by several orders of magnitude.
