# Anticipating Innovation Using Large Language Models

**Authors**: Enrico Maria Fenoaltea, Filippo Santoro, Giordano De Marzo, Segun Taofeek Aroyehun, Andrea Tacchella
**Date**: 2026-05-06
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.04875](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04875)

## Summary

This paper addresses the challenge of forecasting technological innovation by modeling patent descriptions as a language system. The authors introduce TechToken, a transformer-based model that treats International Patent Classification (IPC) codes as tokens to learn the latent semantic relationships between technologies. Results demonstrate that linguistic convergence in the embedding space provides predictive signals for future technological combinations, often years in advance. The model sets a new standard for patent representation quality and innovation trajectory forecasting.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces TechToken, a transformer-based model that maps International Patent Classification (IPC) codes into a learned embedding space to represent technological knowledge.
- Demonstrates that linguistic convergence of IPC embeddings serves as a long-range predictive signal for emerging technological combinations.
- Achieves state-of-the-art performance across diverse patent-related tasks, validating the efficacy of the proposed technology-as-language representation.
