# The geometry of conflict : 3D Spatio-temporal patterns in fatalities prediction

**Authors**: Thomas Schincariol
**Date**: 2026-04-22
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.21067](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21067)

## Summary

This paper introduces a framework for predicting conflict fatalities by analyzing the 3D spatio-temporal geometry of conflict diffusion. By transforming historical data at the Prio-Grid level into identifiable patterns, the study employs an adapted ShapeFinder model with Earth Mover's Distance to classify and match these patterns. Empirical results show that this pattern-recognition approach significantly improves forecasting accuracy compared to the established Views ensemble benchmark, offering new potential for early warning systems.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a framework that transforms conflict fatality data into 3D spatio-temporal patterns for improved predictive accuracy.
- Adapts the ShapeFinder model using Earth Mover's Distance (EMD) to detect, classify, and match complex conflict diffusion trends.
- Demonstrates that pattern-based recognition outperforms the Views ensemble model on historical conflict fatality benchmarks.

### Approved Open Questions
