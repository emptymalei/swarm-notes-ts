# Transformer self-attention encoder-decoder with multimodal deep learning for response time series forecasting and digital twin support in wind structural health monitoring

**Authors**: Feiyu Zhou, Marios Impraimakis
**Date**: 2026-04-02
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.01712](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01712)

## Summary

This paper introduces a transformer-based encoder-decoder model for forecasting wind-induced structural responses in long-span bridges, facilitating structural health monitoring and digital twin applications. By leveraging the temporal characteristics of the system, the model achieves superior predictive performance without requiring assumptions of stationarity or prior knowledge of normal vibration behavior. The approach is validated on real-world measurements from the Hardanger Bridge, demonstrating its efficacy in detecting structural changes through vibration deviation analysis under varying excitation conditions.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a transformer-based encoder-decoder architecture for wind-induced structural response forecasting in bridge health monitoring.
- Eliminates the requirement for assumptions regarding wind stationarity or structural normal vibration behavior, enhancing robustness under changing environmental conditions.
- Demonstrates a digital twin framework for anomaly detection by identifying deviations between predicted and measured vibrations as early-warning indicators for structural changes.
