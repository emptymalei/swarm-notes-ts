# Thermodynamic Liquid Manifold Networks: Physics-Bounded Deep Learning for Solar Forecasting in Autonomous Off-Grid Microgrids

**Authors**: Mohammed Ezzaldin Babiker Abdullah
**Date**: 2026-04-13
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.11909](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11909)

## Summary

This paper addresses the failure of standard deep learning models to adhere to physical laws in solar forecasting, such as phantom nocturnal generation and temporal phase lags. The author introduces the Thermodynamic Liquid Manifold Network, which leverages Koopman-linearization and Riemannian manifold projection to synthesize meteorological data with clear-sky boundary conditions. By integrating a Spectral Calibration unit and a Thermodynamic Alpha-Gate, the model enforces strict celestial geometry compliance while maintaining high predictive performance. Evaluation over a five-year period confirms the model's robustness, achieving near-perfect correlation and zero nocturnal error in a compact, edge-ready architecture.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the Thermodynamic Liquid Manifold Network, a physics-bounded architecture that utilizes Koopman-linearized Riemannian manifolds to enforce celestial and atmospheric constraints in solar forecasting.
- Eliminates physically impossible nocturnal power generation and achieves zero-lag synchronization during rapid cloud transients through a multiplicative Thermodynamic Alpha-Gate.
- Achieves high forecasting accuracy (RMSE 18.31 Wh/m2, Pearson 0.988) on a 5-year semi-arid climate dataset while remaining highly compact (63,458 parameters) for edge-deployment.
