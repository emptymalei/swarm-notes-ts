# Kinematic Lensing Ratio: Reviving Weak Lensing Cosmography as a Geometric Dark Energy Probe

**Authors**: Qinxun Li
**Date**: 2026-04-28
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.26015](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26015)

## Summary

This paper introduces the Kinematic Lensing Ratio (KiLeR), a novel geometric probe designed to enhance dark energy constraints from weak gravitational lensing. By integrating galaxy kinematic data with standard shear measurements, the approach effectively cancels out leading-order systematic errors such as intrinsic alignments and baryonic contamination. Forecasting with the Roman Space Telescope reveals that KiLeR significantly boosts dark energy measurement precision, offering a robust tool for investigating cosmological evolution.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces Kinematic Lensing Ratio (KiLeR), which combines shear ratios with intrinsic galaxy shape information from kinematics.
- Demonstrates that KiLeR mitigates major weak lensing systematics including redshift errors, intrinsic alignments, and baryonic feedback effects.
- Achieves a 192% improvement in dark energy constraints for the Roman Space Telescope forecast compared to existing lensing methods.
