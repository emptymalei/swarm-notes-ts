# A Systematic Evaluation of Current Architectures in Wind Power Forecasting

**Authors**: Vinicius Bortolini, Gilson Adamczuk Oliveira, Erick Oliveira Rodrigues, Matheus Henrique Dal Molin Ribeiro
**Date**: 2026-06-01
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2606.02849](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02849)

## Summary

This paper presents a systematic review of hybrid interval forecasting techniques for wind power generation, focusing on the synergy between deep learning models and modal decomposition methods. It identifies a dominant dual-model strategy where lower and upper bounds are predicted independently, often facilitated by decomposition pre-processing (e.g., VMD, EEMD). The review concludes that while these hybrid approaches enhance prediction reliability, the field is currently hindered by non-standardized evaluation metrics and a lack of rigorous, large-scale real-world validation.

## Key Contributions

- Provides a systematic literature review of hybrid interval wind power forecasting models combining deep learning, modal decomposition, and statistical techniques.
- Identifies that Variational Mode Decomposition (VMD) and Ensemble Empirical Mode Decomposition (EEMD) consistently improve interval width without sacrificing coverage.
- Highlights the prevalence and effectiveness of the dual-model strategy for independent lower and upper bound prediction in wind resource uncertainty quantification.
