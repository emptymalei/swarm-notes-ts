# Inferring the role of binary neutron star mergers in r-process nucleosynthesis with multi-messenger observations using Cosmic Explorer and Einstein Telescope

**Authors**: Aman Agarwal, Suvodip Mukherjee, Daniel M. Siegel
**Date**: 2026-05-22
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.23554](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23554)

## Summary

This paper presents a new method to constrain the contribution of binary neutron star (BNS) mergers to cosmic r-process nucleosynthesis by analyzing correlations between gravitational-wave data and r-process abundances at redshifts up to z=1. Using projected data from future observatories like Cosmic Explorer and the Einstein Telescope, the authors demonstrate that they can accurately estimate the fractional contribution of BNS mergers for both multi-messenger bright-siren and dark-siren events. The approach provides a precise tool, with estimated uncertainties of 5-6%, to distinguish the role of BNS mergers from other astrophysical sites like core-collapse supernovae. Additionally, the method allows for the simultaneous estimation of BNS delay-time distribution parameters.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a method to quantify the contribution of binary neutron star (BNS) mergers to r-process nucleosynthesis using 3rd-generation gravitational-wave detector data.
- Leverages the redshift-dependent correlation between cumulative BNS GW event counts and average r-process abundances at z <= 1.
- Demonstrates via Fisher forecasts that the fractional cumulative contribution of BNS mergers can be estimated with 5-6% precision at 1σ.
- Shows that the proposed framework works for both 'bright-siren' and 'dark-siren' GW event scenarios.
