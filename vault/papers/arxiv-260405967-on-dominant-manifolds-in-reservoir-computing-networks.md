# On Dominant Manifolds in Reservoir Computing Networks

**Authors**: Noa Kaplan, Alberto Padoan, Anastasia Bizyaeva
**Date**: 2026-04-07
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.05967](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05967)

## Summary

This paper investigates the emergence of low-dimensional dominant manifolds in Reservoir Computing (RC) networks trained for temporal forecasting. By focusing on linear continuous-time reservoir models, the authors analytically connect the reservoir's dominant modes to the Koopman eigenfunctions of the underlying dynamical system, establishing a theoretical bridge between RC and Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD). The study explains how the geometry of the network dynamics evolves during training, providing insights into how RC effectively approximates complex dynamical systems through dimensionality reduction.

## Key Contributions

- Establishes a theoretical link between the dimensionality of dominant manifolds in Reservoir Computing (RC) and the intrinsic dimensionality of training data for linear continuous-time reservoir models.
- Demonstrates that dominant modes in trained linear reservoirs approximate Koopman eigenfunctions, providing an explicit bridge between RC and Dynamic Mode Decomposition (DMD).
- Characterizes the geometry of training via eigenvalue motion, showing how RC learns to represent autonomous dynamical systems through dominant manifolds.
