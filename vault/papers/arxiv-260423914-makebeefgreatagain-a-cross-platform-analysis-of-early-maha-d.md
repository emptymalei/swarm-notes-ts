# #MakeBeefGreatAgain: A Cross-Platform Analysis of Early #MAHA Discourse

**Authors**: Haoning Xue, Yue Li, Benjamin A. Lyons, Andy J. King
**Date**: 2026-04-26
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.23914](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23914)

## Summary

This study investigates the evolution of the #MAHA movement using a cross-platform analysis of 41,819 social media posts between September 2024 and January 2025. By applying structural topic modeling and interrupted time-series analysis, the authors evaluate how public discourse relates to the movement's official campaign agenda. The results reveal a significant thematic divergence, with over 80% of public posts failing to address stated campaign priorities, suggesting the hashtag functions more as a fluid, symbolic frame than a unified political agenda.

## Key Contributions

- Analyzed the #MAHA discourse using 41,819 posts across platforms between September 2024 and January 2025.
- Demonstrated a disconnect where 81.3% of public #MAHA posts did not engage with the official campaign priorities.
- Identified three distinct discourse environments: grassroots partisan-support, informational sources, and health-focused spaces.
