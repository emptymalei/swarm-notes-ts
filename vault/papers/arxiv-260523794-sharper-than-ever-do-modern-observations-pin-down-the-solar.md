# Sharper Than Ever: Do Modern Observations Pin Down the Solar Radius to Converge on New Standards?

**Authors**: Jean-Pierre Rozelot, Alexander Kosovichev
**Date**: 2026-05-22
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.23794](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23794)

## Summary

This paper addresses the long-standing challenge of accurately defining and measuring the solar diameter by synthesizing diverse observational data. It evaluates the consistency of modern solar radius estimates and concludes that seismic observations from SOHO and SDO provide the most stable reference points. The authors introduce a new glossary and advocate for standardized metrological measures that account for the specific solar layers being measured, such as the leptocline.

## Key Contributions

- Provides a new glossary and physical definitions for solar diameter to standardize measurement inconsistencies across different observational methods.
- Demonstrates that seismic radius measurements from SOHO and SDO currently yield the most reliable solar diameter determinations compared to ground-based observations.
- Emphasizes the critical importance of specifying the solar layer (leptocline) in radius measurements to achieve standardized consensus in solar physics.
