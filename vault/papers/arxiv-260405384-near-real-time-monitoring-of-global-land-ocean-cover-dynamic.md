# Near real-time monitoring of global land-ocean cover dynamics

**Authors**: Lixing Wang, Tao Li, Xinyu Dou, Zhu Liu
**Date**: 2026-04-07
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.05384](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05384)

## Summary

This paper introduces an integrated monitoring framework to track global land-ocean cover dynamics by fusing multi-source remote sensing and reanalysis data. The proposed framework provides a near-real-time 5-day resolution time series covering 2018–2025, enabling the detection of seasonal rhythms and regional land-ocean patterns. Crucially, the study evaluates these dynamics against Earth system safety thresholds, revealing that global forest cover and Arctic sea ice are approaching or exceeding critical climate stability limits.

## Key Contributions

- Developed an integrated monitoring framework that fuses multi-source remote sensing and reanalysis data for near-real-time global land-ocean cover tracking.
- Generated a 5-day resolution time series of global land cover and sea ice coverage from 2018 to 2025.
- Quantified the proximity of global forest and Arctic sea ice indicators to established Earth system safety thresholds, identifying significant negative trends and critical limit breaches.
