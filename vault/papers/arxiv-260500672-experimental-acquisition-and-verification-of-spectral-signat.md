# Experimental Acquisition and Verification of Spectral Signatures of Dynamic Bifurcations

**Authors**: Suvradip Maity, Debajyoti Guha, Soumitro Banerjee
**Date**: 2026-05-01
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.00672](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00672)

## Summary

This paper presents the first experimental framework for generating Spectral Bifurcation Diagrams (SBDs) to identify dynamical transitions in nonlinear systems. By integrating analog electronic circuits with automated data acquisition, the authors successfully map frequency-domain signatures of bifurcations such as period-doubling and quasiperiodicity. The experimental findings align with numerical predictions, confirming SBDs as a robust tool for real-world nonlinear system analysis.

## Key Contributions

- Developed an automated experimental framework for generating Spectral Bifurcation Diagrams (SBDs) using analog electronic circuits and DAQ systems.
- Validated the use of SBDs to experimentally capture complex bifurcation signatures including period-doubling, quasiperiodicity, and torus length-doubling.
- Demonstrated strong qualitative agreement between experimental SBD results and existing numerical bifurcation predictions in nonlinear systems.

The paper introduces a method for experimentally generating Spectral Bifurcation Diagrams (SBDs). I have approved the SBD concept as a central, reusable frequency-domain diagnostic tool for nonlinear dynamics. The open question regarding noise decoupling is also approved as a substantive limitation for experimental deployment. No datasets were approved as none were provided.

### Approved Concepts
- Spectral Bifurcation Diagrams (SBDs): SBDs shift the focus of nonlinear dynamical system identification from time-domain to frequency-domain, providing a robust, reusable signature for characterizing bifurcations.

### Approved Open Questions
- Experimental Noise Decoupling in SBDs: Robust noise separation is critical for transitioning SBD diagnostics from controlled numerical simulations to noisy real-world physical and industrial applications.
