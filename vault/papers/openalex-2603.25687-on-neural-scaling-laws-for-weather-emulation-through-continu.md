# On Neural Scaling Laws for Weather Emulation through Continual Training

**Authors**: Shashank Subramanian, Alexander Kiefer, Arnur Nigmetov, Amir Gholami, Dmitriy Morozov, Michael W. Mahoney
**Date**: 2026-03-26
**Paper ID**: [openalex:2603.25687](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25687)

## Summary

This work investigates the application of neural scaling laws to scientific machine learning, specifically focusing on weather forecasting models built upon a minimal Swin Transformer architecture. The authors employ a continual training strategy involving constant learning rates and periodic cooldowns, finding it yields predictable scaling trends superior to standard learning rate schedules. By systematically varying model size and compute, the study constructs IsoFLOP curves to identify compute-optimal training regimes for weather emulation. Furthermore, the cooldown phases were repurposed to enhance downstream performance, particularly improving accuracy for longer forecast horizons. This analysis offers a diagnostic tool for resource allocation in large-scale weather simulation via neural networks.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrated that models trained with continual training (constant learning rates and periodic cooldowns) follow predictable scaling trends in weather emulation tasks.
- Showed that continual training can outperform standard cosine learning rate schedules for weather forecasting models.
- Identified compute-optimal training regimes by constructing and analyzing IsoFLOP curves across model and dataset sizes.
- Showed that cooldown phases can be leveraged to improve multi-step rollouts over longer forecast horizons via spectral loss adjustments.

## Limitations

Potential performance limits are hinted at when extrapolating trends to larger scales, suggesting inherent limitations to the current scaling regime.
