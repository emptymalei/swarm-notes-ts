# Quo vadis, stochastic thermodynamics?

**Authors**: Jan Korbel, Artemy Kolchinsky, Sarah A. M. Loos, Gonzalo Manzano, Rosalba Garcia-Millan, Olga Movilla Miangolarra, Édgar Roldán
**Date**: 2026-04-29
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.26601](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26601)

## Summary

This perspective provides a comprehensive review of stochastic thermodynamics, a framework that models non-equilibrium processes through fluctuating trajectories rather than ensemble averages. It details foundational theoretical developments, such as fluctuation theorems and thermodynamic uncertainty relations, while examining extensions to systems with memory, active matter, and information-theoretic interpretations. Finally, the authors discuss the challenges of applying these microscopic principles to macroscopic, complex, and non-physical systems, proposing a unified thermodynamic approach for interdisciplinary research.

## Key Contributions

- Reviews the evolution and current scope of stochastic thermodynamics, from trajectory-level fluctuations to universal bounds like thermodynamic uncertainty relations.
- Synthesizes recent advancements in handling memory effects, hidden degrees of freedom, and optimal transport geometric frameworks.
- Maps out critical challenges in scaling stochastic thermodynamic principles from microscopic systems to macroscopic and complex non-physical domains.
