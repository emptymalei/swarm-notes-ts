# Pinching Antenna Systems (PASS): Enabling Reconfigurable and Controllable Wireless Channels -- A Comprehensive Survey

**Authors**: Elmehdi Illi, Marwa Qaraqe
**Date**: 2026-04-06
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.04521](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.04521)

## Summary

This paper provides a comprehensive survey of Pinching Antenna Systems (PASS), a new wireless channel-altering technique for 6G networks. PASS extends base station coverage by connecting radio-frequency chains to flexible waveguides where antennas can be positioned at custom points, effectively overcoming propagation challenges like path loss and non-line-of-sight conditions. The survey categorizes various PASS implementations and analyzes their performance benefits across network coverage, data rate, sensing, and security, while outlining key challenges for future deployment.

## Key Contributions

- Provides the first comprehensive survey of Pinching Antenna Systems (PASS), a novel wireless channel-altering paradigm.
- Systematizes PASS schemes based on their utility for enhancing network coverage, data rate, information-theoretic security, and integrated sensing and communication (ISAC).
- Evaluates the potential of PASS to overcome traditional propagation limitations such as line-of-sight blockage and high free-space path loss.
