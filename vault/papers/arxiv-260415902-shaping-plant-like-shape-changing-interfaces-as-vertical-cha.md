# Shaping Plant-Like Shape-Changing Interfaces as Vertical Charts: Maximizing Readability, Aesthetics, and Naturalness

**Authors**: Elodie Bouzekri, Guillaume Riviere
**Date**: 2026-04-17
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.15902](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15902)

## Summary

This paper explores the design and implementation of plant-like, shape-changing physical interfaces for visualizing environmental data series. Through a research-through-design approach, the authors evaluate four physical prototypes, focusing on how material selection and folding mechanisms impact readability, aesthetics, and user learnability. Results indicate that physical plant-like charts provide an intuitive, high-naturalness alternative to traditional graphical displays for public-facing data visualization.

## Key Contributions

- Proposed a design space for plant-like shape-changing interfaces to represent environmental data series as vertical charts.
- Conducted a user study comparing four prototypes across modality and material dimensions in the context of renewable energy forecasting.
- Demonstrated that physical plant-like charts offer a superior balance of naturalness and readability compared to graphical infographics, particularly when leveraging materials that evoke sustainability.
