# Anomalous, pre-yield grain-boundary sliding in copper revealed with in-situ high-resolution strain mapping

**Authors**: Benjamin Poole, David Lunt, Luke Hewitt, Chris Hardie
**Date**: 2026-04-29
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.26515](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26515)

## Summary

This study investigates grain boundary sliding (GBS) in oxygen-free high-conductivity copper at room temperature using in-situ high-resolution digital image correlation (HRDIC) within a scanning electron microscope. The researchers discover that GBS occurs significantly before macroscopic yield and the onset of crystallographic slip, exhibiting strain and rotation magnitudes that exceed those driven by traditional slip mechanisms. By correlating these strain maps with surface height and crystallographic orientation data, the study establishes a multi-modal characterization of sliding behavior in engineering alloys.

## Key Contributions

- Identified that extensive grain boundary sliding (GBS) occurs in copper at room temperature prior to macroscopic yield.
- Demonstrated through high-resolution strain mapping that pre-yield grain boundary sliding produces strain and rotation values exceeding those from crystallographic slip.
- Characterized GBS modes—pure in-plane, out-of-plane, and mixed-mode—by integrating HRDIC strain maps, confocal height mapping, and EBSD orientation data.
