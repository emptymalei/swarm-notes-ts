# A novel three-step approach to forecast firm-specific technology convergence opportunity via multi-dimensional feature fusion

**Authors**: Fu Gu, Ao Chen, Yingwen Wu
**Date**: 2026-04-01
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.00803](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.00803)

## Summary

This paper presents a three-step framework for forecasting firm-specific technology convergence (TC) by integrating multidimensional patent data including bibliometric, network, and textual features. The approach uses attention-based feature fusion, an ensemble learning model for identifying IPC-level convergence, and an LLM-driven RAG pipeline to evaluate and refine these opportunities for specific firms. Empirical results demonstrate the framework's effectiveness in predicting emerging opportunities for a leading manufacturer in the thermal management and robotics sectors.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a three-step framework for firm-specific technology convergence (TC) forecasting by fusing bibliometric, network structure, and textual features from patent documents.
- Implements a two-stage ensemble learning model with imbalance-handling strategies to identify IPC-level TC opportunities.
- Proposes an LLM-as-a-judge evaluation mechanism using retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) to refine and rank firm-specific technology opportunities.
