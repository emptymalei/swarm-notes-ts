# Data Poisoning Attacks on Informativity for Observability: Invariance-Based Synthesis

**Authors**: Iori Takaki, Ahmet Cetinkaya, Hideaki Ishii
**Date**: 2026-04-13
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.11657](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11657)

## Summary

This paper investigates data poisoning attacks targeting informativity-based analysis in data-driven control systems, specifically focusing on strong observability. The authors propose a method using invertible linear transformations to manipulate data matrices, effectively embedding malicious states into the system's invariant subspace. By formulating the attack as a minimum-norm optimization problem, the study quantifies the minimal distortion necessary to invalidate system informativity certificates. Numerical experiments confirm that even small, structured transformations can successfully deceive observability analysis.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces an invariance-based data poisoning attack framework against strong observability in data-driven control.
- Derives feasibility conditions for the existence of linear transformations that invalidate informativity certificates in finite time-series datasets.
- Formulates a minimum-norm optimization problem to compute the smallest data distortion required to destroy system informativity.
