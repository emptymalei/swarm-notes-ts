# Machine Learning and Deep Learning Models for Short Term Electricity Price Forecasting in Australia's National Electricity Market

**Authors**: Wei Lu, Jay Wang, Dingli Duan, Ding Mao, Caiyi Song, John Huang
**Date**: 2026-04-26
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.23908](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23908)

## Summary

This paper conducts a systematic evaluation of various machine learning and deep learning models for short-term electricity price and demand forecasting in the South Australian National Electricity Market. By establishing a unified benchmarking framework with consistent preprocessing and feature engineering, the authors compare the predictive performance of tree-based ensembles and recurrent neural architectures. The results demonstrate that while GBRT excels in both tasks, electricity price remains exceptionally difficult to predict compared to demand, likely due to high volatility and renewable-driven price spikes. The study underscores the persistent challenges in forecasting irregular power market dynamics despite advanced model performance.

## Key Contributions

- Develops a unified benchmarking framework for short-term electricity price and demand forecasting within Australia's National Electricity Market.
- Provides a systematic performance comparison of six algorithms (AWMLSTM, CatBoost, GBRT, LSTM, LightGBM, and SVR) under identical preprocessing and feature engineering.
- Identifies that tree-based models, specifically GBRT, significantly outperform RNN-based models (LSTM) in both price and demand forecasting tasks.
