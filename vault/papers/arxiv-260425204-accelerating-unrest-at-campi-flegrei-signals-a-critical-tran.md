# Accelerating unrest at Campi Flegrei signals a critical transition within the next decade

**Authors**: Davide Zaccagnino, Didier Sornette, Antonio Giovanni Iaccarino, Matteo Picozzi
**Date**: 2026-04-28
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.25204](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25204)

## Summary

This paper investigates the accelerating volcanic unrest at Campi Flegrei by modeling seismic and geodetic data as a regularised finite-time singularity. The authors identify a critical transition window between 2030 and 2034, driven by increasing deep magmatic volatile pressure. While the research highlights an approaching mechanical threshold, it emphasizes that the ultimate system evolution—whether leading to an eruption or a new steady-state—remains uncertain.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that accelerating seismicity and uplift at Campi Flegrei are better modeled by a regularised finite-time singularity compared to exponential growth.
- Identifies a critical time window of 2030–2034 for a potential mechanical threshold transition based on converging independent geophysical data.
- Proposes that deep magmatic volatile input is the primary physical driver behind the observed pressurisation of the crustal system.

## Limitations

The model does not provide a definitive prediction of an eruption; the specific outcome of the critical mechanical threshold (e.g., eruption versus bradyseismic peak) remains scientifically uncertain.
