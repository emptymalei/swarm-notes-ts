# On the robustness of Mann-Kendall tests used to forecast critical transitions

**Authors**: Tristan Gamot, Nils Thibeau--Sutre, Tom J. M. Van Dooren
**Date**: 2026-04-16
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.15230](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15230)

## Summary

This paper provides a robustness analysis of the Mann-Kendall trend test, specifically examining its application to detecting early warning signals (EWS) of critical transitions. The authors demonstrate that the standard assumption of a Gaussian asymptotic distribution is violated due to the autocorrelation inherent in common EWS indicators. This violation causes inflated type I error rates, leading the authors to recommend against using Mann-Kendall tests for this purpose and to advocate for more reliable alternatives.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that the theoretical Gaussian assumption of the Mann-Kendall test fails significantly in the presence of autocorrelation generated by Early Warning Signal (EWS) indicators.
- Provides empirical evidence that applying standard Mann-Kendall tests to EWS leads to systematically inflated type I error rates, resulting in false positives for critical transitions.
- Critically evaluates and advises against the use of Mann-Kendall tests for trend detection in EWS-based critical transition forecasting.
