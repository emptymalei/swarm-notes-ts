# PPG-Based Affect Recognition with Long-Range Deep Models: A Measurement-Driven Comparison of CNN, Transformer, and Mamba Architectures

**Authors**: Karim Alghoul, Hussein Al Osman, Abdulmotaleb El Saddik
**Date**: 2026-04-28
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.26078](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26078)

## Summary

This paper evaluates the efficacy of modern long-range sequence models, specifically Transformers and Mamba, against traditional CNN and CNN-LSTM architectures for affective state recognition using wrist-based PPG signals. Through a standardized, subject-independent 5-fold cross-validation protocol, the authors show that advanced sequence models do not consistently outperform simpler CNN baselines in small, noisy PPG datasets. The study concludes that CNNs remain the most efficient choice for wearable implementations, while Transformers exhibit specific advantages in F1 performance for arousal and relaxation tasks.

## Key Contributions

- Conducts the first measurement-driven comparison of CNN, CNN-LSTM, Transformer, and Mamba architectures for PPG-based affect recognition.
- Demonstrates that despite theoretical advantages, Transformers and Mamba do not consistently outperform CNN baselines on small, noisy PPG datasets for arousal, valence, and relaxation classification.
- Provides practical model selection guidance for wearable affective computing, showing CNNs offer the best accuracy-to-size trade-off.
