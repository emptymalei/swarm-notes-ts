# A Dynamic Factor Model for Level and Volatility

**Authors**: Haroon Mumtaz, Sofia Velasco
**Date**: 2026-04-04
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.03681](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03681)

## Summary

This paper introduces a dynamic factor model that enables the endogenous interaction between common level and volatility factors within a large-information framework. By modeling these factors jointly, the approach captures state-dependent and asymmetric tail risks in predictive distributions, with heavy-tailed idiosyncratic shocks effectively isolating persistent uncertainty. Empirically, the framework provides improved density forecast accuracy and reveals significant heterogeneity in the drivers of international inflation across advanced and emerging economies.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a dynamic factor model with endogenous joint evolution of common level and volatility factors.
- Enables the modeling of state-dependent and asymmetric tail risks in predictive distributions by allowing volatility to affect both the location and dispersion of outcomes.
- Demonstrates superior density forecast accuracy, particularly in the distribution tails, using international inflation datasets.
