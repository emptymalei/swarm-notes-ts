# AI Sovereignty: A Qualitative Model of Strategic Competition as AI Becomes an Instrument of National Power

**Authors**: Timothy Clancy, Asmeret Naugle
**Date**: 2026-06-05
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2606.07245](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.07245)

## Summary

This paper explores the strategic implications of AI sovereignty, defining it as the degree to which a nation maintains independent control over its AI technological stack. The authors introduce a novel qualitative model that maps micro, meso, and macro contributors to AI-driven national power, identifying key physical and human capital leverage points. Through this model, the authors analyze the competitive dynamics of frontier AI, highlighting how nations may manipulate these variables to secure economic and military advantages. The findings suggest that AI control will fundamentally redefine 21st-century geopolitical power dynamics.

## Key Contributions

- Defines the concept of AI sovereignty as a framework for assessing national control over AI technology.
- Presents a novel qualitative model of AI sovereignty incorporating micro, meso, and macro levels of national power contributors.
- Identifies strategic leverage points (e.g., accelerators, energy, workforce) and activation mechanisms (kinetic and non-kinetic) for national competitive advantage.
