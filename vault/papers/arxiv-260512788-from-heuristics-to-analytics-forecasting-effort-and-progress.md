# From Heuristics to Analytics: Forecasting Effort and Progress in Online Learning

**Authors**: Eric S. Qiu, Danielle R. Thomas, Boyuan Guo, Vincent Aleven, Conrad Borchers
**Date**: 2026-05-12
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.12788](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12788)

## Summary

This paper formalizes engagement forecasting in intelligent tutoring systems (ITS) as a supervised prediction task to estimate weekly student effort and learning progress. By benchmarking various predictive models against traditional heuristic baselines on a year-long dataset of 425 middle-school students, the study demonstrates significant improvements in accuracy and trajectory tracking. The authors further interpret model behavior to reveal divergent drivers for effort versus progress, and validate these findings through human-in-the-loop qualitative analysis with academic tutors.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces engagement forecasting as a formal supervised learning task for predicting weekly practice duration and skill mastery in intelligent tutoring systems.
- Demonstrates that feature-based predictive models achieve 22-33% MAE reduction over heuristic baselines in tracking student practice and progress.
- Identifies distinct feature importance drivers: recent activity for effort forecasting versus learner-state and content difficulty for progress forecasting.
