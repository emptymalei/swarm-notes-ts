# Statistical Analysis of Spatial and Temporal Variability of Maximum Precipitation Events on the Rio Grande do Sul

**Authors**: Cleber Souza Corrêa
**Date**: 2026-04-07
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.05275](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05275)

## Summary

This paper presents a statistical analysis of maximum annual precipitation patterns in Rio Grande do Sul, Brazil, by fitting theoretical variograms to identify spatiotemporal variability. The study links these precipitation extremes to synoptic-scale atmospheric dynamics, such as low- and high-level jet interactions characteristic of middle latitudes. These findings are framed within the context of enhancing climate trend forecasting for critical applications in the aerospace and aeronautical sectors.

## Key Contributions

- Identifies spatiotemporal patterns of maximum annual rainfall in Rio Grande do Sul using theoretical variogram fitting.
- Connects maximum precipitation events to middle-latitude atmospheric phenomena, specifically low- and high-level jet interactions.
- Provides a methodological framework for characterizing extreme weather for aerospace and aeronautical risk assessment.
