# Resilience Quantification and its Support for Operational Resilience

**Authors**: Ion Matei, Maksym Zhenirovskyy
**Date**: 2026-04-13
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.12073](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12073)

## Summary

This paper introduces a formal framework to quantify system resilience by calculating the volume of degradation space within which performance requirements are maintained. To mitigate the computational cost of high-dimensional boundary estimation, the authors employ a machine-learning-based classification approach accelerated by entropy-guided active sampling. The resulting model supports operational resilience by enabling health state diagnostics, remaining useful life forecasting, and actionable reconfiguration strategies for human operators. The approach is validated through a manufacturing production line case study.

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a resilience capacity metric defined as the volume of the degradation space where functional requirements are satisfied.
- Integrates entropy-guided active sampling with machine learning classifiers to efficiently approximate high-dimensional resilience boundaries.
- Demonstrates a resilience-aware operational workflow linking diagnostics, health-state forecasting, and human-in-the-loop reconfiguration in a manufacturing use case.
