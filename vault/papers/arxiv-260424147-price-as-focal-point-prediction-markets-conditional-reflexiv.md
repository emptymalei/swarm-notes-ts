# Price as Focal Point: Prediction Markets, Conditional Reflexivity, and the Politics of Common Knowledge

**Authors**: Maksym Nechepurenko
**Date**: 2026-04-27
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.24147](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24147)

## Summary

This paper re-evaluates prediction markets not merely as forecasting tools, but as coordination mechanisms that organize societal behavior through 'common knowledge.' The author introduces the Signal Credibility Index (SCI), a microstructure-grounded metric that identifies which price movements are likely to influence elite and institutional coordination. Empirical analysis of the 2024 U.S. presidential election demonstrates a systematic decoupling where the most prominent markets often exhibit the least epistemic robustness, challenging standard accuracy-centric evaluation paradigms.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the Signal Credibility Index (SCI) to measure the behavioral traction of market signals in prediction markets.
- Demonstrates that prediction market social authority can decouple from epistemic accuracy, where visibility does not imply forecasting robustness.
- Provides an analytical framework using transaction-level data to evaluate how political events serve as coordination mechanisms through market pricing.
