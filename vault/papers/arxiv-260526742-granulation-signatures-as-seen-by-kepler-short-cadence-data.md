# Granulation signatures as seen by Kepler short-cadence data. II. A hierarchical route to inferring stellar radii from granulation

**Authors**: Jens R. Larsen, Mia S. Lundkvist, Guy R. Davies, Martin B. Nielsen
**Date**: 2026-05-26
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2605.26742](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26742)

## Summary

This paper presents a Bayesian hierarchical framework to infer stellar radii directly from convective granulation signatures observed in space-based photometric time series. By relating granulation amplitude and characteristic frequency to stellar radius, metallicity, and temperature, the model successfully recovers fundamental stellar properties across a heterogeneous sample of over 700 stars. The approach provides a robust alternative for radius estimation in stars lacking clear asteroseismic mode identification, reaching a 10% precision comparable to established benchmark techniques.

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a Bayesian hierarchical model that predicts stellar radii from granulation observables with ~10% precision.
- Demonstrates that granulation signatures provide an unbiased structural diagnostic for stellar populations across Kepler, TESS, and PLATO datasets.
- Validates the framework on 367 independent stars, achieving 1σ agreement with established seismic and interferometric benchmarks in ~73% of cases.
