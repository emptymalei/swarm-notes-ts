# "The Worst Weather In America": Augmenting the Information Design of Extreme Cold Weather Forecasts

**Authors**: Michael Correll, Jay Broccolo, Drew Bush
**Date**: 2026-04-28
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.25818](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25818)

## Summary

This paper investigates how to improve the communication of extreme weather conditions for Mount Washington visitors by replacing text-heavy forecasts with color-coded hazard icons. Using insights from a participatory workshop, the authors developed a visual design intervention and tested its efficacy through a crowd-sourced study. The results indicate that the icon-based approach successfully increases the perceived risk associated with outdoor activities. The work concludes by discussing the ethical implications and design challenges inherent in communicating complex environmental data to audiences with varying levels of literacy and experience.

## Key Contributions

- Introduced a participatory design-informed framework for translating text-heavy extreme weather forecasts into intuitive hazard icons.
- Demonstrated through a crowd-sourced study that visual hazard icons significantly increase user perception of risk for activities in extreme weather environments.
- Identified critical intersections between visualization design and ethics in communicating public safety risks to diverse user demographics.
