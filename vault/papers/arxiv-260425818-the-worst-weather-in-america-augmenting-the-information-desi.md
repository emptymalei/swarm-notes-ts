# "The Worst Weather In America": Augmenting the Information Design of Extreme Cold Weather Forecasts

**Authors**: Michael Correll, Jay Broccolo, Drew Bush
**Date**: 2026-04-28
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.25818](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25818)

## Summary

This paper investigates methods to improve the clarity and risk-communication efficacy of extreme weather forecasts for Mount Washington. The authors implement a design intervention that replaces or supplements dense text reports with color-coded hazard icons developed through a participatory workshop. A crowd-sourced evaluation confirms that these visual summaries significantly improve the user's perception of risk associated with mountain activities. The work serves as a foundational exploration of how information design and visual communication can improve public safety in high-stakes, volatile environments.

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrated that integrating color-coded hazard icons into text-heavy mountain weather forecasts significantly increases user perception of safety risks.
- Established a participatory design framework for translating expert textual forecasts into accessible visual representations for diverse audiences.
- Identified critical open challenges regarding design ethics and the balance between information simplification and visual literacy in safety-critical communication.
