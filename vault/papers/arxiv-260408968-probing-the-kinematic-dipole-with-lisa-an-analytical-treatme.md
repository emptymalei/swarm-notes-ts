# Probing the Kinematic Dipole with LISA: an analytical treatment

**Authors**: Jacopo Fumagalli, Giulia Cusin, Cyril Pitrou, Gianmassimo Tasinato
**Date**: 2026-04-10
**Paper ID**: [arxiv:2604.08968](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08968)

## Summary

This paper provides a theoretical framework for detecting the kinematic dipole in the stochastic gravitational-wave background using LISA. By deriving the detector's analytical response and constructing an optimal estimator, the authors show that the dipole signal is characterized by a frequency-dependent symmetry function. The findings establish specific signal-to-noise requirements for detection and highlight the potential of this signal to break degeneracies with galactic foregrounds or instrumental noise.

## Key Contributions

- Derived a fully analytic expression for the LISA interferometer's response to a kinematic dipole induced by Solar System motion relative to the cosmic rest frame.
- Constructed an optimal estimator for the detection of the kinematic dipole in the stochastic gravitational-wave background.
- Quantified detectability thresholds for the kinematic dipole signal, identifying sensitivity requirements for both fiducial LISA and next-generation instrument configurations.
